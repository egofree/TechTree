# Implantation process

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Dopant gases (PH₃, AsH₃, B₂H₆, BF₃) are ionized in a plasma source, accelerated through a magnetic mass analyzer (selects the desired ion species by charge-to-mass ratio), then accelerated to high energy (1 keV to several MeV) and scanned across the wafer surface. Implant dose: 10¹¹-10¹⁶ atoms/cm² (determines dopant concentration in the semiconductor). Implant energy: 1 keV to 5 MeV (determines the depth of the implanted layer, from 10 nm to several μm).
