# Wet vs dry process

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Wet process pumps slurry (30-40% water) into kiln — simpler raw material handling but wastes 30-40% of fuel energy evaporating water. Dry process uses preheater tower (4-6 stages of cyclone heat exchangers): hot kiln exhaust gases preheat raw meal to 800°C before it enters the kiln, reducing fuel consumption 30-40%. Modern plants are all dry process with preheater + precalciner.
