# Bootstrap relevance

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

A small DRI shaft furnace or rotary kiln is technically achievable at a smaller scale and lower capital cost than a blast furnace. It does not require coke (coal-based rotary kiln) or high-temperature refractory (no molten iron contact). However, DRI production rates per unit are much lower than blast furnaces, and the gangue remains in the product (requiring slag-forming fluxes in the subsequent melting step).
