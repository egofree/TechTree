# Celluloid (1869)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

The first practical thermoplastic. Nitrocellulose (cellulose treated with nitric acid + sulfuric acid, 10-13% nitrogen content — guncotton range) plasticized with camphor (20-30% by weight). Alexander Parkes produced "Parkesine" in 1856; John Wesley Hyatt improved it as "Celluloid" in 1869, originally to create a substitute for ivory billiard balls.
