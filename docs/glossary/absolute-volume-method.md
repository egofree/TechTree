# Absolute volume method

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The standard approach to designing a concrete mix. The sum of the absolute volumes of all components equals 1 m³ of concrete. Absolute volume = weight / (specific gravity × 1000). For a 25 MPa mix: cement 350 kg (SG 3.15, volume 0.111 m³), water 180 kg (SG 1.00, volume 0.180 m³), fine aggregate 700 kg (SG 2.65, volume 0.264 m³), coarse aggregate 1100 kg (SG 2.70, volume 0.407 m³), entrapped air 2% (0.020 m³), total = 0.982 m³ (adjust quantities to reach exactly 1.000 m³).
