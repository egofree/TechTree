# Working loads

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Apply safety factor of 5:1 for general applications (static loads, non-critical), 10:1 for lifting loads and life-safety (hoisting people, critical rigging, overhead loads). Example: 24 mm hemp rope, breaking strength ~40 kN → working load = 4 kN (general) or 2 kN (lifting). Safety factors account for fiber degradation (UV, moisture, abrasion), shock loading, knots, and uncertainty in actual vs. rated strength.
