# Lantern pinion and crown gear

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The standard gear pair for converting slow wheel rotation to faster output speeds. The crown gear (also called the wallower or face gear) is a flat disc with wooden or iron teeth pegged around its circumference. The lantern pinion is a pair of flanged discs connected by iron or hardwood staves (round pins). The two mesh at 90° to transfer motion between horizontal wheel shaft and vertical millstone shaft. A single stage achieves 3-5:1 speed ratio.
