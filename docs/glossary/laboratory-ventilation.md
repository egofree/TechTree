# Laboratory ventilation

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: gas-handling

For non-toxic gases used at bench scale (N₂, Ar, compressed air), a general laboratory ventilation rate of 6-12 air changes per hour provides adequate dilution. For toxic gases (CO, H₂S, Cl₂, NH₃), a chemical fume hood with 80-120 fpm face velocity is the minimum requirement. Fume hoods are tested annually with a smoke pencil or vaneometer to verify face velocity across the entire sash opening.
