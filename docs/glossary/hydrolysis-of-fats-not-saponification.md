# Hydrolysis of fats (not saponification)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Fat + water (at 250-260°C, 40-50 bar, in an autoclave) → fatty acids + glycerol. No alkali consumed. The "Twitchell process" uses sulfuric acid + aromatic sulfonic acid catalyst. The "Colgate-Emery process" uses continuous high-pressure hydrolysis without catalyst. Fatty acids separated by distillation. Glycerol recovered from aqueous phase by evaporation and distillation. This route produces fatty acids for further chemical processing rather than soap directly.
