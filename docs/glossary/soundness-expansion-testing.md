# Soundness (expansion testing)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Le Chatelier method: cement paste placed in a split brass mold (indicator needles 170 mm apart), cured 24 hours in water at 20°C, then boiled 3 hours. Expansion must not exceed 10 mm. Autoclave method (ASTM C151): cement paste bars steamed at 2.0 MPa (215°C) for 3 hours; expansion must not exceed 0.8%. These tests detect excessive free lime (CaO) or periclase (MgO) that hydrate slowly in hardened concrete, causing delayed expansion and cracking months or years after placement.
