# Bauxite processing for high-alumina refractories

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Bauxite (Al₂O₃ 55-70%, remainder SiO₂, Fe₂O₃, TiO₂) is calcined at 1500-1600°C to produce dense, stable corundum (α-Al₂O₃) grains. The calcination drives off chemically bound water and converts the aluminum hydroxide minerals (gibbsite, boehmite) to corundum. The resulting "calcined bauxite" has bulk density 3.0-3.3 g/cm³ and is graded by Al₂O₃ content: 70%, 80%, 85%, 90% grades.
