# Distribution

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: energy, health, knowledge, photolithography, polymers

The gutta-percha belt extends from Myanmar and Thailand through Malaya and Indonesia to the Philippines and Papua New Guinea. Peak production areas were the Malay Peninsula and the islands of Borneo and Sumatra. The geographic concentration of gutta-percha trees in a relatively narrow tropical band mirrored the concentration of rubber production, creating similar supply chain vulnerabilities for consuming nations.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Distribution refers to the geographic sourcing and logistical delivery of materials, energy, and information. Raw material distribution determines supply chain vulnerability — gutta-percha and rubber are both concentrated in narrow tropical bands. Energy distribution (transmission and distribution grids) moves electricity from generation to consumption. Knowledge distribution (printing and libraries) spreads technical information. In semiconductor fabrication, particle distribution in cleanrooms determines yield.

## Technical Details

In electrical power systems, distribution refers to the lower-voltage network (100-400V) delivering electricity from substations to end users. Distribution follows transmission (high-voltage long-distance transport) and uses single-phase or three-phase service with fuses or circuit breakers at each load for overcurrent protection. Power distribution design must balance voltage drop (longer runs need thicker conductors or higher transmission voltage) against infrastructure cost.

In cleanroom design, particle distribution follows statistical models that directly affect yield. The exponential yield model — Yield = (1 - D·A)ⁿ where D is defect density, A is die area, and n is the number of critical layers — means that even small improvements in particle distribution translate to large yield gains. Cleanrooms are classified by particle concentration (ISO Class 1 through Class 9), with semiconductor processing requiring Class 1-5.

In pharmaceutical logistics, distribution tracks lot numbers and expiry dates at each transfer point using first-expire-first-out (FEFO) stock rotation. Segregating expired drugs prevents re-labeling or redistribution.

In printing, type distribution refers to returning individual metal type characters to their proper compartments in the type case after printing — a laborious step that movable type imposes on every print run.
