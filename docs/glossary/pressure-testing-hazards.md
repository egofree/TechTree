# Pressure testing hazards

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: measurement

Hydraulic and pneumatic proof-testing of Bourdon gauges, manometer fittings, and pressure connections risks explosive failure. A Bourdon tube at 100 bar stores sufficient strain energy to launch brass fragments through sheet metal. Test behind a polycarbonate blast shield (minimum 6 mm thickness). Use hydraulic fluid (incompressible — stores minimal energy) rather than compressed air (compressible — stores energy proportional to volume × pressure) for all proof tests.
