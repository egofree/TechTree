SPECIFIC EXPOSURE LIMITS INCLUDED
==================================

Hydrofluoric Acid (HF):
  TLV-TWA: 0.5 ppm, PEL: 3 ppm, IDLH: 30 ppm
  NFPA 704: Health 4, Flammability 0, Instability 1, COR

Silane (SiH4):
  TLV-TWA: 5 ppm, PEL: 5 ppm, IDLH: withdrawn (pyrophoric)
  NFPA 704: Health 1, Flammability 4, Instability 3, PYRO

Arsine (AsH3):
  TLV-TWA: 0.005 ppm (5 ppb), PEL: 0.05 ppm, IDLH: 3 ppm
  NFPA 704: Health 4, Flammability 4, Instability 2

Phosphine (PH3):
  TLV-TWA: 0.3 ppm, TLV-STEL: 1 ppm, PEL: 0.3 ppm, IDLH: 50 ppm
  NFPA 704: Health 4, Flammability 4, Instability 2

Hydrogen Chloride (HCl):
  TLV-Ceiling: 2 ppm, PEL: 5 ppm, IDLH: 50 ppm
  NFPA 704: Health 3, Flammability 0, Instability 1, COR

Ammonia (NH3):
  TLV-TWA: 25 ppm, TLV-STEL: 35 ppm, PEL: 50 ppm, IDLH: 300 ppm
  NFPA 704: Health 3, Flammability 1, Instability 0

Sulfuric Acid (H2SO4):
  TLV-TWA: 0.2 mg/m3, IDLH: 15 mg/m3
  NFPA 704: Health 3, Flammability 0, Instability 2, COR

Hydrogen Peroxide (H2O2):
  TLV-TWA: 1 ppm, IDLH: 75 ppm
  NFPA 704: Health 3, Flammability 0, Instability 3, OXY

Nitrogen Trifluoride (NF3):
  TLV-TWA: 10 ppm

TOPICS COVERED
==============

Chemical Safety:
  - Semiconductor process chemicals (HF, silane, arsine, phosphine, HCl, NH3)
  - TLV-TWA, PEL, IDLH, ERPG values for each chemical
  - NFPA 704 diamond ratings with full explanations
  - Chemical compatibility matrix
  - Gas classification (pyrophoric, toxic, corrosive, flammable, inert, oxidizer)
  - SDS requirements (GHS 16-section)
  - Exposure monitoring protocols (continuous gas monitoring, personal sampling)
  - Additional chemicals: piranha, H2O2, NF3, PFCs (CF4, C2F6)

Ventilation & Exhaust:
  - LEV capture velocities for semiconductor operations
  - Fume hood design (face velocity 0.4-0.6 m/s)
  - Wet bench exhaust systems
  - Gas cabinet design (200+ CFM, fire-rated, gas detection)
  - Duct material selection (PVDF for HF, 316L SS for toxic gases)
  - Point-of-use abatement (thermal oxidizer, plasma, wet/dry scrubbers)
  - Central scrubbing systems (acid, toxic gas)
  - Cleanroom air management (makeup air, pressurization, energy recovery)
  - Maintenance and inspection schedules

PPE:
  - PPE selection matrix by operation (HF, silane, arsine, solvents, CMP)
  - Respiratory protection (half-face, full-face, PAPR, SCBA, supplied air)
  - Chemical glove selection (neoprene for HF, nitrile for solvents, butyl for ketones)
  - Eye/face protection (safety glasses, goggles, face shields)
  - Body protection (chemical suits, aprons, cleanroom garments)
  - PPE program management (written program, training, fit testing)
  - Specialized PPE kits (HF station, gas leak response)

Emergency Response:
  - Eyewash station specifications (0.4 L/min, tepid water, 10-second rule)
  - Deluge shower specifications (75 L/min, 15-minute continuous)
  - Spill kits by type (acid, HF-specific, solvent, hydride gas)
  - Chemical-specific first aid (HF, silane fire, arsine, phosphine)
  - Evacuation procedures (3-tier alarm system)
  - Emergency response organization
  - Drill requirements

Waste Management:
  - Waste stream classification table
  - Fluoride treatment (CaCl2 precipitation → CaF2)
  - Acid neutralization (NaOH)
  - Heavy metal treatment (hydroxide/sulfide precipitation, Cr(VI) reduction)
  - Solvent recovery (distillation)
  - TMAH biological degradation
  - Effluent monitoring (continuous and periodic parameters)
  - Waste minimization strategies
  - Waste segregation rules
