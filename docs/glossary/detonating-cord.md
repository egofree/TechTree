# Detonating cord

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

PETN (pentaerythritol tetranitrate) core (3-40 g/m) in textile/plastic sheath. Detonation velocity 6,000-7,000 m/s. Used to connect multiple boreholes in a surface pattern: one blast initiates the entire cord network simultaneously. Downlines: cord extends from surface to the booster in the borehole, replacing a detonating cap in some designs.
