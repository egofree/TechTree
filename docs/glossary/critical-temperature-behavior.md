# Critical temperature behavior

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Below 600°C, silica bricks are vulnerable to large volume changes from the α→β phase transitions of cristobalite (around 270°C) and tridymite (around 160°C). These inversions involve 2-5% volume change and can crack the brick if heating or cooling is too rapid through these temperature ranges. Once above 600°C, silica bricks become remarkably stable, with near-zero thermal expansion. This makes them ideal for coke oven walls and glass furnace crowns that operate continuously above 1000°C.
