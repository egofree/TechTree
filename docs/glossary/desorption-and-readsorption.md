# Desorption and readsorption

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: gas-handling

After a system reaches base pressure and the bake-out is complete, the chamber walls are relatively clean. But every time the system is vented to air (even with dry nitrogen), water vapor and atmospheric gases adsorb on the internal surfaces. The first pump-down after a vent is always slower than subsequent pump-downs on the same system without venting, because the freshly adsorbed gas layers must be desorbed.
