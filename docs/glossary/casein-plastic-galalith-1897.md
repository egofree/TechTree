# Casein plastic (Galalith, 1897)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Milk protein (casein) precipitated from skim milk by acid (rennet or lactic acid), then hardened by immersion in formaldehyde solution (40% formalin, 2-7 days at room temperature or several hours at 50-60°C). The formaldehyde cross-links the protein chains via methylene bridges (—CH₂—) between amino groups on adjacent protein molecules, creating a hard, horn-like material.
