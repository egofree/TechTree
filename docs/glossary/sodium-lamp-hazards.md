# Sodium lamp hazards

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: measurement

High-pressure sodium lamps operate at internal pressures above 0.1 MPa and temperatures above 700°C. Lamp envelope failure ejects hot sodium metal, which reacts violently with water and skin moisture. Handle with face shield and leather gloves. Enclose in a protective outer envelope. Low-pressure sodium lamps (SOX type, used in polarimeters) operate at lower pressure but still contain sodium.
