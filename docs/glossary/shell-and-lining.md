# Shell and lining

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The SAF shell is a large-diameter (3-12 m), relatively shallow steel cylinder lined with carbon blocks or rammed carbon mass. The carbon lining withstands the strongly reducing environment and temperatures above 1800°C that would destroy MgO or Al₂O₃ refractories. Lining life: 2-5 years for the sidewalls, though the hearth may last 10+ years. The shell is often open-top or semi-covered, with fume extraction hoods above.
