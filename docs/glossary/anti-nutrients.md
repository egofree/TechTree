# Anti-nutrients

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: plants

Many raw plants contain compounds that interfere with nutrient absorption or are mildly toxic. Oxalates (spinach, rhubarb leaves) can cause kidney damage at high doses — boil and discard water. Phytic acid in raw seeds and grains binds minerals — soaking, sprouting, or fermentation neutralizes it. Tannins in acorns and some tree fruits are bitter and reduce protein digestion — leach in repeated changes of water.
