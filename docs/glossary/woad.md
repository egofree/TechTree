# Woad

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Woad (*Isatis tinctoria*) — Biennial (flowers in second year, then dies). Sow seed in early spring, 1 cm deep, 30 cm between plants. First-year leaves harvested 2-3 times per season. Prefers rich, alkaline soil. Yield: ~3-5 kg fresh leaf per 10 m² per cutting. Each kg fresh leaf yields ~2-4 g indigotin pigment. Grows throughout temperate Europe — was the primary blue dye source before imported indigo. See: [Dyeing](../textiles/dyeing.md)
