# Silica brick raw materials

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Quartzite rock (SiO₂ >97%) is the primary raw material. The quartzite is crushed to 0-5 mm particle size, mixed with 2-3% milk of lime (Ca(OH)₂ as bond) and 0.5-1.0% iron oxide (mineralizer), pressed into bricks at 50-100 MPa, and fired at 1450-1500°C for 48-96 hours. The long firing time is necessary to convert quartz to cristobalite and tridymite (>80% conversion required for dimensional stability in service).
