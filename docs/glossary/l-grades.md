# L-grades

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

(304L, 316L): Low carbon variants (≤0.03% C). Carbon content is critical because chromium carbides (Cr₂₃C₆) precipitate at grain boundaries when the steel is heated in the 425-815°C range — a process called **sensitization**. Carbide precipitation depletes chromium from the grain boundary region below the 10.5% threshold, creating a narrow zone susceptible to intergranular corrosion (weld decay).
