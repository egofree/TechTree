# Task 23: Precision Motion Control Domain

## SIK Placement Test

### Precision Motion Control vs Machine-Tools

**Infrastructure overlap: ~30%**
- Machine-tools: lathes, mills, grinders, EDM, cutting tools, ball screws, CNC controllers
- Precision motion: linear motors, piezo actuators, air bearing stages, laser interferometers, active vibration platforms
- Overlap: precision grinding (stage components), air bearings (mentioned in edm-cnc.md ultra-precision CNC), CNC controllers

**Knowledge overlap: ~25%**
- Machine-tools: cutting physics, tool materials, feeds/speeds, G-code programming
- Precision motion: control theory (PID/feedforward), structural dynamics, piezoelectric physics, magnetic bearing design, servo loop tuning, interferometry

**Practitioner overlap: ~20%**
- Machine tools: machinists, CNC programmers, tool-and-die makers
- Precision motion: mechatronics engineers, servo/control engineers, dynamics specialists

**Result: All dimensions <50%. Precision Motion Control is a NEW standalone domain.**

### Precision Motion Control vs Measurement

**Infrastructure overlap: ~40%** (shared laser interferometers, optical encoders, calibration)
**Knowledge overlap: ~35%** (shared metrology principles)
**Practitioner overlap: ~25%** (metrologists vs mechatronics engineers)

**Result: Below threshold on all dimensions.**

### Decision: NEW DOMAIN `precision-motion/`
- Sufficient size: 4 capabilities, each with substantial content
- Organizing axis: Precision Ladder (micron stages → nanometer wafer scanner positioning)
- No inter-domain coupling override needed
