# Magnesite processing

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Raw magnesite (MgCO₃) is mined from deposits in China, Russia, North Korea, Turkey, and Brazil. Beneficiation: crush, grind, and remove silica and iron impurities by flotation or magnetic separation. Calcination stages: (1) light-burned at 900-1100°C (reactive, used for Mg(OH)₂ production and refractory castables), (2) dead-burned at 1700-2000°C in shaft or rotary kilns (dense, inert periclase grains for brickmaking).
