# Water

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: animals, ceramics, chemistry, health, machine-tools

Clean water is non-negotiable. Contaminated water spreads leptospirosis, giardiasis, and coccidiosis. Troughs or natural water sources must be accessible within 500 m of grazing. Flow rate from a spring or well should exceed peak demand (morning and evening drinking).

## Context in the Tech Tree

Water is the most widely used substance in industrial civilization — as a chemical reactant, heat transfer medium, solvent, cleaning agent, and biological necessity. In [Animal Domestication](../animals/domestication.md), clean water supply is fundamental to livestock health and productivity. In [Lime](../ceramics/lime.md) production, water slakes quicklime to produce hydrated lime for mortar and whitewash. In [Cement](../chemistry/cement.md), water-cement ratio (0.4-0.7 by mass) directly determines concrete strength — lower ratio produces stronger concrete but reduces workability. In [Pharmacology](../health/pharmacology.md), water is the universal solvent for pharmaceutical preparation. In [Casting](../machine-tools/casting.md), water cools molds and serves as a component of some bonding sands.

## Technical Details

In concrete production, the water-cement ratio is the single most important mix parameter. At 0.40, concrete achieves high strength (50+ MPa) but is stiff and difficult to place. At 0.50, standard structural concrete (30-40 MPa) with good workability results. Above 0.70, strength drops below 15 MPa as excess water creates capillary pores that weaken the structure. Only 25-35% of water by weight of cement is consumed chemically in hydration; the excess creates porosity.

For livestock, water quality directly affects animal health and productivity. Peak demand occurs during morning and evening drinking periods. A standard trough for 20 cattle needs to deliver 400 L/hour during these peaks. In freezing conditions, troughs must be heated or delivered to manually — snow is not an adequate water source because animals cannot consume enough to meet their needs.

In industrial cooling, water's high specific heat capacity (4.184 J/g·°C) and heat of vaporization (2,260 J/g) make it an excellent heat transfer medium. Recirculating cooling systems with cooling towers reject process heat through evaporative cooling.
