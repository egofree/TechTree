# French polish

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

The highest-quality traditional wood finish. Shellac dissolved in ethanol (typically 2 lb cut = 1 lb shellac per gallon alcohol = ~240 g/L) applied by hand with a rubber — a pad of cloth wrapped around a wad of cotton, charged with shellac and a drop of oil (linseed or mineral oil to lubricate). The rubber is stroked rapidly across the wood surface in overlapping passes, building up thin coats. After 20-40 coats: a deep, glossy, mirror-like finish with extraordinary depth of color.
