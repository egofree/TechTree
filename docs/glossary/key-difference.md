# Key difference

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Coke is stronger (essential for large blast furnaces), burns hotter (essential for silicon reduction), and is available in unlimited quantities from mining. Charcoal is cleaner (no sulfur/phosphorus) but weaker and limited by forest resources. The transition from charcoal to coke was the pivotal moment that enabled large-scale iron and steel production.
