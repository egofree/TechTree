# Reduction smelting

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

SnO₂ + 2C → Sn + 2CO at 1200-1300°C in a reverberatory or electric furnace. Two-stage smelting is common: first stage produces impure tin (95-99% Sn) with a high-tin slag; second stage re-smelts the slag at higher temperature with more carbon and flux (limestone) to recover remaining tin, producing a low-grade tin and a discard slag.
