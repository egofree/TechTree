# Cupola furnace

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Cupola furnace (for remelting pig iron and scrap) — - **Construction**: Cylindrical steel shell (~1-3 m diameter), lined with refractory brick. Charging door at middle height. Tuyeres (air nozzles) near the bottom connected to blower. Tap hole at the very bottom for molten iron. Slag hole slightly above the tap hole (slag floats on iron). - **Operation**: Layer coke (fuel) and pig iron/scrap (metal charge) alternately through the charging door.
