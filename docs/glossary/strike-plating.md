# Strike plating

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

A thin initial layer (0.1-1 µm) deposited from a specialized strike bath at high current density and low metal concentration. Ensures adhesion on difficult substrates (stainless steel, aluminum). Without a strike, the main plated layer may peel. Common: Woods nickel strike (NiCl₂ + HCl, pH <1) for stainless steel; zincate dip + cyanide copper strike for aluminum.
