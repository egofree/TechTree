# Flash smelting

> **Type**: process | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Flash smelting (Outokumpu process, dominant since 1970s) — dry concentrate (moisture <0.3%) is blown with preheated air or oxygen-enriched air (30-95% O₂) through a burner into a tall furnace shaft. The sulfide particles ignite and self-roast in flight — the exothermic oxidation of iron sulfides (2FeS + 3O₂ → 2FeO + 2SO₂) supplies 40-70% of the process heat.
