# Flywheel energy storage

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: energy

Energy storage in a rotating mass: kinetic energy (E = ½Iω²) is stored by spinning a heavy rotor and extracted by decelerating it. Modern flywheels achieve 5–50 Wh/kg using composite rotors at 10,000–50,000 RPM in vacuum bearings; useful for short-duration high-power applications.
