# Piranha clean

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: photolithography

(H₂SO₄:H₂O₂ 3:1 to 4:1): The most aggressive organic removal chemistry available. The mixture generates persulfuric acid (Caro's acid, H₂SO₅), a powerful oxidizer that attacks even heavily crosslinked carbon. Temperature: 100-130°C (exothermic mixing provides the heat). Immersion time: 10-30 minutes for heavily crosslinked resist. Must be used in a dedicated acid fume hood with acid-resistant plumbing (Piranha attacks most organic drain materials).
