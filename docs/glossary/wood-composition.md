# Wood composition

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Dry hardwood contains approximately 48-50% cellulose, 20-25% hemicellulose, 20-25% lignin, and 1-5% extractives (resins, terpenes, tannins). Softwoods have similar cellulose content but higher lignin (25-30%) and different hemicellulose composition (more mannose, less xylose than hardwoods). Higher heating value (HHV) of dry wood: 18-20 MJ/kg (slight variation by species; resinous softwoods are marginally higher due to hydrocarbon extractives).
