# Natural cement

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: ceramics

Overfired hydraulic lime (1100-1200°C) produces a quick-setting cement. Grind the resulting clinker to fine powder. Mix with water — sets in 15-30 minutes. Stronger than hydraulic lime. Roman cement (natural cement from septaria nodules) was the standard water-resistant binder before Portland cement. The step from natural cement to Portland cement is small — it requires only a slightly higher firing temperature (1400-1450°C) and a precisely controlled raw mix.
