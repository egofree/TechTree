# Acrylic emulsion

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Polymer particles (methyl methacrylate/butyl acrylate copolymers) dispersed in water — the dominant binder in modern house paints. Film forms by coalescence as water evaporates — polymer particles fuse into continuous film above minimum film-forming temperature (MFFT, typically 5-15°C). Fast drying (1-4 hours), low odor, excellent UV resistance, water cleanup.
