# Processing

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: foundational | **Domains**: plants, polymers, textiles, mining

Amber can be pressed and molded when heated above 150°C. "Pressed amber" (Ambroid) is made by fusing small amber fragments under heat and pressure — heat to 180-200°C under 10-20 MPa in a steel mold. The resulting block can be carved, turned on a lathe, or polished like natural amber.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Processing — the transformation of raw materials into useful forms — is the fundamental activity of industrial bootstrapping. In [Natural Polymers](../polymers/natural.md), raw rubber latex is processed through coagulation, mastication, and vulcanization. In [Thermoplastics](../polymers/thermoplastics.md), polymer granules are processed by injection molding, extrusion, and thermoforming. In [Edible Plants](../plants/edible-plants.md) and [Structural Plants](../plants/structural-plants.md), plant materials are processed by drying, mechanical separation, and chemical treatment. In [Textile Fibers](../textiles/fibers.md) and [Mining Processing](../mining/processing.md), raw materials are separated, refined, and formed into usable products.

## Technical Details

Processing methods in the tech tree span a continuum from simple mechanical operations (grinding, cutting, pressing) through thermal processes (heating, melting, annealing) to chemical transformations (leaching, reduction, polymerization). The bootstrapping challenge is that each processing level typically requires tools and materials produced by earlier processing levels.

Amber processing illustrates thermoplastic behavior in natural materials: heat softens the resin, pressure consolidates fragments, and cooling locks the shape. This same principle applies to gutta-percha (softens at 60-80°C), shellac (dissolves in alcohol, thermoplastic at 75-85°C), and synthetic thermoplastics (PE softens at 105-135°C, PP at 160-170°C).

Gutta-percha processing follows a specific sequence: coagulation (boiling leaves to separate the polymer), mastication (kneading at 50-60°C), washing (removing plant matter), rolling (forming sheets at 60-80°C), and refining (dissolving in turpentine for electrical-grade purity). Each step progressively improves the material quality from crude harvest to specification-grade product.
