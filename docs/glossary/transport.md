# Transport

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: chemistry, energy, textiles

By barge (cheapest per tonne-km, ~0.01-0.03 $/tkm), rail (standard for inland transport, 0.03-0.08 $/tkm), truck (flexible but expensive, 0.10-0.30 $/tkm), or conveyor (mine-mouth to power station, <10 km distance). A standard rail gondola car carries 90-100 tonnes of coal; a unit train of 100 cars delivers ~10,000 tonnes.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Transport — the movement of raw materials, intermediate goods, and finished products — is a critical enabler and cost factor throughout the industrial bootstrapping sequence. In [Coal](../energy/coal.md) supply, transport cost often exceeds mining cost, making proximity to fuel sources a dominant factor in plant siting. In [Ammonia](../chemistry/ammonia.md) production, the transport of natural gas feedstock or finished fertilizer determines plant location economics. In [Rope Making](../textiles/rope-making.md), transport of natural fiber raw materials from agricultural regions to manufacturing centers was historically a major logistics challenge.

## Technical Details

Transport cost per tonne-kilometer varies dramatically by mode. Water transport (barge, ship) is cheapest due to low rolling resistance and high payload capacity, but limited to waterway-accessible locations. Rail offers the best balance of cost, speed, and inland reach — a unit train of 100 gondola cars carrying 10,000 tonnes of coal can deliver fuel from mine to power plant at roughly one-third the per-tonne cost of truck transport.

Truck transport provides flexibility and door-to-door delivery but at 3-10× the cost of rail or barge. For bulk commodities (coal, ore, limestone, cement), trucking is economical only for short distances (<50 km) or where no rail or water infrastructure exists.

Conveyor systems serve specialized short-distance transport (mine to processing plant, quarry to cement kiln) with continuous operation and low per-tonne cost, but require high capital investment and fixed routing.

The choice of transport mode influences plant location, storage requirements, and inventory management throughout the supply chain.
