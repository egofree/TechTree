# Acidic vs Basic Bessemer (Thomas process)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: metals

Two variants of the Bessemer converter distinguished by their refractory lining: acidic (silica) cannot remove phosphorus, while basic (dolomite/magnesite, called the Thomas process) can dephosphorize high-phosphorus pig iron. The Thomas process was essential for using phosphoric European iron ores.
