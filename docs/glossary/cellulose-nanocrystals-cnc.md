# Cellulose nanocrystals (CNC)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Dissolving pulp treated with 64% H₂SO₄ at 45°C for 30-60 minutes — acid hydrolyzes amorphous cellulose, leaving crystalline rod-shaped nanoparticles (5-20 nm diameter, 100-300 nm length). Extraordinary mechanical properties: Young's modulus ~150 GPa (stiffer than Kevlar), tensile strength ~7 GPa. Applications: nanocomposite reinforcement, biomedical scaffolds, iridescent films (self-assembly into chiral nematic structures), rheology modifier, security features (optical signatures).
