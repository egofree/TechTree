# Catchment-to-storage ratio

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

As a rule, the catchment area should be sized to fill the storage tank at least 1.5-2× during the wet season, allowing for below-average rainfall years. A 100 m² roof with 600 mm annual rainfall, of which 450 mm falls in a 4-month wet season, captures roughly 38,000 liters during the wet months. This fills a 30 m³ tank with margin to spare in a normal year but may fall short in a drought year receiving only 60% of average rainfall.
