# Equals sign

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

Equals sign (=) — robert Recorde, 1557 — "noe .2. thynges, can be moare equalle." - **Decimal point** (or comma): Simon Stevin, 1585 — unified whole numbers and fractions into a single notation. - **Exponential notation**: Descartes, 1637 — x², x³ for powers. Enables compact representation of physical laws (F = ma, E = mc²). - **Chemical notation**: Berzelius, 1814 — letter symbols for elements (H, O, C, Fe), subscript numbers for proportions (H₂O, Fe₂O₃).
