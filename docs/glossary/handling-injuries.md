# Handling injuries

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: animals

A kick from a cow delivers 1,500-2,000 N of force, enough to fracture ribs or cause internal bleeding. A horse kick is similarly powerful and directed backward. A bull charge can kill. Never stand directly behind any large animal. Always have an escape route planned when working in close quarters. Use a crush or chute for any procedure on cattle. Two people should be present when handling large animals.
