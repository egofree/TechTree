# Tapping detail

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

The spiral cut descends from upper-left to lower-right at ~30° from horizontal, cutting through the bark to a depth of 1.5-2 mm — just reaching but not penetrating the cambium. Each tapping removes a thin shaving of bark (~1-2 mm) from the lower edge of the previous cut, gradually moving down the trunk over years. A single panel can be tapped for 3-5 years before the bark must regenerate. Skilled tappers process 200-300 trees per morning.
