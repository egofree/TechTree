# Surface seeps

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Surface seeps (Foundations-Metallurgy stages) — Oil seeps to surface naturally in many geological settings. Collect by hand with buckets or by digging shallow pits (1-3 m deep) where oil accumulates. Filter through cloth to remove sand and water. Yield: 5-50 liters/day per seep. - **Oil springs**: Where oil flows with water, dig collecting pool. Oil floats on water — skim with ladles. This is how the first oil wells began (Titusville, Pennsylvania, 1859). See: [Petroleum & Alternative Chemistry](../chemistry/petroleum-alternatives.md)
