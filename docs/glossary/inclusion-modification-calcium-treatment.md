# Inclusion modification — calcium treatment

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Inject Ca (as CaSi wire, 0.3-1.5 kg/t) to transform hard, solid Al₂O₃ clusters (which clog continuous caster nozzles and cause fatigue failure in finished steel) into liquid calcium aluminates (12CaO·7Al₂O₃) that remain spherical during rolling and are less harmful to mechanical properties. Calcium also modifies MnS stringers (which cause anisotropic toughness) into globular CaS + (Ca,Mn)S inclusions. Essential for clean steel production.
