# Marine coatings

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Ship hulls face immersion in seawater, UV exposure, mechanical damage, and fouling. Antifouling coatings release biocides (copper oxide, zinc pyrithione — tributyltin banned since 2008) to prevent barnacle, algae, and tubeworm attachment. Modern self-polishing copolymers (SPC): acrylic polymer backbone with biocide chemically bonded — hydrolysis at the coating surface slowly releases biocide and exposes fresh surface. Antifouling coating life: 3-5 years between dry-docking.
