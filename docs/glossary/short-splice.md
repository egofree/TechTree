# Short splice

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Short splice (joining two rope ends permanently) — Unlay both rope ends for ~10 rope diameters. Interleave the 6 strands (3 from each end) by alternating them. Tuck each strand through the opposing rope body, following the lay. - Result: a permanent join. Doubles the diameter at the splice — will not pass through pulleys or fairleads. Used where a strong join is needed and rope does not need to run through blocks. See: [Rope and Cordage](../textiles/rope-making.md)
