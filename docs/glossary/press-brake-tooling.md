# Press brake tooling

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: machine-tools

The upper punch (V-blade) and lower V-die determine the bend geometry. Acute-angle punches (30° or 60° tip) allow bends tighter than 90°. Gooseneck punches clear previously-formed flanges on complex parts. Die shoulders should be polished to prevent marking the workpiece surface. Standard V-die widths range from 6 mm (for thin sheet) to 100 mm (for thick plate).
