# Spinning mule

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

(Crompton, 1779): Combined the roller drafting of the water frame with the manual carriage travel of the hand spinning wheel. The carriage moves outward 1.5-2 m, simultaneously drawing and twisting the yarn. Then the carriage returns, winding the spun yarn onto the spindles. This intermittent action allowed much finer yarn than the water frame: the mule could spin yarn fine enough to match hand-spun Indian muslin (Ne 100+), which the water frame could not achieve.
