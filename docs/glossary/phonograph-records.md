# Phonograph records

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Shellac was the primary binder in 78 RPM records from the 1890s through the 1950s. The record material was a composite: ~25% shellac binder, ~75% filler (crushed slate ~40%, limestone ~20%, carbon black for color ~5%, cotton flock for strength ~10%), plus small amounts of plasticizer (stearic acid ~2%) and lubricant (zinc stearate ~1%).
