# Clay tablet durability

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

Fired clay tablets represent the most durable writing medium in human history. The ~500,000 surviving cuneiform tablets from Mesopotamia, many readable after 4000+ years, testify to ceramic permanence. Tablets fired to 800-1000°C become chemically stable and resistant to water, insects, mold, and fire (they are already fired ceramic).
