# Molten metal burns

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Copper and bronze melt at 1085-1200°C and cause devastating deep-tissue burns on skin contact. Molten metal splashes readily when poured into damp or cold molds. Wear heavy leather apron, gauntlet gloves, face shield, and closed-toe boots. Preheat all molds and crucibles to 200-400°C before contact with molten metal — a cold or damp crucible may shatter violently, spraying molten copper. Never pour molten metal into wet molds; trapped moisture flashes to steam and ejects metal explosively.
