# Manufacturing

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: computing, machine-tools, mining, polymers

The organized system of production that transforms raw materials into finished goods using machinery, tooling, and standardized processes. Manufacturing encompasses the broader production infrastructure — factory layout, process flow, quality control, and scale-up — as distinct from the specific chemical or mechanical steps of "manufacture." Gutta-percha cable manufacturing illustrates the system: heating to 80-100°C, extruding around copper conductors through dies, cooling in water baths, and building up 3-5 mm insulation thickness in multiple passes.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Manufacturing capability defines the boundary between prototype and production. The computing domain requires manufacturing precision to produce mechanical calculators (Babbage-era tolerances of ±0.025 mm for gear teeth), electronic computers (vacuum tubes, printed circuit boards), and integrated circuits (photolithographic patterning at micron scale). The machine-tools domain provides the manufacturing equipment — lathes, mills, grinders, presses — that every other domain depends on for precision components.

The polymers domain illustrates manufacturing scale-up: from laboratory-scale polymerization to production extrusion, injection molding, and transfer molding. Thermoplastic processing alone encompasses dozens of manufacturing methods (injection molding, blow molding, extrusion, thermoforming, rotational molding), each with specific temperature, pressure, and tooling requirements.

## Technical Details

Manufacturing systems share common organizational principles: process flow (raw material → intermediate → finished product), quality gates (inspection and testing at each stage), tooling (dies, molds, fixtures specific to the product), and throughput targets (units per hour, yield percentage). The key manufacturing challenge is repeatability — producing identical parts within tolerance specifications across production runs.

Manufacturing also encompasses the metrology and inspection systems that verify output quality. Dimensional inspection (gauges, micrometers, coordinate measuring machines), material testing (tensile, hardness, impact), and functional testing (leak testing, electrical continuity, performance verification) are integral to the manufacturing process, not afterthoughts.
