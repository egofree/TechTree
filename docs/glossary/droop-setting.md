# Droop setting

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Governor droop is the percentage speed change from no-load to full-load. A 4% droop means the turbine runs 4% faster unloaded than at rated load. Droop is essential for load sharing between parallel turbines: each turbine picks up load in proportion to its droop setting. Lower droop gives faster response but risks hunting (oscillation). Isochronous (0% droop) mode is possible with electronic governors for single-turbine installations.
