# Softening

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: ceramics, metals

Air or oxygen blown through molten lead at 600-700°C. As, Sb, Sn oxidize preferentially and skimmed as "speiss" and "matte." - **Parkes process (desilverization)**: Add zinc at 450-480°C. Silver (and gold) dissolve preferentially in zinc, which is immiscible in lead. Zinc-silver crust floats and is skimmed. Zinc is distilled off under vacuum; silver is refined electrolytically.
