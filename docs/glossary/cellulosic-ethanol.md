# Cellulosic ethanol

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Acid hydrolysis (dilute H₂SO₄ at 0.5-2% concentration, 120-200°C, 10-60 minutes) or enzymatic hydrolysis (cellulase enzymes at 45-50°C, pH 4.8-5.2, 48-120 hours) breaks cellulose into fermentable sugars. Yields: 280-340 liters ethanol per tonne of dry biomass (corn stover, wheat straw, wood chips). Enzymatic route gives higher yield but requires enzyme production capability.
