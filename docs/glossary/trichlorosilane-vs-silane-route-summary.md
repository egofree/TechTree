# Trichlorosilane vs Silane Route Summary

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The TCS-to-silane route involves more unit operations (fluidized bed chlorination, multi-column distillation, catalytic redistribution, cryogenic separation) but produces a feedstock (SiH₄) that deposits polysilicon more efficiently in FBR reactors. The direct TCS route (reducing TCS with H₂ in Siemens reactors) has fewer process steps but higher energy consumption per kg of polysilicon.
