# SPICE simulation (analog)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: vlsi-scaling

Solves Kirchhoff's voltage and current laws as coupled differential equations. Simulates transistor-level behavior with high accuracy. Used for analog circuits (amplifiers, PLLs, ADCs), I/O buffers, and critical digital paths. Computationally expensive — a full-chip SPICE simulation is infeasible for billion-transistor designs. Typical SPICE runs cover 1-1000 ns of simulated time for subcircuits of 100-10,000 transistors.
