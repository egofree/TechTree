# Aluminum smelter fluoride emissions

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

HF, CF₄, and C₂F₆ emissions from Hall-Héroult cells are controlled by dry scrubbing: alumina (Al₂O₃) is injected into the cell exhaust gas, where it reacts with HF to form aluminum fluoride (AlF₃). The fluoridated alumina is then fed to the cells (closing the fluoride loop). CF₄ and C₂F₆ are formed during anode effects (voltage spikes from low alumina concentration) and are extremely potent greenhouse gases (GWP₁₀₀: CF₄ = 6,630, C₂F₆ = 11,100).
