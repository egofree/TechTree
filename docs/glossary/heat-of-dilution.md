# Heat of dilution

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Mixing concentrated H₂SO₄ with water releases substantial heat: 74 kJ/mol for diluting 100% H₂SO₄ to infinite dilution. Diluting 1 kg of 98% H₂SO₄ to 50% releases ~420 kJ, enough to raise the solution temperature by over 80°C. This is why acid must always be added to water slowly (never water to acid): the large water volume absorbs the heat safely.
