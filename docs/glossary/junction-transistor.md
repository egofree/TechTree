# Junction transistor

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: computing

Junction transistor (Shockley, 1951) — Structure**: Three-layer semiconductor sandwich: n-p-n or p-n-p. The narrow middle layer (base, ~10-50 μm thick) controls current flow between emitter and collector. - **Advantages over point-contact**: More reproducible, higher gain (β = 50-300), lower noise, better temperature stability.
