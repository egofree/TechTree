# Grating production

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: measurement

Ruled gratings require a precision engine with a diamond tip cutting grooves into a metal (aluminum-on-glass) substrate. Engine precision must be better than 0.1 μm over the full grating width. Replica gratings (pressing plastic onto a ruled master) make the technology accessible without building a ruling engine. Holographic gratings (recording interference fringes in photoresist) offer lower stray light but lack the flexibility of custom groove profiles.
