# Low carbon steel (mild steel, 0.05-0.25% C)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: metals

Iron-carbon alloy containing 0.05–0.25% carbon. The most widely used structural steel: excellent weldability, ductility (20–30% elongation), and toughness. Yield strength 200–400 MPa. Used for beams, plates, sheet, and wire.
