# Melamine-formaldehyde (MF)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Melamine (C₃H₆N₆, produced from urea at 350-400°C, 70-150 atm — the high temperature causes urea to decompose and recombine into the triazine ring structure of melamine) + formaldehyde at 1:2-3 molar ratio. Base-catalyzed methylolation at 60-80°C, followed by acid cure. MF resins cure to hard, heat-resistant (continuous service to 150°C — higher than UF at ~80°C), scratch-resistant, and chemical-resistant surfaces.
