# Knowledge transmission chains

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

(master → journeyman → apprentice): - The chain is the fundamental mechanism of skill preservation across generations. Each master was once an apprentice who learned from a previous master, creating an unbroken lineage of practical knowledge. When a chain breaks (a master dies without training a successor), specialized knowledge can be permanently lost. - **Redundancy requirement**: Never allow a critical skill to depend on a single practitioner.
