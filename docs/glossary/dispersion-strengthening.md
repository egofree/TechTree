# Dispersion strengthening

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Hard, insoluble particles (oxides, carbides) introduced mechanically or by internal oxidation. Unlike precipitates, they do not coarsen at high temperature — making them suitable for creep-resistant alloys. Example: thoria-dispersed (TD) nickel, oxide-dispersion-strengthened (ODS) steels.
