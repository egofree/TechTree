# Quench converter

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

(original design, 1910s-1960s): - Multiple adiabatic catalyst beds (typically 3-5 beds) stacked in a single pressure vessel. - Cold feed gas injected between beds (cold-shot quench) to control temperature. After each bed, the gas heats up (exothermic reaction); quench gas brings it back down before the next bed. - Simple mechanical construction — no internal cooling tubes.
