# Uses

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: ceramics, chemistry, energy, metals, plants

Structural shapes (I-beams, channels, angles — A36 specification at 0.26% C max), sheet and plate (automotive body panels, appliance housings, ductwork), wire (fencing, nails, baling wire), pipe and tubing, cold-headed fasteners (bolts, rivets). The workhorse of civilization — ~90% of all steel produced is mild steel.

## Technical Details

The breadth of uses for mild steel illustrates why it dominates industrial civilization. Its 0.05-0.26% carbon content provides an optimal balance of strength, ductility, weldability, and machinability. At room temperature, mild steel (A36 grade) yields at 250 MPa and has ultimate tensile strength of 400-550 MPa — sufficient for structural applications while remaining formable by bending, rolling, and stamping.

Key use categories for mild steel: structural shapes (beams, columns, channels) for construction and infrastructure; sheet and plate for vehicles, appliances, and enclosures; wire products for fasteners, fencing, and concrete reinforcement; pipe and tubing for fluid transport and mechanical applications; and cold-headed fasteners that hold virtually every mechanical assembly together.

Similarly, the uses of acids illustrate cascading applications: sulfuric acid (the "king of chemicals") is used in steel pickling, fertilizer production, petroleum refining, battery manufacturing, and chemical synthesis — a single chemical enabling dozens of downstream processes.
