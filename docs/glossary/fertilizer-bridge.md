# Fertilizer bridge

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Phosphoric acid is reacted with ammonia to produce monoammonium phosphate (MAP, 11-52-0) and diammonium phosphate (DAP, 18-46-0) — the world's most important phosphate fertilizers. Reacted with phosphate rock to produce superphosphate (single: Ca(H₂PO₄)₂, or triple: higher P content).
