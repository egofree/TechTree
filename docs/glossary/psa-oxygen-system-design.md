# PSA oxygen system design

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

A typical PSA oxygen generator uses two zeolite beds (13X molecular sieve) operating in alternating cycles. Compressed air (3-5 bar) enters Bed A: N₂ is adsorbed by the zeolite (strong quadrupole interaction with the zeolite cations), O₂ passes through as product (90-95% purity, balance Ar and N₂). When Bed A saturates with N₂, the feed switches to Bed B. Bed A depressurizes to atmospheric (N₂ desorbs and is vented), then is purged with a small fraction of product O₂ to fully clean the bed.
