# Crazing

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: ceramics

Fine network of crackle lines in the glaze after cooling. The glaze has a higher thermal expansion coefficient than the body, so it contracts more on cooling and cracks. Fix: increase silica in the glaze recipe (lowers thermal expansion), decrease feldspar, or add a small amount of boron (B₂O₃ acts as a flux that also reduces expansion). Crazing weakens the glaze-body bond and allows moisture penetration, though it is sometimes desirable as a decorative effect.
