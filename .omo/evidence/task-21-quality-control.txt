## SIK Placement Test (schema-spec.md §6)

### Quality Control vs Measurement comparison:

1. Infrastructure overlap: ~30%
   - Shared: some measurement instruments (micrometers, gauges, thermocouples)
   - Distinct QC equipment: SPC software systems, sampling tables, Pareto chart tools,
     FMEA worksheets, inspection stations, test fixtures, burn-in chambers, ATE systems
   - Measurement domain builds/calibrates instruments; QC domain applies statistical
     methods to make manufacturing decisions using those instruments

2. Knowledge overlap: ~20%
   - Shared: basic measurement principles
   - Distinct QC knowledge: probability theory, statistical inference, sampling theory,
     process capability analysis, Six Sigma methodology, design of experiments (DOE),
     reliability engineering, FMEA methodology, yield modeling (Murphy's, Seeds)
   - Measurement: optics, thermodynamics, instrument physics, calibration science
   - QC: statistics, decision theory, risk analysis

3. Practitioner overlap: ~15%
   - Metrologists: calibrate instruments, maintain measurement standards, NIST traceability
   - QC engineers: design sampling plans, analyze control charts, conduct FMEAs,
     drive yield improvement through DMAIC, perform root cause analysis
   - Different training paths, different professional societies (ASQ vs NCSLI)

All three dimensions <50%. DECISION: Quality Control is a standalone domain.

### Quality Control vs Foundations comparison:
- Infrastructure overlap: ~5% (QC needs precision instruments; foundations uses stone/fire)
- Knowledge overlap: ~5% (QC requires statistics/probability; foundations is survival)
- Practitioner overlap: ~5% (completely different skill sets)

Result: Trivially separate. No merge consideration.
