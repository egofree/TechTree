# Heat-sensitive organic purification

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Compounds that decompose at their atmospheric boiling point can be distilled at reduced temperature by steam co-distillation. Glycerol (bp 290°C, decomposes above 200°C) can be steam-distilled at ~180°C. Fatty acids (bp >300°C) can be purified by steam stripping. The principle applies wherever immiscible water-organic systems allow reduced boiling temperature through combined vapor pressure.
