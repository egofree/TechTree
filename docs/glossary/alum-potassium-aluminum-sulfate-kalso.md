# Alum (potassium aluminum sulfate, KAl(SO₄)₂)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

The universal mordant. Source alunite stone (KAl(SO₄)₂(OH)₆) by roasting and leaching, or collect stypteria (naturally occurring alum deposits) from volcanic soils. Dissolve 10-20% of fiber weight in hot water (60-80°C). Soak fiber for 1 hour. Works on both protein and cellulose fibers, though cellulose benefits from a tannin pre-mordant.
