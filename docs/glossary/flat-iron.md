# Flat iron

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

(sad iron): - A solid cast iron slab, 2-4 kg, with a flat bottom and a handle on top. Heated on a stove or over a fire. Working temperature: 150-200°C. The iron is too hot if it scorches a test scrap; too cool if it does not set a crease. - Temperature is tested by spitting on the iron: drops that dance and skitter indicate 150-200°C (the Leidenfrost effect). Drops that evaporate immediately indicate >250°C (too hot for most fabrics). Drops that just sit and steam indicate <120°C (too cool).
