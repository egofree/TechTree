# Duplex telegraphy

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: transport

Sending two messages simultaneously in opposite directions on a single wire. Uses a differential relay and a balancing network (resistors and capacitors that simulate the impedance of the distant station's line). Each station's transmitter creates equal and opposite currents in the two halves of its relay coil, so the local transmitter does not activate the local receiver. Only the distant station's signal activates it. Doubles line capacity without adding wires.
