# Radial-flow converter

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Radial-flow converter (modern, 1960s-present) — Gas flows radially inward through an annular catalyst bed (from outer perimeter to central collection pipe) rather than axially through a cylindrical bed. - Much larger cross-sectional flow area → lower pressure drop (0.2-0.5 MPa vs. 1-2 MPa for axial flow).
