# Pharmaceutical and food coating

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

"Confectioner's glaze" or "pharmaceutical glaze" — purified, de-waxed shellac dissolved in ethanol and sprayed onto candies (jelly beans, candy corn, chocolate sprinkles) and pharmaceutical tablets. The shellac coating provides gloss, moisture barrier (extends shelf life by 2-5× for hygroscopic products), and controlled release (enteric coatings on tablets — shellac resists stomach acid but dissolves in the alkaline intestinal environment, pH >7).
