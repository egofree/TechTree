# Silane pyrophoricity

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

SiH₄ auto-ignites on contact with air at room temperature or below (if impure). Even 2-3% silane in air can auto-detonate without an ignition source. Silane fires burn intensely (producing SiO₂ fume and H₂). **Cannot be extinguished with water or CO₂** — water reacts with burning silane to produce more H₂; CO₂ provides no suppression effect.
