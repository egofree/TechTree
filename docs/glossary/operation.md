# Operation

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: ceramics, chemistry, computing, energy, health, machine-tools, metals, textiles

The active running of equipment, processes, and systems according to established procedures. Operation encompasses the entire cycle from startup through steady-state production to shutdown. In the blast furnace, operation proceeds as: ignite coke bed at tuyere level, turn on blower at 5-15 kPa forced air, molten iron and slag drip down through the coke bed, iron collects in the well, slag floats on iron and overflows through the slag notch, and iron is tapped every 10-20 minutes through the tap hole.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Operation is the most widely referenced activity across the tech tree. Every piece of equipment and every process has operational procedures. Kiln operation involves loading, firing (controlling fuel and air), soaking at temperature, and controlled cooling. Steam engine operation involves boiler firing, steam admission control, condenser management, and lubrication. Computing equipment operation involves power-up sequences, program loading, execution monitoring, and error handling.

The distinction between design/construction and operation is fundamental: a well-designed piece of equipment that is poorly operated will fail, while a mediocre design with excellent operational discipline can produce acceptable results. Operational procedures codify the knowledge of how to run processes correctly — they are as valuable as the physical equipment itself.

## Technical Details

Operational procedures typically follow a standard structure: pre-start checks (verify all systems ready), startup sequence (bring equipment to operating conditions in specified order), steady-state monitoring (track key parameters against expected values), troubleshooting guides (response to off-normal conditions), and shutdown sequence (bring equipment to safe idle state). Each step has defined actions, acceptance criteria, and contingency plans.

Operational safety is paramount. Pre-start safety checks verify that all guards are in place, emergency stops are functional, and personnel are clear of hazardous areas. During operation, continuous monitoring of critical parameters (temperature, pressure, vibration, current draw) detects developing problems before they become failures. Lockout/tagout procedures prevent accidental energization during maintenance. Emergency shutdown procedures provide rapid, safe response to critical failures.
