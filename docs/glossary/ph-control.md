# pH control

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry, mining

Most fermentations are sensitive to pH. S. cerevisiae: pH 4.0-5.0 (acidic conditions suppress bacterial contamination). Lactobacillus: pH 5.5-6.0 (acid inhibition below pH 5). Aspergillus niger (citric acid): pH 2.0-3.0 (maintained naturally by acid production). pH is controlled by automatic addition of acid (H₂SO₄, HCl) or base (NaOH, NH₄OH, CaCO₃ slurry) via peristaltic pumps triggered by a pH controller with setpoint ±0.1 pH unit.
