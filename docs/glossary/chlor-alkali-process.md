# Chlor-alkali process

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Chlor-alkali process (most important industrial electrolysis) — - **Cell types**: - **Diaphragm cell**: Asbestos or polymer diaphragm separates anode and cathode compartments. Prevents Cl₂ and NaOH from mixing (would form NaOCl — bleach). Products: Cl₂ gas, H₂ gas, 10-12% NaOH solution (requires evaporation to 50%). - **Membrane cell** (modern): Ion-exchange membrane (Nafion or equivalent — perfluorinated polymer chemistry). More efficient, produces directly 30-35% NaOH. But membrane requires PTFE-like fluoropolym... See: [Electrolysis](../chemistry/electrolysis.md)
