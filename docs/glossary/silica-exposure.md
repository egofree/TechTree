# Silica exposure

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: silicon

Silicon metal dust (crystalline Si) is less hazardous than pure SiO₂ dust (quartz) for silicosis risk, but SiO₂ contamination from quartz raw material is always present. Occupational exposure limits: 0.025 mg/m³ respirable crystalline silica (8-hour TWA). Achievable only with enclosed, ventilated equipment and respiratory protection. Health monitoring: chest X-ray and lung function testing every 1-3 years for workers with regular silica dust exposure.
