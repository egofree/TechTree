# Halladay pattern (1854)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The classic American farm windmill, designed by Daniel Halladay. The multiblade rotor consists of 15-30 flat or slightly curved steel (or wooden) vanes mounted on a lattice frame. Rotor diameter 3-8 m. The high number of blades gives a high starting torque, essential for starting a reciprocating pump under load. Low tip-speed ratio (~1.0, compared to 5-7 for electricity-generating rotors) means the wheel turns slowly but powerfully.
