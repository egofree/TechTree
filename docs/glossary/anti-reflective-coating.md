# Anti-reflective coating

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: glass

A thin layer of magnesium fluoride (MgF₂) deposited on the glass surface reduces reflection losses. The coating thickness is λ/4 (quarter-wavelength, typically 100-130 nm for visible light), and the coating's refractive index should be the geometric mean of glass (1.52) and air (1.00): √(1.52 × 1.00) = 1.23.
