# MgO-C bricks

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Modern steelmaking uses magnesite-carbon composite bricks (MgO-C). Graphite (8-20% by weight) is added to the magnesite mix. Carbon provides three benefits: (1) non-wetting by slag, reducing chemical corrosion; (2) high thermal conductivity, improving thermal shock resistance; (3) reducing environment at the hot face that protects MgO from slag attack. Bonded with resin or pitch, fired to develop carbon bond. PCE 38-40, service temperature 1700-1800°C.
