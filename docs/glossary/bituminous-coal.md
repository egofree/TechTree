# Bituminous coal

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy, foundations

Black, with a layered fracture pattern (breaks along flat planes). Softer than anthracite; can be scratched with a knife. Energy density: 25-35 MJ/kg. This is the most abundant and widely used industrial coal. It softens and cokes when heated in a restricted-air environment, which is the basis of coke production. When burned in open air, it produces thick smoke and sulfur smell. Bituminous coal is the workhorse fuel for steam engines, forges, and furnaces.
