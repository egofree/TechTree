# Blow the bubble

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: glass

Cap the blowpipe with your thumb and blow a short puff of air into the pipe. The trapped air expands in the molten glass, forming a bubble. Reheat, blow again to expand. Glass temperature: 800-900°C for soda-lime. Blow steadily, not forcefully — the bubble expands as the glass softens under air pressure.
