# White blood cell count

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: health

Fill hemocytometer chamber with diluted blood (1:20 dilution in white cell diluting fluid, which lyses red cells). Count cells in the four corner squares under microscope. Multiply by dilution factor and chamber factor. Normal: 4,000-11,000/μL. Elevated (leukocytosis) in bacterial infection, inflammation. Low (leukopenia) in viral infection, bone marrow suppression, severe sepsis.
