# Flat-felled seam

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Flat-felled seam (strongest standard seam) — - Both seam allowances are trimmed, wrapped around each other, and topstitched flat. Creates a smooth, low-profile seam with no raw edges exposed on either side. - Strength retention: 95-100% of fabric strength. The mechanical interlock of the folded edges distributes stress over a wider area. Standard for jeans inseams, sail panels, awnings, canvas goods. - Construction requires precision folding.
