# Speed ratio

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The peripheral speed of the runner at the pitch circle is expressed as a fraction of the jet velocity: φ = π × D × n / (60 × √(2gH)), where n is the rotational speed in RPM. The optimal speed ratio for maximum efficiency is approximately 0.46 (the runner moves at roughly half the jet speed). This is derived from momentum transfer theory: maximum power transfer occurs when the bucket speed is half the jet speed, because the water exits the bucket with near-zero velocity relative to the ground.
