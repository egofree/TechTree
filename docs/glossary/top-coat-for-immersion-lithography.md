# Top coat for immersion lithography

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: photolithography

At 193 nm immersion, water fills the gap between the projection lens and the wafer. The top coat is a thin (30-90 nm) hydrophobic polymer layer spun onto the resist surface to prevent water from leaching resist components (PAG, solvent) into the immersion fluid. Top coat contact angle with water: 70-90° (hydrophobic enough to prevent water residues, wettable enough for uniform water meniscus during scanning).
