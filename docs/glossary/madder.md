# Madder

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Madder (*Rubia tinctorum*) — Perennial, hardy to Zone 5. Plant root cuttings in spring, spaced 30-40 cm apart in rows 50 cm apart. Requires 2-3 years for roots to reach harvestable dye concentration. Prefers deep, well-drained loam — roots penetrate 60-100 cm. Harvest in autumn of the second or third year. Dry roots thoroughly before storage. Yield: ~2-4 kg dried root per 10 m². Contains 1-3% alizarin by dry weight. See: [Dyeing](../textiles/dyeing.md)
