# Procedure

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: foundational | **Domains**: metals, health, chemistry

Stack or overlap the pieces to be joined. Heat both evenly in forge fire. When metal reaches bright yellow-white (visible through scale breaking surface), quickly remove, brush off loose scale, apply flux if needed, position pieces on anvil, and strike firmly with hammer. Multiple rapid blows. Reheat and re-strike if needed for longer joints.

## Context in the Tech Tree

A procedure is a standardized sequence of steps that reliably produces a desired result. Procedures appear throughout the tech tree: [Iron & Steel Production](../metals/iron-steel.md) documents forge welding procedures; [Pharmacology](../health/pharmacology.md) defines drug preparation procedures (extraction, crystallization, tablet compression); [Casting](../machine-tools/casting.md) specifies mold-making and pouring procedures. Standardized procedures ensure repeatability — critical when training new practitioners or scaling production.

## Technical Details

Effective procedures share common structural elements: a one-sentence purpose statement, scope (applicable materials and limitations), safety requirements (required PPE and identified hazards), materials list with specifications, step-by-step instructions with measurable criteria, and acceptance/rejection standards for the finished product.

In forge welding, the procedure encodes centuries of empirical knowledge: the metal must reach bright yellow-white (approximately 1300-1400°C for mild steel) — too cold and the weld won't take, too hot and the metal burns. The scale must be brushed off because iron oxide prevents metal-to-metal contact. The flux (typically borax or sand) melts and dissolves residual oxide, creating a clean surface. Rapid hammering expels trapped flux and slag while forging the metal together. Each step exists for a specific metallurgical reason.

In pharmaceutical preparation, procedures prevent dosing errors. Geometric dilution ensures uniform mixing of potent drugs. Recrystallization procedures specify solvent choice, cooling rate, and washing steps to achieve target purity. Tablet compression procedures define die pressure, binder percentage, and hardness acceptance range.
