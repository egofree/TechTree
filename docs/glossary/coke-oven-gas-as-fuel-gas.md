# Coke oven gas as fuel gas

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

After tar, naphthalene, benzol, ammonia, and H₂S removal, the cleaned gas (primarily H₂ + CH₄) has a calorific value of ~16-18 MJ/m³. It fuels the coke oven heating flues (consuming ~40-45% of the gas produced), with the surplus available for steelworks reheating furnaces, boiler fuel, or town gas distribution. A typical by-product battery produces 300-350 m³ gas per tonne of coal — roughly self-sustaining for oven heating with 55-60% surplus.
