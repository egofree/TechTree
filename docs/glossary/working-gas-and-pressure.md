# Working gas and pressure

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Helium and hydrogen are the preferred working gases due to their high thermal conductivity and low viscosity, which minimize heat exchanger losses. Operating pressures of 5-20 MPa mean the engine casing must be a pressure vessel. Air at atmospheric pressure works for simple demonstration engines but produces very low power density (roughly 1/10 the output of helium at equivalent pressure).
