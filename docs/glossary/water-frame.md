# Water frame

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Water frame (Arkwright, 1769) — The breakthrough machine for mechanized spinning. Three or four pairs of fluted iron rollers draw the roving (lightly twisted fiber strand) progressively thinner. Each roller pair rotates faster than the previous: typical speed ratios 1:2:4:8 from first to last pair. The flyer spindle inserts twist into the attenuated strand.
