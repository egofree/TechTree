# Filtration

Filtration is the mechanical separation of solids from liquids (or gases) by passing the mixture through a porous medium that retains the solid particles while allowing the fluid to pass. In chemical processing, CaCO₃ precipitate is filtered off using filter presses or rotary vacuum filters. The filtered CaCO₃ is washed and sent to the lime kiln for reburning (CaCO₃ → CaO → Ca(OH)₂), closing the calcium loop in alkali manufacturing.

Industrial filtration equipment ranges from simple gravity bed filters to rotary vacuum filters, filter presses, and cartridge filters. Filter media include woven fabrics (cotton, synthetic), paper, sintered metals, and porous ceramics. The choice depends on particle size, chemical compatibility, throughput requirements, and whether the valuable product is the filtrate (liquid) or the filter cake (solids).

In semiconductor manufacturing, filtration is critical for removing particulate contamination from photoresists, developers, and rinse water—particles as small as 0.1 μm can cause defects on wafers. In water treatment and sanitation, sand filters and membrane filtration provide pathogen removal. Filtration is one of the most universally applicable unit operations across all industrial domains.
