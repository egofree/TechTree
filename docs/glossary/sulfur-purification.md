# Sulfur purification

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: mining

Raw sulfur from volcanic deposits or pyrite roasting contains arsenic and organic impurities. Purify by sublimation: heat sulfur to 445°C in a sealed retort (sulfur boils at this temperature). Sulfur vapor condenses on a cooled surface as bright yellow crystalline "flowers of sulfur." The impurities remain in the retort as residue.
