# Copper

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: gas-handling, health, machine-tools

Copper serves as a gasket material in high-temperature and high-pressure flanged connections, sealing by plastic deformation. OFHC (oxygen-free high-conductivity) copper gaskets are the standard for ultra-high vacuum CF (ConFlat) flanges, where knife-edge flanges cut into the soft copper to create a metal-to-metal seal leak-tight to below 10⁻¹¹ Pa·m³/s.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Copper appears throughout the tech tree as a utility metal: electrical conductor, heat exchanger material, gasket metal for vacuum systems, and alloying base for bronze. In gas handling, copper tubing and gaskets provide the standard for leak-tight connections. In sanitation, copper's biocidal properties make it valuable for water distribution. In casting and forming, copper and its alloys are among the earliest metals worked after native copper and copper smelting were developed.

## Technical Details

In gas handling systems, copper serves multiple roles. Copper tubing (hard-drawn or annealed) connects gas cylinders to regulators and process equipment for inert and non-flammable gases. Copper gaskets (OFHC, typically 2-3 mm thick) provide the sealing element in CF vacuum flanges — the soft copper flows into knife-edge grooves under bolt compression, creating an all-metal seal that tolerates bake-out temperatures up to 450°C. Copper-nickel alloys resist corrosion in condenser tubes exposed to brackish or seawater cooling.

For vacuum systems, copper's key properties are its low outgassing rate, ductility (which allows the gasket to deform into the sealing surface), and resistance to hydrogen permeation. Copper is specifically excluded from acetylene service, where it forms explosive copper acetylide compounds.

In sanitation, copper pipes deliver potable water with inherent antimicrobial properties — copper surfaces kill bacteria, viruses, and fungi on contact through oligodynamic action. This property made copper the preferred water distribution material before PVC became available.
