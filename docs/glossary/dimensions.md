# Dimensions

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: ceramics, knowledge, silicon

Minimum 6 m × 8 m for a small reading room (seats 12-16 readers at individual desks). Larger institutions: reading rooms of 15 m × 20 m or more, accommodating 50+ readers. Ceiling height minimum 3.5 m for natural light penetration and air volume.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Dimensions — the specified measurements of a component, room, or device — are the bridge between design intent and physical reality. In library design, room dimensions determine reader capacity and lighting quality. In pottery, vessel dimensions affect drying behavior and firing success. In silicon crystal growth, ingot dimensions are constrained by crucible size, pulling rate, and thermal gradients. Dimensional tolerances tighten as the tech tree advances from hand-formed pottery to precision-machined vacuum chambers.

## Technical Details

In library design, reading room dimensions follow functional requirements: 6 m × 8 m minimum for a small room seating 12-16 readers, with 3.5 m ceiling height for natural light and air volume. Floor loading must accommodate 5-7 kN/m² for full book shelving (200-300 kg per linear meter). Larger institutions scale reading rooms to 15 m × 20 m or more.

In pottery, vessel wall thickness affects drying rate and firing behavior. Walls that are too thick trap moisture, causing steam explosions during firing. Uniform wall thickness prevents differential shrinkage and cracking. Standard practice limits wall thickness to 6-12 mm for hand-thrown ware.

In silicon crystal growth, ingot dimensions are determined by the Czochralski pulling parameters. Standard silicon ingots are 150-300 mm diameter and 1-2 m long. The diameter is controlled by pulling rate and rotation speed — faster pulling produces smaller diameter. Crystal dimensions directly affect wafer count per ingot and therefore manufacturing cost per wafer.

Dimensional tolerances in technical drawings use notation such as 25.00 ±0.05 mm, where the tolerance band defines acceptable variation from nominal. Every manufacturing process has inherent dimensional capability: hand-forging achieves ±1-2 mm, precision machining achieves ±0.01-0.1 mm, and semiconductor lithography achieves ±0.001-0.01 mm.
