# Fire Risk

Fire risk evaluates the probability and severity of fire incidents in specific industrial operations. Foundry work involves open flames, molten metal, and combustible materials, making fire risk assessment essential. Dry sand and Class D fire extinguishers for metal fires must be immediately accessible at all melting and pouring stations. Water must never be applied to metal fires—water causes violent steam explosions when it contacts molten metal, scattering liquid metal and potentially causing severe burns over a wide area.

Electrical systems present a distinct fire risk profile: overloaded conductors heat up and ignite surrounding insulation or building materials. Proper circuit protection (fuses and breakers sized to wire ampacity), adequate ventilation for electrical panels, and regular inspection of connections for loose terminals (which arc and overheat) are the primary prevention measures.

In woodworking and textile operations, combustible dust accumulations create explosion hazards. Dust fires and explosions occur when fine particles suspended in air ignite—this is distinct from bulk material fires. Dust collection systems, regular housekeeping, and explosion venting in dust-producing equipment are essential controls. Risk assessment should be performed for each new process introduced in a bootstrap industrial setting.
