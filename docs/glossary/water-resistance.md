# Water resistance

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Moderate. Shellac is not waterproof — standing water left for hours leaves white marks (water rings on tabletops — water penetrates the film and causes micro-cracking and haze). However, shellac provides good resistance to brief moisture exposure and humidity cycling. Alcohol will redissolve it — shellac-finished surfaces are damaged by alcoholic beverages (wine, spirits), perfumes, and some cleaning products. Hot water (>60°C) softens shellac more rapidly than cold water.
