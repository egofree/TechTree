# Molybdenum disilicide (MoSi₂)

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Operates to 1700-1800°C in oxidizing atmospheres. At high temperature, silicon oxidizes to form a viscous SiO₂ glass layer that self-heals cracks. This glassy coating is what makes MoSi₂ viable despite molybdenum's tendency to oxidize catastrophically. Brittle at all temperatures, requiring careful mounting and protection from mechanical shock. Often supplied as hairpin-shaped elements that hang from the furnace roof.
