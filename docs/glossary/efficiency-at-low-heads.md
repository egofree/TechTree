# Efficiency at low heads

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Kaplan turbines achieve 85-92% efficiency at design flow for heads of 2-30 m. The flat efficiency curve across a wide flow range is their key advantage: 85%+ efficiency from roughly 30-100% of rated flow. This contrasts with Francis turbines, which may drop to 70% at half-load. For run-of-river installations with widely varying seasonal flows, the Kaplan's part-load performance is critical to annual energy production.
