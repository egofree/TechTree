# Phase 1: Foundations — Surplus & Basics

**Timeline**: Years 0–10+  
**Goal**: Food security, population/labor growth, and basic materials extraction/processing.  
**Motto**: No surplus = no industry.

## Objectives

- Establish reliable food production to support specialist labor
- Produce charcoal for metallurgy and energy
- Develop basic tool-making from stone and wood
- Begin mining and prospecting for critical raw materials
- Create containers and early refractories from clay

## Key Technologies

### Fire & Heat
- Friction fire-making, later flint-and-steel
- Controlled charcoal production via pyrolysis of wood in earth-covered pits or simple kilns
- Basic temperature control for kilns and forges

### Stone & Wood Tools
- Knapping for cutting edges (flint, obsidian)
- Grinding and polishing for axes, adzes
- Wooden handles, hafts, digging sticks
- Basic woodworking: axes, adzes, wedges

### Pottery & Kilns
- Clay identification and processing (wedging, tempering)
- Coil or slab pottery for containers
- Simple updraft kilns for firing pottery
- Early refractory clay linings for furnaces (later phases)

### Agriculture & Food
- Crop cultivation (grains, legumes, vegetables)
- Animal domestication for labor and food
- Food preservation (drying, smoking, fermentation)
- Irrigation basics

### Mining & Prospecting
- Surface collection of useful minerals
- Prospecting for: copper ores, iron ores, quartz/silica, limestone, clays, sulfur/pyrite, fluorite, tin ores
- Basic surface mining and shallow pits
- Ore identification by color, weight, streak

## Enables (Downstream)

| Output | Used By |
|--------|---------|
| Charcoal | Phase 2 (smelting), Phase 3 (foundry) |
| Food surplus | All phases (labor) |
| Kilns | Phase 2 (smelting), Phase 5 (chemistry) |
| Basic tools | Phase 2 (mining, metalwork) |
| Raw materials | Phase 2 (ores), Phase 7 (quartz) |
| Clay/pottery | Phase 2 (crucibles), Phase 5 (vessels) |

## Dependencies

None — this is the starting point.

## Practical Bottlenecks

- **Population growth**: Must be fast enough to create surplus labor for non-food-producing specialists
- **Resource location**: Must find area with access to diverse minerals (quartz, iron, copper, coal, limestone)
- **Knowledge preservation**: Writing system needed early to pass techniques to next generation (see [Side Quest 1](../side-quests/sq-01-knowledge-preservation.md))

## Safety Concerns

- Mine collapses in shallow pits
- Kiln/fire burns
- Charcoal production CO hazards
