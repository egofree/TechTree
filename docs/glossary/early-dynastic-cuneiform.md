# Early dynastic cuneiform

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

Early dynastic cuneiform (~2900-2600 BCE) — signs became abstract, losing pictographic origin. Sumerian scribes developed syllabic values — signs could represent sounds, not just things. This allowed writing of names, grammar, and abstract concepts. Sign inventory reduced to ~600 common signs. See: [Writing & Record-Keeping](../knowledge/writing.md)
