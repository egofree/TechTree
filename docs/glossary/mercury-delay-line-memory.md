# Mercury delay line memory

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: computing

(EDVAC, early stored-program machines): - **Principle**: Electrical signal converted to acoustic pulse in mercury-filled tube. Pulse travels length of tube at ~1,450 m/s (speed of sound in mercury). At far end, transducer converts back to electrical signal, amplifies, and retransmits. Data circulates as a continuous stream of pulses. - **Capacity**: ~500-1,000 bits per tube.
