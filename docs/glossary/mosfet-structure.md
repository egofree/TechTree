# MOSFET structure

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: silicon

Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistor. A gate electrode insulated from the semiconductor channel by a thin layer of SiO₂ (gate oxide). Source and drain regions are doped opposite to the substrate. Voltage on the gate creates an electric field that induces a conductive channel between source and drain — no current flows through the gate itself (unlike bipolar transistors).
