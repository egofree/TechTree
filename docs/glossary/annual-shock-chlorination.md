# Annual shock chlorination

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

Pump the well dry or draw down the water level. Add chlorine (sodium hypochlorite or calcium hypochlorite) to achieve approximately 500 mg/L free chlorine in the well water. For a 1 m diameter well with 5 m of water depth (~3,900 L), this requires roughly 2 L of 10% sodium hypochlorite solution. Agitate by surging (raising and lowering a bailer) to distribute chlorine throughout the water column and over the well walls. Let stand 12-24 hours with the well sealed.
