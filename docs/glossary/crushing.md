# Crushing

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: energy, mining, silicon

Break large lumps to uniform size before washing and sizing. Roll crusher or jaw crusher reduces run-of-mine coal to <150 mm. Avoid over-crushing — fines are harder to wash and handle.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Crushing is the first stage of comminution — the size reduction sequence that prepares raw materials for processing. Ore must be crushed before grinding and concentration. Quartz must be crushed to 10-100 mm lumps before feeding into silicon furnaces. Coal must be sized before washing. The energy cost of comminution dominates mineral processing, consuming 50-70% of total plant energy, making efficient crushing a priority over grinding.

## Technical Details

Primary crushing reduces run-of-mine material (up to 300 mm ore, quarry rock) to a manageable size. The jaw crusher is the standard primary stage: two jaw plates (one fixed, one oscillating) crush material between them with an adjustable gap of 20-100 mm. Manganese steel jaw plates (Mn12-14%) work-harden under impact to resist abrasion. Capacity ranges from 1-50 tonnes/hour depending on crusher size and material hardness.

For bootstrap operations, the stamp mill is historically proven: vertical stamps (50-500 kg each) lifted by cams on a rotating shaft and dropped by gravity onto a die plate. A 5-stamp battery with 100 kg stamps processes 5-10 tonnes/day, powered by water wheel. No precision manufacturing required — cams and tappets can be made from hardwood shod with iron.

In silicon production, MG-Si ingots are crushed in two stages: primary crushing breaks the ingot into 100-300 mm chunks with a hydraulic hammer or drop ball, then a jaw crusher reduces to 10-50 mm lumps. Silicon's hardness (Mohs 7) and abrasiveness wear crusher liners, introducing iron contamination (~0.01-0.05% Fe per pass). Magnetic separation after crushing removes steel fragments. Crushing energy is 0.5-2 kWh per tonne for primary and secondary stages combined — far less than the 10-30 kWh/tonne for ball mill grinding, which is why maximizing crushing and minimizing grinding is a fundamental process optimization.
