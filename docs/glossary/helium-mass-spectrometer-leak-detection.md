# Helium mass spectrometer leak detection

> **Type**: process | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: gas-handling

The most sensitive and widely used method for finding vacuum leaks. A mass spectrometer tuned to helium (mass 4) is connected to the vacuum system. Two modes: (1) Vacuum mode (spray probe): the system is under vacuum and connected to the leak detector. Helium is sprayed on the exterior surface using a fine nozzle. When the spray passes over a leak, helium enters the system and is detected within seconds (response time depends on pumping speed and internal volume). Sensitivity: 10⁻¹² atm·cc/s....
