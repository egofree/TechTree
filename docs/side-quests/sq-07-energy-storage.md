# SQ 7: Energy Storage &amp; Diversification

**Starts**: Phase 4+  
**Priority**: Medium — grows in importance as energy demands increase and grid stability matters.

## Problem

Semiconductor fabrication equipment cannot tolerate power interruptions — a power glitch during a crystal growth run or lithography exposure destroys expensive work-in-progress. Energy storage provides buffer capacity, and diversification prevents single-source dependency.

## Technologies &amp; Systems

### Battery Technology
- **Lead-acid batteries**: Pb + PbO₂ in H₂SO₄ electrolyte
  - Requires: lead (Phase 2), sulfuric acid (Phase 5), rubber or plastic cases
  - Good for: stationary backup, grid buffering, moderate energy density
- **Nickel-iron (Edison) batteries**: Longer life, more rugged
- **Later chemistries**: NiCd, lithium-based (requires advanced chemistry and materials)

### Mechanical Energy Storage
- **Pumped hydro**: Pump water uphill during surplus → release through turbine during demand
  - High efficiency, large scale, requires geography (elevation difference)
- **Flywheels**: Rotating mass stores kinetic energy
  - Requires: precision bearings (Phase 3), high-strength materials
  - Good for: short-duration buffering, power quality
- **Compressed air**: Compress air into caverns/tanks, release through turbine
  - Requires: strong vessels, compressors (Phase 3 + 4)

### Energy Diversification
- **Hydroelectric**: Water wheels → turbines → generators (highly reliable baseload)
- **Wind power**: Windmills → wind turbines (intermittent but valuable supplement)
- **Solar cells** (Phase 7): Early cells provide supplemental power — positive feedback loop
- **Biomass**: Wood/charcoal gasification for supplemental fuel
- **Nuclear power** (very long-term): Fission requires uranium mining, enrichment (centrifuges), reactor materials, safety systems
  - Provides massive, reliable baseload power for gigafab-scale operations
  - Decades of development even with full knowledge

### Grid Infrastructure
- **Power distribution**: Transmission lines, substations, transformers
- **Load management**: Demand response, scheduled loads, priority systems
- **UPS (uninterruptible power supply)**: Battery + inverter for critical loads
  - Essential for: crystal growers, lithography tools, cleanroom HVAC

## Integration Points

| Phase | Contribution |
|-------|-------------|
| Phase 4 | Lead-acid batteries, pumped hydro, grid basics |
| Phase 7 | Solar cells add generation capacity |
| Phase 8 | UPS systems for fab equipment |
| Phase 9+ | Nuclear baseload for industrial scale |

## Key Deliverables

- Lead-acid battery bank for critical equipment backup
- At least one diversified energy source beyond primary
- Grid infrastructure with load management
- UPS for semiconductor fabrication equipment
