Metallurgy

Extraction and working of metals from ore. Spans copper, bronze, iron, and steel production with increasingly complex smelting, casting, forging, and joining techniques. Also covers basic glass, lime, water and wind power, and spring making — the mechanical foundation for all machinery.

Capabilities

  • Basic Glass & Lime

    Glass production from silica sand + potash + limestone at 1100-1400°C, potash extraction from wood ashes, and lime production by calcining limestone at 900-1000°C. Glass for beads, vessels, and window panes; lime for mortar, whitewash, plaster, and hide processing.

  • Copper & Bronze Production

    Copper smelting from malachite, azurite, and chalcopyrite in forced-draft charcoal furnaces at 1085-1200°C. Bronze alloying with 5-12% tin for harder, more castable tools. Sand, clay, and stone mold casting techniques.

  • Iron & Steel Production

    Bloomery smelting of iron ore at 1200-1400°C producing wrought iron, carburization for steel, and crucible steel (Wootz method). Heat treatment: annealing, quenching, and tempering. This is the critical metallurgical capability that enables all subsequent domains.

  • Metalworking & Joining

    Forge welding, brazing (brass and silver), soft soldering, and spring making. Anvils, hammers, bellows, and essential smithy tools. These joining techniques enable construction of complex mechanisms, watertight joints, and electrical connections.

  • Water & Wind Power

    Undershot and overshot water wheels (1-5 HP), power transmission via flat leather belts and line shafts, and wooden peg gear trains. The primary motive power source before steam, driving grain mills, bellows, saws, and machine tools.
