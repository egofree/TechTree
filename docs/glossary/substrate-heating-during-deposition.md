# Substrate heating during deposition

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: optics

Many coating materials require elevated substrate temperature for proper film density and adhesion. MgF₂ deposits optimally at substrate temperatures of 250-300°C, which drives off adsorbed water from the glass surface and promotes dense film growth. Substrate heating is achieved by radiant heaters (quartz lamps or resistance heaters) behind the substrate holder.
