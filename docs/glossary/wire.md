# Wire

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals, transport

Iron or copper wire, 2-4 mm diameter. Iron stronger (less sag between poles) but higher resistance (poor conductor). Copper better conductor but requires more support. Galvanized iron wire (zinc-coated for corrosion resistance) is the common compromise. Resistance: ~10-50 Ω/km depending on wire gauge.
