# Configuration

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: computing, energy, transport

13+ rods standard (21 rods for advanced work). One upper bead (value 5) and four lower beads (value 1 each) per rod, separated by a horizontal reckoning bar. Frame dimensions: roughly 30 × 6 cm for a 13-rod unit, beechwood or bamboo.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Configuration refers to the physical layout and arrangement of components in a mechanical or engineering system. In computing, the abacus configuration of beads on rods encodes a positional number system. In engine design, cylinder configuration (inline, V, opposed, radial) determines power delivery, weight distribution, and manufacturing complexity. Aircraft configuration (high-wing vs. low-wing, tractor vs. pusher propeller) affects stability, visibility, and structural requirements.

## Technical Details

The abacus suanpan configuration uses a bi-quinary system: each rod represents one decimal digit with one upper bead (value 5) and four lower beads (value 1 each), separated by a reckoning bar. A 13-rod frame handles numbers up to 10¹³. The soroban, optimized from the suanpan, reduces to one upper and four lower beads — the minimal bi-quinary representation of decimal digits.

In engine design, cylinder configuration determines the firing order, balance characteristics, and crankshaft complexity. Inline configurations are simplest to manufacture but longer and taller. V-configurations package cylinders in a compact form at the cost of two cylinder banks with separate heads. Opposed (flat) configurations deliver excellent balance and low center of gravity. Radial configurations arrange cylinders around a common crankshaft for high power density in aviation applications.

Aircraft wing configuration has cascading effects: high-wing designs offer better ground clearance and downward visibility with simpler strut bracing, while low-wing designs provide shorter landing gear and better crash protection.
