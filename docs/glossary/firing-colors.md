# Firing colors

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

Pit-fired pots take on varied color from the combustion environment. Areas exposed to air turn orange or red (oxidized iron in the clay). Areas buried in ash or smothered by fuel turn gray or black (reduced iron). Polished or burnished surfaces develop a deeper sheen. These color variations are a natural feature of pit firing, not a defect.
