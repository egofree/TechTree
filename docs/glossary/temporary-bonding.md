# Temporary bonding

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Shellac's fast drying and reversibility (dissolves in ethanol or alcohol) make it useful for temporary holds in machining and assembly — parts can be bonded with shellac for a machining operation, then released by applying ethanol. Jewelers and watchmakers use this technique (called "shellacking" or "lapping") to hold small parts during grinding or polishing.
