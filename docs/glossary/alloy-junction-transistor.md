# Alloy junction transistor

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: silicon

Place indium dots on both sides of thin n-type germanium or silicon wafer (~100-200 μm). Heat to 500-600°C. Indium alloys into semiconductor, creating p-type regions. Result: pnp transistor. Simple but low performance (low frequency, high leakage). Historically important (first transistors, 1950s).
