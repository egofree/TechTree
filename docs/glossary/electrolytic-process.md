# Electrolytic process

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Electrolytic process (ISF/Dow process) — - Feed: Anhydrous MgCl₂ (critical — water contamination generates HCl gas and hydrolyzes the cell). - **Dow process**: Dehydrated MgCl₂·1.5H₂O fed directly to cell (partially hydrated — generates some HCl, managed by cell design). Electrolyte: NaCl-KCl-MgCl₂ melt at 700-750°C. Steel cathode, graphite anode. Mg²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Mg(l) (rises to surface, protected by inert gas blanket). 2Cl⁻ → Cl₂(g).
