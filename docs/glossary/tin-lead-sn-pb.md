# Tin-lead (Sn-Pb)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: machine-tools

The classic solder. 60/40 Sn/Pb melts at 183-190°C (eutectic 63/37 melts sharply at 183°C). 50/50 melts at 183-215°C (pasty range). Produce by melting tin and lead together in an iron ladle. Tin from cassiterite (SnO₂) reduction; lead from galena (PbS) roasting and smelting.
