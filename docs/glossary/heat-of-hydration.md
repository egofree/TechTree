# Heat of hydration

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Type I (ordinary Portland cement) generates 350-500 kJ/kg total heat over 7 days. This heat is beneficial in cold weather (prevents freezing) but problematic in mass concrete (dams, large foundations) where temperature differentials between the hot interior and cool exterior cause thermal cracking. Type IV (low-heat cement) generates only 250-350 kJ/kg by reducing C₃S and C₃A content and increasing C₂S.
