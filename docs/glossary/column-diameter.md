# Column diameter

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Set by vapor velocity. Too fast → flooding (vapor carries liquid upward, no separation). Too slow → poor vapor-liquid contact, weeping. Design at 50-80% of flooding velocity. Flooding velocity calculated from packing characteristics (packing factor), liquid-to-vapor ratio, and physical properties (densities, viscosities, surface tension). Typical vapor velocity: 0.5-1.5 m/s in tray columns, 1.0-2.5 m/s in packed columns at atmospheric pressure. Vacuum columns may run at 3-5 m/s.
