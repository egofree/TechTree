# Carbureted water gas

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

To increase calorific value, oil (gas oil or tar oil) is sprayed into the hot water gas in a carburetor chamber lined with checker brick at 800-1000°C. The oil cracks into light hydrocarbons (CH₄, C₂H₄, higher aromatics), raising the calorific value to 14-20 MJ/m³. This was the standard town gas production method before natural gas pipelines.
