# Wooden gear trains

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: animals

lantern gears (two discs connected by trunnions) and crown gears (flat disc with projecting teeth). Tooth profiles are involute or cycloidal, but wooden gears are typically cut by eye and fitted by trial. Material: oak for teeth, elm for hubs. Lubrication: tallow or beeswax on tooth faces. Service life: 5-10 years before tooth replacement is needed.
