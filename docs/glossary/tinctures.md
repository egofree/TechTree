# Tinctures

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: health

Tinctures (ethanol-based extracts) — Prepare by maceration or percolation with 40-70% ethanol. Standard ratios: 1:5 (1 g plant per 5 mL solvent) for dried herbs; 1:2 for fresh herbs (higher water content). Let steep 14+ days, shake daily. Decant, press residue, filter. Dose: typically 1-5 mL diluted in water. Example: iodine tincture (5% I₂, 10% KI in 70% ethanol). See: [Pharmacology](../health/pharmacology.md)
