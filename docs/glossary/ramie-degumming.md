# Ramie degumming

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Ramie (*Boehmeria nivea*) bast fiber contains 20-30% gum (pectin and hemicellulose) that must be removed before spinning. Treatment: 1-5% NaOH solution at 90-100°C for 1-4 hours. Multiple treatments may be needed for complete removal. Degummed ramie fibers are white, strong (~600 MPa tensile), and lustrous — similar to linen but stronger. Ramie yields ~1,500-2,000 kg fiber per hectare — the highest yield of any bast fiber.
