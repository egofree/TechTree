# Saw gin

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

(Whitney, 1793) — high throughput, damages long-staple fiber: - A rotating cylinder with 40-80 circular saw blades (15-20 cm diameter, 2-3 mm thick, spaced ~12 mm apart) with sharpened teeth. The cylinder spins at 300-400 rpm inside a wooden housing. A grid (iron bars with narrow slots) covers the front of the saws — slots are narrower than cotton seeds (~5 mm wide). - Cotton fed into the hopper contacts the spinning saw teeth.
