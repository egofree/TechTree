# Black liquor firing

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Concentrated black liquor (65-80% solids, heated to 110-120°C to reduce viscosity) is sprayed into the furnace through nozzles. The liquor droplets dry, pyrolyze, and burn in suspension. The organic component (lignin, hemicellulose degradation products) provides the fuel value. Black liquor heating value: 13-15 MJ/kg dry solids, roughly half that of coal.
