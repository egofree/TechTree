# Mine gases

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Beyond firedamp (methane), blackdamp (CO₂ + N₂, suffocating, collects in low spots) and afterdamp (CO, toxic, follows explosions) are lethal hazards in underground mines. Continuous ventilation and gas monitoring are essential. Canaries in cages were historically used as early-warning gas detectors — a canary succumbs to toxic gases before a human, giving miners time to evacuate.
