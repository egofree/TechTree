# Pyrometric cone equivalent (PCE)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

A standard pyrometric cone (a triangular pyramid of defined refractory composition) is heated alongside the test sample. The PCE is the cone number that softens and bends at the same temperature as the sample. PCE 15 ≈ 1430°C, PCE 20 ≈ 1520°C, PCE 26 ≈ 1600°C, PCE 31 ≈ 1680°C, PCE 38 ≈ 1790°C. PCE gives the maximum service temperature under no-load conditions (actual service temperature with mechanical load is lower).
