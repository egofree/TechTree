# Batch melting

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy, glass

(1400-1500°C): - At ~600°C, carbonates begin decomposing: CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂, Na₂CO₃ → Na₂O + CO₂. Massive CO₂ evolution causes foaming. The batch puffs up to 2-3× its original volume. - At ~800-1000°C (fritting stage), the decomposition products begin reacting with silica. The mass becomes semi-fused (frit).
