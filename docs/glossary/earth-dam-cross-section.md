# Earth dam cross-section

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

A small earth dam for a mill pond (2-10 m height) follows a standard profile. The clay core is the waterproof barrier, 1-2 m wide at the crest and expanding to 3-5 m at the base in a trapezoidal shape. The core is keyed into the natural subsoil or bedrock (a trench cut below the dam base and filled with puddled clay) to prevent under-seepage. On both sides of the core, compacted earth shoulders provide structural mass.
