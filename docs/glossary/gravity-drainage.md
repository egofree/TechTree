# Gravity drainage

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: mining

The simplest and most energy-efficient method. Drive adits (horizontal tunnels) from the nearest valley at a slight upward grade (1-3°) to intercept the mine workings. Water flows out by gravity. Effective for hillside mines where the valley floor is below the workings. Many historical mines were drained this way — the adit serves double duty as both drain and access.
