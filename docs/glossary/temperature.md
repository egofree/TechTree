# Temperature

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: ceramics, chemistry, gas-handling, health, machine-tools, metals, photolithography, silicon

~1800-2100°C in the reaction zone — the carbothermic reduction of quartz in submerged arc electric furnaces produces metallurgical-grade silicon at extreme temperatures, consuming 11-13 kWh per kilogram of product.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Temperature is one of the most fundamental control parameters across all of industrial civilization. From the ~900°C needed to calcine limestone for lime, through the 1450°C burning zone of cement kilns, to the 1800-2100°C reaction zone of silicon furnaces, the ability to achieve, measure, and control high temperatures gates progress through every domain. In [MG-Si Production](../silicon/mg-si-production.md), the reaction zone temperature determines silicon yield and purity. In [Blast Furnace](../metals/blast-furnace.md) ironmaking, temperatures of 1200-1500°C reduce iron ore to molten pig iron. In [Kilns](../ceramics/kilns.md), firing temperature determines whether clay becomes pottery, stoneware, or porcelain.

## Technical Details

Temperature measurement spans several scales and instruments: thermocouples (Type K for 0-1200°C, Type S for 0-1600°C) generate millivolt signals proportional to temperature difference; optical pyrometers measure incandescence color for temperatures above 700°C without physical contact; resistance temperature detectors (RTDs) provide high accuracy from -200°C to +850°C using platinum wire resistance changes.

Critical temperature thresholds in bootstrapping include: 100°C (water boiling, sterilization), 200-400°C (annealing of work-hardened metals), 700-900°C (iron smelting minimum), 1450°C (cement clinker formation), 1800-2100°C (silicon carbothermic reduction), and 2800°C (hydrogen-oxygen flame for fused silica working).
