# Smoke composition and preservation

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

The smoke deposits creosote (a complex mixture of phenols, guaiacols, and syringols) on the rubber surface. These phenolic compounds have antifungal and antibacterial properties — they prevent mold and bacterial growth during the extended drying period. Without smoke exposure, rubber sheets in tropical humidity would mold within 3-5 days. The smoke also imparts the characteristic amber color and smoky odor to RSS grades.
