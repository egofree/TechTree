# Granulated blast furnace slag (GBFS)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Molten slag is directed through a water jet into a granulation tank. The rapid quenching with high-pressure water shatters the slag into sand-sized particles (0-5 mm) of predominantly glassy (amorphous) structure. The quenching prevents crystallization — the resulting glassy granules have latent hydraulic properties (they react with water and calcium hydroxide to form cementitious compounds).
