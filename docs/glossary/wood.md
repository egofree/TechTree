# Wood

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: chemistry, energy, transport

Hardwood (birch, maple, or oak). Laminate 5-7 layers of wood (alternating grain direction — prevents warping, increases strength) with waterproof glue (casein glue or, later, epoxy). Rough-carve on bandsaw, finish-carve with drawknife, spokeshave, and rasp. Balance statically and dynamically.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Wood — the fibrous structural tissue of trees — is one of civilization's most versatile raw materials, serving as fuel, structural material, chemical feedstock, and manufacturing stock. In [Fuels](../energy/fuels.md), wood is the primary fuel through the early stages of bootstrapping, and wood chips provide charge porosity in silicon furnace operation. In [Aviation](../transport/aviation.md), laminated wood construction produces propellers that balance strength, light weight, and fatigue resistance — hardwood laminates with alternating grain direction resist warping while maintaining the structural integrity needed for high-RPM operation.

## Technical Details

Wood propeller manufacturing demonstrates the transition from raw material to precision engineered component. Hardwood species (birch, maple, oak) are selected for density, straight grain, and absence of knots. Laminating 5-7 thin layers with alternating grain direction creates a composite that resists warping and has greater dimensional stability than solid wood — critical for a component spinning at 1500-2500 RPM.

Static balancing (hanging the propeller on a horizontal axle; the heavy side drops; remove material until balanced) ensures the center of mass lies on the rotation axis. Dynamic balancing (spinning at operating RPM and measuring vibration) catches imbalances that static balancing misses. An unbalanced propeller at 2000 RPM generates centrifugal forces that destroy bearings and airframe mountings.

As fuel, wood provides 15-20 MJ/kg (dry basis). In metallurgical applications, wood chips (10-50 mm) mixed with quartz and coke in silicon furnaces provide charge porosity — creating channels for CO and SiO gas to escape, preventing the dense charge problems that occur with coal/coke alone. Wood also serves as a partial carbon reductant in the furnace charge.
