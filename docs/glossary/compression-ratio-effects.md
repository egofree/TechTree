# Compression ratio effects

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Higher compression = more efficient (Otto cycle efficiency = 1 - 1/r^(γ-1), where r is compression ratio and γ ≈ 1.4 for air). Practical limit: gasoline-air mixture auto-ignites (knocks) above ~12:1. Octane rating measures knock resistance — higher octane enables higher compression.
