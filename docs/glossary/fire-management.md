# Fire management

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

The fire uses green wood (hardwood preferred — produces dense, creosote-rich smoke). The fire smolders rather than burns vigorously — temperature must stay in the 60-70°C range. Temperature control is by adjusting the fire size and the ventilation openings. Too hot (>80°C) causes surface oxidation and darkening of the rubber. Too cool (<50°C) allows mold growth. The fire must be tended continuously — a 2-4 week drying cycle requires 24-hour fire attendance.
