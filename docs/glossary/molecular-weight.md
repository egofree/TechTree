# Molecular weight

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Shellac is a natural polyester with average molecular weight ~1000-2000 g/mol (oligomeric — a small polymer compared to synthetic resins at 10,000-100,000+ g/mol). The low molecular weight explains shellac's good solubility in ethanol and its low melt viscosity (easy to hot-press and mold). The material undergoes progressive polymerization over time through esterification between free hydroxyl and carboxyl groups — this is the chemical basis for shellac's aging and eventual insolubility.
