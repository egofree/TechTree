# Marine chronometer

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: measurement

Marine chronometer (the Machine Tools-Energy stage transition) — For navigation. Spring-driven (no gravity dependence). Balance wheel with helical balance spring replaces pendulum (not affected by ship motion). Temperature compensation (bimetallic balance wheel). Accuracy: ±1-2 seconds per day. Historically took decades to perfect — Harrison's H4 (1761) was the breakthrough. See: [Precision Metrology & Standards](../measurement/precision-metrology.md)
