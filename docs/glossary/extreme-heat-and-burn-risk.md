# Extreme heat and burn risk

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Bloomery furnaces reach 1200-1400°C; extracted blooms glow white-hot at ~1200°C. Forge welding requires heating iron to 1300-1400°C (bright yellow-white). At these temperatures, radiation burns occur within seconds of close proximity. Wear heavy leather apron, gloves, face shield, and closed-toe boots. Use tongs sized to the work — dropping white-hot iron causes severe burns and fires. Maintain a clear, dry floor around the forge (no water puddles — steam explosions from spilled molten slag).
