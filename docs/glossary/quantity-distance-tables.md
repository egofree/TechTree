# Quantity-distance tables

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Minimum safe distances between explosive storage and inhabited buildings, public roads, and other magazines. Based on blast overpressure scaling: safe distance ∝ (charge weight)^(1/3). For 1 tonne TNT equivalent: 180 m to inhabited buildings (for 5 kPa overpressure, the threshold for building damage). Underground magazines reduce surface distances by 50-70%. Regulatory tables (ATF 27 CFR Part 555, or equivalent national standards) specify distances by explosive type and quantity.
