# Sintering and blast furnace

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Sintering and blast furnace (traditional) — PbS concentrate is sintered on a Dwight-Lloyd sinter machine — roasted to PbO while agglomerating fines into porous lumps. Sinter + coke + fluxes (limestone, silica) charged into a blast furnace. PbO + CO → Pb + CO₂ at 1000-1200°C. Molten lead (Tm 327°C) collects in the hearth. Lead bullion: 95-98% Pb with Cu, Sn, As, Sb, Ag, Au impurities. See: [Non-Ferrous Metal Production](../metals/non-ferrous.md)
