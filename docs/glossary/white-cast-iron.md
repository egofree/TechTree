# White cast iron

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Carbon present as cementite (Fe₃C, iron carbide) rather than graphite. Very hard and wear-resistant, but extremely brittle and unmachinable. Chilled castings (rapid cooling against metal mold) produce a white iron surface. Used as intermediate for malleable iron production, or in applications requiring abrasion resistance (rock crusher jaws, mill liners).
