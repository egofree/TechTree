Foundations

Food security, population growth, fire-making, charcoal production, stone and wood tools, basic pottery, and early kilns. The starting point for all civilization bootstrapping — no surplus means no specialist labor means no industry.

Capabilities

  • Agriculture & Food Production

    Grain cultivation, animal domestication, food preservation (drying, smoking, salting, fermentation), and irrigation. The food surplus loop — more food → more specialists → better tools → more food — is the engine that drives all subsequent development.

  • Fire-Making & Charcoal Production

    Friction fire, flint-and-steel ignition, and charcoal production via earth-covered pit and simple kiln methods. Charcoal is the primary fuel for all metallurgy until coke arrives. Every kg of iron requires roughly 1-2 kg of charcoal.

  • Pottery & Kilns

    Clay identification, processing, tempering, forming (pinch, coil, slab), and firing in simple updraft kilns reaching 800-900°C. Pottery provides containers for storage, transport, cooking, and critically refractory vessels for metallurgy and chemistry. Early refractory clay linings withstand 1200-1400°C.

  • Stone & Wood Tools

    Flint knapping (hard hammer, soft hammer, pressure flaking), ground stone tools, and wooden implements with hafting using birch tar adhesive. Produces scrapers, axes, drills, and digging sticks for all early construction and material extraction.
