# Phase 6: Vacuum, Optics, Glass &amp; Pre-Semiconductor Tech

**Timeline**: Years 25–40  
**Goal**: Controlled environments and inspection capability.  
**Dependencies**: [Phase 3](phase-03-machine-tools.md) (precision machining), [Phase 4](phase-04-energy.md) (energy), [Phase 5](phase-05-chemistry.md) (glass, chemicals)

## Objectives

- Build vacuum pumps and chambers for deposition/evaporation processes
- Produce advanced glass apparatus (tubing, envelopes, lenses)
- Develop optical inspection tools (microscopes, comparators)
- Establish early metrology for semiconductor-grade measurement

## Key Technologies

### Advanced Glass Production
- Borosilicate glass (boron sources + silica) for thermal shock resistance
- Fused silica / quartz glass (melting pure quartz at ~1700°C) — critical for crucibles
- Glass tubing production (drawing from molten glass)
- Lens blanks and optical flats

### Glassblowing &amp; Apparatus
- Scientific glassblowing for: distillation apparatus, reaction vessels, tubing connections
- Vacuum-tight glass-to-metal seals (later)
- Quartz crucibles for crystal growth (Phase 7)

### Vacuum Technology
- **Mechanical pumps**: Piston pumps → rotary vane pumps
  - Precision machining of cylinders, vanes, and seals from Phase 3
  - Oil-sealed rotary vane pumps for better vacuum
- **Vacuum chambers**: Metal or glass, with gasket seals
- **Vacuum measurement**: McLeod gauges, Pirani gauges (thermal conductivity)
- **Applications**: Evaporation/deposition, tube evacuation, crystal growth atmosphere control

### Optics &amp; Inspection
- **Lens grinding and polishing**: Using machine shop equipment
  - Abrasives: emery, rouge, later cerium oxide
  - Spherical and flat surfaces
- **Microscopes**: Basic compound microscopes for inspection
  - Critical for: crystal quality inspection, lithography alignment, defect analysis
- **Optical comparators**: For measuring part dimensions via shadow projection
- **Spectroscopes** (optional): Flame spectroscopy for material identification

### Early Metrology &amp; Analytical Tools
- Precision length standards (gauge blocks)
- Thermocouples for temperature measurement
- Pressure gauges for vacuum and process monitoring
- Basic electrical measurement (galvanometers, Wheatstone bridges)

### Optional: Vacuum Tube Electronics
- Not strictly required for semiconductor path, but provides:
  - Amplification experience (useful for test equipment)
  - Radio communication capability
  - Understanding of electron physics
- Requires: glass envelopes, vacuum, cathode/anode materials, heaters

## Enables (Downstream)

| Output | Used By |

| Lens/optics capability | Phase 8 (lithography lenses, mask reduction optics) |
| Temperature measurement | Phase 7 (crystal growth control), Phase 8 (diffusion furnaces) |

## Practical Bottlenecks

- **Vacuum seal quality**: Achieving and maintaining good vacuum requires precise machining and good seal materials
- **Optical quality**: Lens precision directly impacts lithography capability later
- **Quartz crucible production**: Fused silica requires extremely high temperatures and pure quartz feedstock

## Safety Concerns

- Implosion of vacuum chambers (use shielding)
- Glass cuts and burns during glassblowing
- High voltage if vacuum tubes are attempted
