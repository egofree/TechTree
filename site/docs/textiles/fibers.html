Fiber Preparation

Node ID: textiles.fibers Domain: Textiles, Fiber & Cordage Timeline: Years 0-5 Outputs: prepared_fibers

Fiber Sources

Flax (linen):

  • Cultivation: Annual plant, ~1 m tall. Sow in spring, harvest ~100 days later when stalks yellow at bottom third. Pull entire plant (don't cut — longer fibers from root end).
  • Retting (separating fiber from stem): Submerge flax stalks in water (pond, stream, or dew-retting on grass) for 5-14 days. Bacteria decompose pectin binding fibers to woody core (shives). Test: when fibers separate easily from stem, retting is complete. Over-retting weakens fibers.
  • Breaking: Crush dried retted stalks with wooden breaker (flax brake — hinged wooden blades that snap the woody core). Shives break into short pieces, fibers remain long.
  • Scutching: Beat broken stalks with flat wooden knife against vertical board. Knock away remaining woody fragments.
  • Hackling (combing): Draw fibers through graduated iron or thorn combs (hackles). Coarse hackle removes remaining shives. Fine hackle separates long line fibers (30-90 cm) from short tow (5-15 cm). Line fibers become high-quality linen; tow becomes coarse cloth or rope.
  • Yield: ~200-400 kg fiber per hectare. Long line fiber ~30% of total; tow ~20%.

Hemp:

  • Cultivation: Annual, 2-4 m tall. Extremely hardy, minimal water. Harvest at pollen shedding (before seeds mature for best fiber). Processing identical to flax: ret (5-10 days in water), break, scutch, hackle.
  • Properties: Stronger than flax, more rot-resistant, coarser. Best for rope, canvas, and heavy cloth. Historically the primary cordage fiber before synthetic ropes.
  • Yield: ~1-3 tonnes dry stalk per hectare → ~300-800 kg fiber.

Cotton:

  • Cultivation: Requires 150-180 frost-free days + moderate water. Warm climate crop. Harvest bolls when they open (fibers emerge white and fluffy).
  • Ginning: Separate seeds from fiber. Simple single-roller gin (wooden roller rotates against fixed blade — fiber passes, seeds are crushed and fall away). Whitney-style saw gin (circular saw teeth pull fiber through grid too narrow for seeds — much faster but damages fibers).
  • Properties: Soft, comfortable, absorbs moisture. Moderate strength when dry, weaker when wet (opposite of linen and hemp). Poor mildew resistance.
  • Yield: ~500-1000 kg lint cotton per hectare.

Wool:

  • Shearing: Clip fleece from sheep annually (spring). 2-5 kg fleece per sheep. Sorted by quality — shoulder and sides are finest, breech (hindquarters) coarsest.
  • Washing: Remove lanolin (natural wool grease), dirt, vegetable matter. Wash in hot water (~50°C) with soap or alkali. Multiple washes. Lanolin recovered from wash water (useful for waterproofing and cosmetics).
  • Carding: Tease washed wool between two carding paddles (leather pads covered with bent wire teeth). Disentangles fibers, removes remaining debris, aligns roughly for spinning. Produces rolag (rolled cylinder of aligned fiber).
