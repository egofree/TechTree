# Minimum bend radius

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: machine-tools

Mild steel bends to approximately 1× sheet thickness (1t) without cracking on the outer surface. Aluminum requires 2-3t depending on alloy and temper: 5052-O soft temper bends to 1t, while 6061-T6 hardened temper needs 3t minimum. Bending below these limits causes grain boundary cracking along the outer fiber. Stainless steel, with its higher work-hardening rate, typically requires 2-2.5t even in the annealed condition.
