# Back splice

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Back splice (preventing rope end unraveling) — - Unlay rope end for ~10 rope diameters. Form a crown knot with the three strands (each strand passes over its neighbor in sequence). Then tuck each strand back down through the rope body, following the lay. - Result: a thickened, sealed end that cannot unravel. Adds bulk — the spliced end will not pass through pulleys or fairleads. See: [Rope and Cordage](../textiles/rope-making.md)
