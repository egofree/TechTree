# Fiber preparation

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Before ropemaking, fibers must be hackled or combed to align them parallel and remove short pieces and debris. Spin the prepared fibers into yarn by twisting — use a spinning wheel, drop spindle, or mechanical spinner. Conventional yarn twist for Western ropemaking is Z-twist (clockwise when viewed from above). Multiple yarns are twisted together to form a **strand** — the intermediate building block between yarn and rope.
