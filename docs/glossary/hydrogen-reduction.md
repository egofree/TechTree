# Hydrogen reduction

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Hydrogen reduction (tungsten, molybdenum, cobalt, nickel) — Metal oxides (WO₃, MoO₃, Co₃O₄, NiO) are reduced by flowing hydrogen gas in a pusher furnace or rotary kiln. The reduction proceeds topotactically — the metal crystal inherits the morphology of the oxide precursor, producing angular or sponge-like particles. - **Tungsten**: WO₃ + 3H₂ → W + 3H₂O at 700-1000°C in a multi-zone furnace (4-6 zones, progressively higher temperature).
