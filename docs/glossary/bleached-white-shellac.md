# Bleached (white) shellac

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Chemically bleached with sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl) to remove all color. The chlorine oxidizes the natural pigments (primarily erythrolaccin and shellolic acid chromophores). Bleached shellac is nearly colorless in solution. However, bleaching weakens the resin — bleached shellac has shorter shelf life (6-12 months vs. 2-3 years for orange) and produces slightly softer films. Used for white or pastel-colored finishes.
