# Headbox and forming

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The pulp slurry (0.5-1.5% consistency, meaning 0.5-1.5% fiber by mass in water) is pumped through the headbox onto a moving wire mesh (the "wire" — typically a synthetic fabric of polyester or nylon monofilaments woven to precise open area). Modern paper machines use a twin-wire former (pulp slurry injected between two converging wires) for better formation and higher speed. Water drains through the wire by gravity, suction, and table rolls. Fiber mat forms on the wire surface as water exits....
