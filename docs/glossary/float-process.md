# Float process

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: glass

Float process (modern flat glass, requires advanced metallurgy) — - Molten glass (1050°C) poured onto a bath of molten tin (232°C melting point, stable in air) in a controlled atmosphere (nitrogen + hydrogen to prevent tin oxidation). Glass floats and spreads to a uniform thickness determined by surface tension and gravity. Default thickness: ~6 mm.
