# Tar cracking (thermal and catalytic)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

At 1000-1100°C, tar molecules decompose to smaller, non-condensable gases (CO, H₂, CH₄). Thermal cracking requires high temperature and 1-3 seconds residence time. Catalytic cracking uses dolomite (CaMg(CO₃)₂), olivine (Mg₂SiO₄), or nickel-based catalysts at 800-900°C to achieve >95% tar destruction. Dolomite is cheap but erodes in fluidized beds.
