# Replacement

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Polyethylene replaced gutta-percha for submarine cables in the 1940s. Polyethylene offers lower dielectric loss (dissipation factor ~0.0002 vs. ~0.003 for gutta-percha — enabling telephone-frequency signals rather than just telegraph, which requires much higher bandwidth), better aging (polyethylene does not oxidize in air as readily), lower cost (petrochemical feedstock vs. imported tropical tree resin), and can be extruded at higher speeds.
