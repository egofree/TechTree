# Hook making

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: animals

Bone hooks (cut from large mammal rib or scapula, sharpened to a point, bent in boiling water or over a heated stone) work for small to medium fish. Iron wire hooks (forged from 2-4 mm wire) are stronger and hold their point longer. Sizes range from 2 cm (small panfish) to 10 cm (large catfish, river fish). Barbless hooks are simpler to make but lose more fish during the fight.
