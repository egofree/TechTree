# Viscose process parameters

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Dissolving pulp (92-96% α-cellulose) is steeped in 17.5-18% NaOH at 20-25°C for 30-60 minutes to form alkali cellulose. Excess NaOH is pressed out (press factor 2.8-3.2× original weight). The alkali cellulose is shredded to crumbs and aged (depolymerized) at 20-30°C for 12-48 hours in humidified air.
