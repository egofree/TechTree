# Sizing

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: energy, knowledge

The determination of appropriate dimensions and capacity for equipment, infrastructure, or printed materials. Correct sizing ensures systems operate within safe and efficient parameters.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Sizing appears in [Coal](../energy/fuels/coal.md) processing, [Pumped Hydro Storage](../energy/storage/pumped-hydro.md) design, and [Printing](../knowledge/printing.md). In hydraulic systems, surge tank cross-sectional area must be large enough to prevent overflow during transients while keeping the minimum level above the penstock connection. In printing, type size and page dimensions determine readability and material usage.

## Technical Details

Surge tank sizing: the cross-sectional area must prevent the maximum surge level from exceeding the tank top and keep the minimum surge level above the penstock connection. A rule of thumb: the surge tank area should be at least 2× the penstock cross-section for stable operation. More precise sizing uses the water hammer equation and considers the length and velocity of the penstock flow.

Coal sizing: raw coal is screened into size fractions for different markets. Lump coal (>50 mm) for domestic heating, sized coal (25-50 mm) for industrial use, small coal (6-25 mm) for boilers, and fines (<6 mm) for briquetting or power generation. Each size fraction has different value and handling requirements.

Printing sizing: paper dimensions (quarto, folio, octavo) determine the number of pages per sheet and thus the economics of book production. Type size (measured in points, 1 point = 0.353 mm) affects readability and page count. Standard body text is 10-12 point.
