# Double absorption (double contact)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

After 3 catalyst beds, SO₃ is absorbed in 98% H₂SO₄ (intermediate absorption). The remaining gas (containing unconverted SO₂) is reheated and passed through a 4th catalyst bed, achieving 99.5-99.9% overall SO₂ conversion. Environmental regulations in most countries require >99% conversion, making double absorption standard for new plants. Single absorption achieves only 97-98% conversion, emitting 20-60 kg SO₂ per tonne H₂SO₄ produced.
