# Cotton bleaching (hydrogen peroxide)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: textiles

Treatment of cotton fabric with 1-3% hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) solution at 80-95°C under alkaline conditions to remove natural waxes, pectins, and pigments, producing white fabric ready for dyeing or finishing. H₂O₂ is preferred over chlorine bleaches for its environmental profile.
