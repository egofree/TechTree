# Undershot wheel (20-30% efficient)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The simplest construction but least efficient. Flat paddles dip into the current and the wheel is driven entirely by the impulse of flowing water. Most energy is lost to turbulence as water deflects around the paddles without doing work. Improved versions use a close-fitting stone or timber channel (mill race) that concentrates flow onto the paddles, raising efficiency to perhaps 30%. Best for flat terrain with no available head.
