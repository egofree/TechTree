# Point-contact transistor

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: computing

Point-contact transistor (Bardeen and Brattain, December 1947) — Structure**: Two fine gold contacts (~50 μm spacing) pressed against a germanium crystal surface. The germanium slab sits on a metal base. One contact is the emitter, one is the collector, the base is the crystal itself. - **Operation**: Small current at the emitter contact modulates a larger current at the collector contact. Current gain ~2-20. Fragile and difficult to reproduce consistently. Noisy and unstable over temperature. - **Significance**: ... See: [Electronic Computing](../computing/electronic.md)
