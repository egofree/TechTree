# Acid hazards

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

Copperplate etching uses nitric acid (HNO₃) or ferric chloride (FeCl₃) solutions. Nitric acid produces toxic nitrogen dioxide gas (brown fumes) during etching — work only in a fume hood or well-ventilated outdoor workspace. Ferric chloride is less hazardous (no toxic gas) but stains everything brown and corrodes metal tools. Wear rubber gloves and eye protection. Neutralize acid spills with sodium bicarbonate (baking soda).
