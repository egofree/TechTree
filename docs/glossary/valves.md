# Valves

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: chemistry, energy, gas-handling, transport

Intake valve — stainless steel or nickel alloy (lower temperature, ~300-400°C). Exhaust valve — high-nickel alloy (Inconel-type, withstands 700-900°C exhaust gas). Stem diameter 7-9 mm. Head diameter: intake 30-40 mm, exhaust 25-35 mm. Face angle 45°. Seat width 1.5-2.5 mm. Machine seat and face to gas-tight fit.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Valves are mechanical devices that control the flow of fluids (gases and liquids) through pipes, channels, and combustion chambers. In [Internal Combustion Engines](../energy/engine.md), intake and exhaust valves regulate gas exchange in cylinders with precise timing and gas-tight sealing. In [Steam Power](../energy/steam-power.md), valves control steam admission and exhaust to cylinders or turbines. In [Gas Handling](../gas-handling/basic.md), valves isolate, direct, and regulate gas flows in chemical and semiconductor processing. In [Dopant & Etch Gases](../chemistry/dopant-etch-gases.md) and [Wood Gasification](../chemistry/petroleum-alternatives/wood-gasification.md), valves must handle corrosive and toxic gases safely.

## Technical Details

Engine valves illustrate the extreme demands placed on valve components. The exhaust valve face contacts combustion gases at 700-900°C continuously, requiring high-nickel alloys (Inconel-type) that maintain strength and resist oxidation at elevated temperature. The intake valve operates in a cooler environment (300-400°C) and can use less exotic materials (stainless steel, chromium-silicon alloy steel).

Valve sealing depends on precise machining: the 45° face angle must mate with the 45° seat with a contact width of 1.5-2.5 mm, ground with lapping compound to a gas-tight fit. Concentricity between the valve guide bore and seat must be within 0.05 mm — any eccentricity causes leakage.

For gas handling in semiconductor manufacturing, valves must provide leak-tight sealing against toxic gases (PH₃, AsH₃, SiH₄) with external leak rates below 10⁻⁹ atm·cc/s. Diaphragm valves (PTFE or metal diaphragm isolating process gas from the actuator mechanism) are standard for corrosive gas service. Pneumatic-actuated valves provide remote operation for safety in hazardous gas cabinets.
