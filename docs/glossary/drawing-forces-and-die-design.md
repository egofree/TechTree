# Drawing forces and die design

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Draw force F = σᵧ × A × ln(A₀/A₁) where σᵧ is yield stress, A₀ and A₁ are initial and final cross-sectional areas. Approach angle of the die cone: 12-18° (steeper angles increase redundant work, shallower angles increase friction). Bearing length: 0.3-0.8× wire diameter. Die wear rate: 1-5 μm bore enlargement per tonne of wire drawn through tungsten carbide dies; diamond dies last 10-50× longer for fine wire.
