# Type IV (delayed hypersensitivity)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

T-cell mediated reaction to rubber chemical additives (accelerators like thiurams and carbamates). Symptoms appear 24-48 hours after exposure: eczematous dermatitis at contact sites. Not life-threatening but chronically debilitating. Prevalence: 2-8% in rubber industry workers. Prevention: use accelerator-free glove formulations (chloroprene or polyurethane gloves for sensitized workers).
