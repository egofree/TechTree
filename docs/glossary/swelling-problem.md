# Swelling problem

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: photolithography

During development, the developer (xylene) penetrates and swells the un-crosslinked resist. When the resist is rinsed (IPA or heptane), it shrinks back, but the pattern dimensions have shifted. This swelling limits resolution to ~2-3 μm and causes pattern distortion in dense features. The shift from negative to positive resists in the 1970s was driven primarily by this swelling limitation.
