# Why cast iron matters

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

It bridges the gap between wrought iron and steel. Easier to cast into complex shapes than either (lower melting point, excellent fluidity). Good enough for many structural and machine applications. The dominant structural metal from ~1700 to ~1850 — cast iron bridges (Iron Bridge, Shropshire, 1779), building columns, steam engine cylinders, machine frames, pipes.
