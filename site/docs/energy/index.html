Energy

Water and wind power, solid fuel production, steam engines, electromagnetic generation, electric furnaces, cooling and refrigeration, and energy storage. The energy revolution transforms manufacturing from muscle-powered to machine-powered industry.

Capabilities

  • Cooling Systems & Refrigeration

    Refrigeration cycles, ice production, cooling systems for industrial processes and climate control.

  • Electric Furnaces

    Electric arc furnace (EAF) for steelmaking and silicon reduction, submerged arc furnace for continuous production, and resistance heating elements (Nichrome, Kanthal, SiC). Arc temperatures reach 3000-3500°C. EAF is critical for MG-Si production and specialty steel.

  • Electricity Generation & Distribution

    Voltaic piles and Daniell cells, wire drawing, generators (Faraday magneto → self-exciting dynamo), DC and AC motors, transformers for voltage step-up/step-down, and power distribution (AC wins long-term). Copper wire gauge reference and motor/generator sizing included.

  • Energy Storage & Diversification

    Lead-acid batteries, nickel-iron batteries, mechanical storage (pumped hydro, flywheels, compressed air), energy diversification (hydroelectric, wind, solar PV, biomass), grid infrastructure, and UPS systems for semiconductor fab equipment. Semiconductor fabrication cannot tolerate power interruptions.

  • Fuel Production

    Solid, liquid, and gaseous fuel production from coal, coke, charcoal, and petroleum sources.

  • Steam Power

    Newcomen atmospheric engine (5-15 HP, 0.5-1% efficiency), Watt separate condenser engine (10-100+ HP, 3-5% efficiency), boiler construction from riveted iron plate, safety valves, and power transmission from reciprocating to rotary motion.

  • Water Power

    Undershot and overshot water wheels (1-5 HP) using gravitational water flow for mechanical power.

  • Wind Power

    Windmills for mechanical power: grain grinding, sawing, and water pumping.
