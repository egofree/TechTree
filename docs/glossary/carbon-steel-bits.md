# Carbon steel bits

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: mining

(standard for hand drilling and early machine drilling): - Medium-carbon steel (0.6-0.8% carbon), forged to shape and hardened by quenching in water or oil from 800-820°C. Tempered at 300-400°C to reduce brittleness while maintaining edge hardness (Rockwell C 50-58). - Sharp chisel edge dulls quickly in hard rock. Needs re-forging every 0.5-1 m of drilling in granite.
