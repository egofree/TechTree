# CO₂ removal

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Caustic soda (NaOH) scrubber or molecular sieve adsorbent beds. CO₂ freezes at -78°C (dry ice) and would accumulate on heat exchanger surfaces, blocking flow. A typical plant uses two molecular sieve beds in alternating cycles — one adsorbing while the other regenerates with heated purge gas.
