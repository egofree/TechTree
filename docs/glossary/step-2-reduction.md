# Step 2 — Reduction

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

TiCl₄(l) + 2Mg(l) → Ti(s) + 2MgCl₂(l) at 800-900°C under argon atmosphere in a sealed steel retort. The reaction is exothermic (ΔH = -260 kJ/mol TiCl₄) — initiated by heating, then self-sustaining. Titanium deposits as a porous sponge ("titanium sponge") — not a molten metal because titanium's melting point (1668°C) far exceeds the reaction temperature.
