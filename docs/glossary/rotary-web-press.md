# Rotary web press

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

(Bullock, 1863): 12,000 sheets/hour continuous feed from roll paper. The Bullock press used a curved stereotype plate on a cylinder, paper feeding from a continuous roll and automatically cutting into sheets after printing. This eliminated the hand-feeding bottleneck entirely. Later rotary presses (Hoe double-octuple, 1880s) reached 24,000-48,000 sheets/hour for newspaper production.
