# Wind power density

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

(power available per unit area swept by the rotor): - P = 0.5 × ρ × A × v³ × Cp - Where: ρ = air density (~1.225 kg/m³ at sea level, 15°C), A = swept area (m²), v = wind speed (m/s), Cp = power coefficient (theoretical max 0.593 — the Betz limit) - Example: 6 m diameter rotor at 7 m/s: A = 28.3 m², P_theoretical = 0.5 × 1.225 × 28.3 × 343 = ~5,950 W. At Cp = 0.25 (typical traditional mill): ~1,490 W (~2 HP)
