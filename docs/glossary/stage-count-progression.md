# Stage count progression

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

A large condensing turbine may have 20-40 stages. The HP end has many short stages with small blade heights (the steam is dense and the volume flow is low). Each successive stage has longer blades and larger flow area as the steam expands. The LP end may have only 2-4 stages but with enormous blade heights (600-1200 mm for the last row). The transition from HP to IP to LP sections is a continuous expansion process, not discrete jumps.
