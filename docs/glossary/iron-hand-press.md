# Iron hand press

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

Iron hand press (Earl Stanhope, ~1800) — Cast iron frame replaces wood — far more rigid, allowing greater impression force with less effort. The Stanhope press used a combination lever (compound leverage mechanism) instead of a simple screw, multiplying the pressman's force by ~6:1. Result: sharper, more even impressions; less physical effort per pull. Approximately 300 sheets/hour. The iron frame does not warp or fatigue like wood — a Stanhope press is functional for decades with minimal maintenance. See: [Paper and Printing Press](../knowledge/printing.md)
