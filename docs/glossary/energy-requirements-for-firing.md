# Energy requirements for firing

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: ceramics

The specific heat of dry clay is approximately 1.0 kJ/(kg·K). To fire 100 kg of ware from 20°C ambient to 1200°C peak temperature, the theoretical minimum energy for heating alone is: 100 kg × (1200 - 20) K × 1.0 kJ/(kg·K) = 118 MJ. Add 20-50 MJ for chemically combined water evolution and mineral transformations (endothermic dehydroxylation of kaolinite at 450-600°C absorbs ~500 kJ/kg).
