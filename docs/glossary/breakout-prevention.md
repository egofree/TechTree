# Breakout prevention

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

A breakout — liquid steel escaping the thin shell — is the most dangerous continuous casting failure. Causes include unstable mold level (±3 mm tolerance), insufficient mold powder, and excessive casting speed. Detection systems measure mold heat transfer (thermocouples embedded in copper mold at 25-50 mm spacing). A sudden temperature spike at any thermocouple indicates a thinning shell and triggers automatic speed reduction.
