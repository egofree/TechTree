# Operating regimes

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Mesophilic (35-38°C) is most common, stable, and tolerant of feedstock variations. Thermophilic (50-55°C) is faster (HRT 10-15 days vs 15-30 days mesophilic), gives 20-30% higher gas yield, but is more sensitive to temperature fluctuations and ammonia inhibition. Feedstock C:N ratio 20-30:1 is optimal. Below 15:1, excess nitrogen produces ammonia that inhibits methanogens at >1500 mg/L NH₃-N. Above 35:1, nitrogen limitation slows bacterial growth.
