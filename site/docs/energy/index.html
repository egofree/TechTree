Energy

Abundant, controllable power from coal and coke, steam engines, electromagnetic generation, electric furnaces, internal combustion engines, and electrolysis. The energy revolution transforms manufacturing from muscle-powered to machine-powered industry.

Capabilities

  • Advanced Welding

    Oxy-acetylene welding and cutting (3100°C flame), arc welding (SMAW/stick) with flux-coated electrodes, and electrode manufacturing. Oxy-acetylene is the primary metal joining method from ~1900 until arc welding dominates ~1940.

  • Coal & Coke Production

    Beehive and by-product recovery coke ovens converting bituminous coal to coke (60-70% yield). Coke burns at 1800-2100°C with forced air — essential for blast furnaces and silicon production. Byproducts include coal gas, coal tar, ammonia, and benzene.

  • Electric Furnaces

    Electric arc furnace (EAF) for steelmaking and silicon reduction, submerged arc furnace for continuous production, and resistance heating elements (Nichrome, Kanthal, SiC). Arc temperatures reach 3000-3500°C. EAF is critical for MG-Si production and specialty steel.

  • Electricity Generation & Distribution

    Voltaic piles and Daniell cells, wire drawing, generators (Faraday magneto → self-exciting dynamo), DC and AC motors, transformers for voltage step-up/step-down, and power distribution (AC wins long-term). Copper wire gauge reference and motor/generator sizing included.

  • Electrolysis

    Electrolytic cells for chlor-alkali (NaCl → Cl₂ + NaOH), water splitting (H₂ + O₂), aluminum Hall-Héroult process, and copper refining. DC power at 2-5V per cell, industrial current 100-10,000+ A. Enables production of ultra-pure hydrogen, chlorine, and aluminum.

  • Internal Combustion Engines

    Otto-cycle piston engines (4-stroke, 5-65 HP), carburetors, magneto ignition, cylinder boring, crankshaft forging, valve trains, and cooling systems. Provides mobile power for transport and aircraft. Requires machine-tool precision boring and grinding.

  • Steam Power

    Newcomen atmospheric engine (5-15 HP, 0.5-1% efficiency), Watt separate condenser engine (10-100+ HP, 3-5% efficiency), boiler construction from riveted iron plate, safety valves, and power transmission from reciprocating to rotary motion.
