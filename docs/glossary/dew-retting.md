# Dew retting

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: plants, textiles

Dew retting (lowest technology) — spread harvested stalks in a thin layer (~10 cm deep) on a grass field. Morning dew and rain keep the stalks moist; soil bacteria and fungi decompose the pectin. Turn stalks every 2-3 days for even retting. Duration: 2-6 weeks depending on climate (requires regular dew or rain; fails in dry weather). Advantages: no water supply needed, low labor. Disadvantages: slow, weather-dependent, uneven results. The most common method in Northern Europe historically.
