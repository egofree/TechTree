# Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT) switching

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: computing

Use of BJTs as on/off switches in digital circuits by driving between cutoff (no base current, collector open) and saturation (base fully driven, collector-emitter ~0.2V). Switching speed limited by charge storage in the base region; faster than relays but slower than MOSFETs.
