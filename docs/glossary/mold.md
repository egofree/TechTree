# Mold

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: knowledge, metals, polymers

A shaped cavity or form into which fluid material (molten metal, liquid polymer, ceramic slip, or papier-mâché) is poured, injected, or pressed to produce a solid object of defined geometry. In continuous casting, a water-cooled copper alloy mold (700-900 mm long) shapes molten steel into slabs or billets, with mold powder lubricating the strand-mold interface and absorbing inclusions. In injection molding, steel molds with polished cavities shape molten thermoplastics at 6-10 MPa into finished parts.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Molds are central to casting (metals, ceramics), molding (polymers), and forming (composites, concrete). In metals, sand molds (single-use) and metal molds (reusable die casting) shape everything from iron cookware to aluminum engine blocks. In steelmaking, the continuous casting mold is the precision component that determines slab geometry and surface quality. In the polymers domain, injection molds for thermoplastics and compression molds for thermosets are the highest-precision tooling, often costing more than the molding machine itself.

The knowledge domain uses molds for library shelf construction (molded concrete brackets), paper-making (mold and deckle for hand papermaking), and printing (type metal cast in molds for letterpress). The progression from hand-packed sand molds to machined steel molds tracks the development of precision manufacturing capability.

## Technical Details

Injection molds for thermoplastics are precision-engineered steel tools. Core and cavity halves are machined from hardened tool steel (P20, H13, or S136) to tolerances of ±0.01-0.05 mm. Surface finish is polished to RA <0.4 μm for optical-quality parts. Cooling channels follow the cavity contour to achieve uniform solidification. Ejector pins push the molded part from the cavity after opening. Runner and gate design controls the flow path of molten polymer into the cavity, affecting weld lines, air traps, and part appearance.

For continuous casting in steelmaking, mold design is critical: water velocity in cooling channels must be 6-12 m/s to prevent nucleate boiling and ensure uniform heat extraction. Heat flux reaches 1-3 MW/m² at the meniscus. Mold taper (slight reduction in cross-section from top to bottom) compensates for steel shrinkage during solidification. Mold oscillation prevents the solidifying shell from sticking to the copper surface.
