# Testing

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: glass, metals, mining, optics, silicon

Measure the focal length by auto-collimation or by projecting a distant point source to a sharp focus. Test surface quality with a knife-edge test (Foucault test) or interferometry — an acceptable lens shows no zones under the knife-edge test, and surface deviation from the ideal curve is less than λ/4 across the full aperture.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Testing encompasses the broader practice of verification and quality assurance across all manufacturing domains. In [Advanced Glass](../glass/advanced.md) and [Optical Inspection](../optics/inspection.md), testing validates that lenses, mirrors, and optical surfaces meet the precision required for their application. In [Iron & Steel](../metals/iron-steel.md), mechanical testing (tensile, hardness, impact) verifies material properties. In [Black Powder](../mining/extraction/black-powder.md) manufacture, testing ensures consistent explosive performance. In [Basic Devices](../silicon/basic-devices.md), electrical testing confirms semiconductor functionality.

## Technical Details

The Foucault knife-edge test is a remarkably sensitive method for evaluating mirror and lens surfaces. A point light source is placed at the center of curvature, and a knife edge is introduced at the return focus. Any deviation of the surface from the ideal sphere creates visible shadows — zones (rings of different curvature) appear as bright and dark bands. A master optician can detect surface errors of λ/20 or better by eye with this test.

Interferometry provides quantitative measurement by comparing the test surface against a reference. Fringes (Newton's rings, Fizeau fringes, or Twyman-Green interferograms) map surface deviation: each fringe represents λ/2 (approximately 0.25 μm for visible light) of height difference.

For semiconductor devices, testing progresses from wafer-level parametric tests through packaged-part functional tests to system-level verification, each stage catching different defect types.
