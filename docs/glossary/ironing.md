# Ironing

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: machine-tools

After initial drawing, the cup wall thickness can be reduced and uniformized by forcing the cup through a die slightly smaller than the cup outer diameter, with a tightly-fitted punch inside. Each ironing pass reduces wall thickness 15-30%. Beverage cans undergo 2-3 ironing passes to thin the walls to 0.1 mm from an initial draw thickness of 0.3 mm. Ironing requires good lubrication (chlorinated oil or phosphate coating) to prevent galling between the die and the cup wall.
