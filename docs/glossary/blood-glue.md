# Blood glue

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Animal blood (albumin) mixed with lime and water. Heat-cured at 70-80°C in a press. Used historically for plywood — blood-glued plywood was waterproof enough for boat building. The albumin proteins coagulate irreversibly on heating, creating a water-resistant bond. Production declined after synthetic adhesives (phenol-formaldehyde, urea-formaldehyde) became available.
