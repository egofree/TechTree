# Manganese (MnO₂)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: glass

Purple at 1-3%. Also used as a decolorizer at low concentrations: MnO₂ oxidizes Fe²⁺ (blue-green) to Fe³⁺ (pale yellow), and the resulting purple-yellow combination produces a neutral gray that appears colorless in thin sections. Roman glassmakers called manganese the "glassmaker's soap." - **Lead (PbO)**: Not primarily a colorant, but lead glass has a higher refractive index (1.6-1.7) that makes it appear more brilliant and "brighter" than soda-lime glass.
