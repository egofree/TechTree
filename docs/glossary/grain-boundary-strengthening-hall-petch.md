# Grain boundary strengthening (Hall-Petch)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

σ_y = σ₀ + k·d^(-1/2), where d is grain diameter. Finer grains → higher yield strength AND better toughness — the only mechanism that improves both simultaneously. Achieved by controlled rolling, recrystallization, and grain-refining additions (Nb, Ti, Al form pinning particles that prevent grain growth). This relationship breaks down below ~10-20 nm (nanograins), where grain boundary sliding dominates.
