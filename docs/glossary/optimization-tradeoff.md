# Optimization tradeoff

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Increasing the number of theoretical plates (taller column, more capital) allows operation at lower reflux ratio (less energy, lower operating cost). The economic optimum occurs where the annualized capital charge for the additional plates equals the annual energy savings from reduced reflux. This tradeoff is evaluated by plotting total annualized cost (capital + energy) versus number of plates, finding the minimum.
