# Oxygen content

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: silicon

Oxygen content: 5-20 ppma, introduced by crucible dissolution. Oxygen is higher at the seed end (crucible wall is thickest) and decreases toward the tail. Oxygen forms thermal donors (electrically active complexes) if the crystal is not annealed. Standard practice: heat-treat wafers at 600-800°C to precipitate oxygen into inert SiO₂ particles.
