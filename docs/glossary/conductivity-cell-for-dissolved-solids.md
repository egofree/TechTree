# Conductivity cell for dissolved solids

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

Two electrodes immersed in water measure electrical conductivity, which correlates with total dissolved solids (TDS). A handheld conductivity meter reads in microsiemens per cm (μS/cm). Convert to approximate TDS in mg/L by multiplying by 0.5-0.7 (the conversion factor depends on the mix of dissolved ions). Drinking water TDS should be below 1,000 mg/L.
