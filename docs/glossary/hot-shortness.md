# Hot shortness

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Steel containing >0.03% sulfur becomes "hot short" — cracks during hot working because FeS forms a low-melting eutectic (988°C) that coats grain boundaries. Manganese is added (Mn:S ratio ≥ 4:1) to form MnS inclusions (melting point 1610°C) instead. This is why all structural steel specifications require minimum manganese content.
