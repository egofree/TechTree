# Pressure drop calculation

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: gas-handling

Gas flowing through a pipe loses pressure due to friction against the pipe wall. The Darcy-Weisbach equation gives the pressure drop: ΔP = f × (L/D) × (ρv²/2), where f is the friction factor (from Moody chart, depends on pipe roughness and Reynolds number), L is pipe length, D is pipe inside diameter, ρ is gas density, and v is gas velocity.
