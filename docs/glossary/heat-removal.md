# Heat removal

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Fermentation generates heat from microbial metabolism (~3-5 kW/m³ for fast-growing cultures). Temperature must be controlled within ±1°C. Heat removal via: cooling jackets (surface area limited, adequate for <10 m³ vessels), internal coils (provide additional surface area, but interfere with mixing and cleaning), or external heat exchangers (pump broth through shell-and-tube exchanger and return to fermenter).
