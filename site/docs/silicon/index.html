Silicon

Production of metallurgical-grade silicon, purification to semiconductor grade, Czochralski crystal growth, wafer slicing and polishing, and fabrication of basic solar cells and semiconductor devices. Solar cells are the primary early target — far simpler than logic chips and creating a critical positive feedback loop.

Capabilities

  • Basic Semiconductor Devices

    Solar cell fabrication (p-type wafer → phosphorus diffusion → SiNₓ AR coating → screen-printed metallization, targeting 15-18% efficiency), point-contact diodes, alloy junction transistors, and diffused transistors. Vacuum evaporation and screen printing for metallization.

  • Crystal Growth & Wafering

    Czochralski (CZ) crystal pulling from quartz crucibles with precision motion control, wire saw wafering, lapping (2-5 μm TTV), and CMP polishing (<0.5 nm Ra). RCA clean standard for wafer preparation. CZ puller is the most mechanically demanding equipment in the silicon path.

  • Metallurgical-Grade Silicon Production

    Carbothermic reduction of quartz (SiO₂ + 2C → Si + 2CO) in submerged arc electric furnace at 1800-2100°C. Requires 5-30 MW power, produces 97-99% purity MG-Si. Energy consumption: ~11-13 kWh/kg. The starting material for all semiconductor-grade silicon.

  • Silicon Purification

    Chemical purification via Siemens-like process (hydrochlorination → fractional distillation of chlorosilanes → CVD deposition, 9N-11N purity) and physical purification via directional solidification and zone refining (5-9N). Siemens process achieves electronic grade but consumes ~100-200 kWh/kg.
