# Manufacture

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: chemistry, knowledge, mining, transport

The process of producing goods from raw materials through controlled chemical and mechanical operations. In the tech tree, manufacture encompasses the specific production steps for materials like nitrocellulose (soaking cellulose in mixed acid at 20-30°C for 30-60 minutes), explosives (pulverizing ingredients separately, mixing, corning, drying, and sieving), and cement (raw grinding, preheating, kiln burning at 1450°C, finish grinding with gypsum).

## Context in the Tech Tree

Manufacture appears as a cross-cutting activity across every domain that produces finished goods from raw inputs. The chemistry domain manufactures acids, alkalis, solvents, and gases through sequential chemical reactions. The metals domain manufactures alloys, forgings, and castings through thermal and mechanical processing. The knowledge domain manufactures books through printing. The transport domain manufactures aircraft components.

The distinction between "manufacture" (the specific production process) and "manufacturing" (the broader system of production) is reflected in the tech tree. Manufacture focuses on process chemistry and technique — the specific steps, temperatures, times, and reagents that transform raw materials into products.

## Technical Details

Manufacturing processes in the bootstrap sequence share common principles regardless of the specific product. Feedstock quality control (purity, particle size, moisture content) determines product quality. Temperature control within specified ranges prevents side reactions and ensures complete conversion. Time management (reaction duration, cooling rates, curing periods) affects yield and product properties. Waste management (off-gas treatment, byproduct recovery, effluent treatment) prevents environmental contamination and sometimes recovers valuable co-products.

Black powder manufacture illustrates the general pattern: grind ingredients separately to specific particle size, mix thoroughly in controlled ratios, moisten and press into cakes for density control, dry under controlled conditions, crush and sieve to graded particle sizes for specific burn rates. Each step has defined parameters — deviation produces inferior or dangerous product.
