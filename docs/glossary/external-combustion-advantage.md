# External combustion advantage

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Unlike internal combustion engines, the Stirling engine's working gas never contacts combustion products. The heat source warms the hot heat exchanger from outside, and the working gas (sealed permanently inside the engine) transfers that thermal energy into mechanical work. This means any heat source at sufficient temperature works: concentrated solar, biomass combustion, waste heat from industrial processes, geothermal, even radioisotope decay (as in space probes).
