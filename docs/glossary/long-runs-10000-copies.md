# Long runs (>10,000 copies)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

Paper dominates. At 80 gsm, a 200-page octavo book consumes ~0.5 kg paper per copy. For 10,000 copies, paper cost (at roughly 1/3 the cost of the finished book) exceeds typesetting cost. Press time becomes significant — 10,000 copies of a 200-page book requires ~80 press-hours on a hand press, or ~5 hours on a rotary press. Binding labor adds another substantial cost layer at any run length.
