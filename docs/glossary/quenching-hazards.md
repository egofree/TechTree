# Quenching hazards

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Plunging hot steel (780-850°C) into water produces violent boiling and steam splash. Oil quenching (used for springs and high-carbon steel to prevent cracking) creates risk of oil ignition — the oil can flash if the workpiece is too hot. Use a deep quench tank, lower the workpiece quickly and completely, and keep a lid nearby to smother oil fires. Do not use oil quenching near open flames.
