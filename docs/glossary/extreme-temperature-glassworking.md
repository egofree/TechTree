# Extreme-temperature glassworking

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: glass

Borosilicate melts at 1500-1600°C; fused silica requires 1700-2100°C working temperatures. Hydrogen-oxygen torches produce flame temperatures of ~2800°C. At these temperatures, severe burns occur on brief skin contact and radiation burns are possible at close range. Wear heat-resistant gloves, arm guards, face shield, and closed-toe boots.
