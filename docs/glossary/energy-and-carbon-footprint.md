# Energy and carbon footprint

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Refractory manufacturing is energy-intensive. Fireclay bricks: 2-4 GJ/tonne. High-alumina: 4-8 GJ/tonne. Magnesite (dead-burned): 8-15 GJ/tonne (the highest, due to the extreme calcination temperature). Firing is the dominant energy consumer (natural gas or heavy fuel oil in tunnel kilns). Carbon dioxide emissions: 0.2-0.8 tonnes CO₂ per tonne of refractory product, plus process emissions from calcination of carbonates (MgCO₃ → MgO + CO₂, CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂).
