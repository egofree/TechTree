# LP blades (near-ambient temperature but highest stress)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The last few rows of LP blades are the longest in the turbine (up to 1200 mm in large units) and spin at full rated speed. Centrifugal stress at the blade root scales with the product of blade mass and the square of rotational speed. Titanium alloy (Ti-6Al-4V) is preferred for blades longer than 900 mm because its density (4430 kg/m³) is roughly half that of steel (7850 kg/m³), halving the centrifugal load for the same blade geometry.
