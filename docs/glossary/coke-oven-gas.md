# Coke oven gas

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Coal carbonization in coke ovens produces gas containing ~1% NH₃. Scrub with sulfuric acid to produce (NH₄)₂SO₄ (ammonium sulfate fertilizer). The NH₃ can be liberated by reaction with Ca(OH)₂ for Solvay use. This was the primary ammonia source before Haber-Bosch.
