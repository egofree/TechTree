# Nitroglycerin (Sobrero, 1847)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: mining

A liquid high explosive, extremely sensitive to shock and friction. Too dangerous for practical use until Alfred Nobel absorbed it in diatomaceous earth to create dynamite (1867). Dynamite detonates at 6,000-7,000 m/s (vs. 400-800 m/s for black powder), producing a shattering force 5-10× greater.
