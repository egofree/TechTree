# Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA)

> **Type**: process | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The standard method for purifying SMR-derived hydrogen. Four to twelve adsorption vessels in parallel, filled with layered beds: activated carbon (removes H₂O, CO₂), zeolite 5A (removes CH₄, CO), and zeolite 13X (final polishing). Feed gas (SMR output shifted to maximize H₂, CO₂ removed) at 15-30 bar passes through beds — impurities adsorbed, H₂ passes through at 99.97-99.999% purity. Recovery: 75-90% of feed hydrogen.
