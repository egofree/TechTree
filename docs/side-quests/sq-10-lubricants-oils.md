# SQ 10: Lubricants, Oils & Fluid Mechanics

**Starts**: Phase 1  
**Priority**: Critical — without lubrication, every moving part seizes.

## Problem

Every machine with moving parts generates friction and heat. Without lubrication, bearings gall, slides bind, gears wear out in hours, and cutting tools destroy both themselves and the workpiece. A civilization rebuilding its industrial base needs lubricants from day one, starting with animal fats and evolving toward refined mineral oils and synthetic hydraulic fluids.

## Technologies & Systems

### Animal Fats & Vegetable Oils
- Tallow (beef/mutton fat): first lubricant for axles, slides, simple bearings
- Lard (pig fat): lighter lubricant for small mechanisms and cutting fluids
- Whale oil (if coastal): excellent light lubricant, later displaced by mineral oils
- Olive oil: early bearing lubricant and cutting fluid
- Linseed oil: drying oil for protective coatings and paint binder
- Castor oil: high-viscosity lubricant for high-speed bearings and early engines

### Mineral Oils
- Petroleum-derived lubricants (see [SQ 12](sq-12-petrochemicals.md)): paraffinic and naphthenic base oils
- Distillation cuts tailored to viscosity grade (spindle oil, machine oil, gear oil)

### Lubrication Regimes & Cutting Fluids
- Boundary lubrication: thin film, relies on polar additives (fatty acids from animal/vegetable sources)
- Hydrodynamic lubrication: full fluid film separates surfaces, requires correct viscosity and bearing clearance
- Water-based emulsions (soluble oil) for cooling and chip clearing in machining
- Straight oils for heavy cutting and tapping operations

### Grease & Hydraulic Fluids
- Oil thickened with soap (sodium/calcium/lithium stearate) or clay for wheel bearings, open gears, slides
- Incompressible hydraulic fluid for power transmission (presses, jacks, machine tools)
- Vegetable oil-based initially, mineral oil-based later (links to SQ 8 seals)

### Bearing Lubrication & Vacuum Oils
- Plain bearings: oil-ring, wick, or splash lubrication systems
- Ball/roller bearings: grease-packed, sealed for life
- Low-vapor-pressure oils for vacuum pump seals and diffusion pumps (Phase 6+)

## Integration Points

| Phase | Contribution |
|-------|-------------|
| Phase 1 | Animal fat collection, basic axle grease for carts and simple machines |
| Phase 2 | Tallow-lubricated bearings for treadle lathes and bellows |
| Phase 3 | Cutting fluids and machine oils for precision machine tools |
| Phase 4 | Cylinder oils for steam engines, high-temperature bearing lubrication |
| Phase 5 | Chemical processing of oils, soap thickeners for grease |
| Phase 6 | Low-vapor-pressure oils for vacuum pump seals and diffusion pumps |
| Phase 7 | Ultraclean lubricants for crystal pullers and wafer handling equipment |
| Phase 8 | Cleanroom-compatible lubricants and hydraulic fluids for fab equipment |

## Key Deliverables

- Reliable supply chain for animal and vegetable lubricants
- Cutting fluid formulations for lathe, mill, drill, and grinding operations
- Grease production capability (soap-thickened and clay-thickened)
- Hydraulic fluid supply for presses and machine tools
- Vacuum-compatible oils for Phase 6+ pump systems
- Bearing lubrication protocols for plain and rolling-element bearings
