# Angora wool

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: animals

Fiber from Angora rabbits, 12-16 microns in diameter — finer than cashmere. 7× warmer than sheep wool by weight due to hollow fibers. Harvested by plucking, shearing, or combing every 90-100 days. Yield: 400-1200 g/year per rabbit depending on breed. Blended with sheep wool for structure.
