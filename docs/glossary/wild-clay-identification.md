# Wild clay identification

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

Clay deposits often appear as sticky, blue-gray or red-orange layers in eroded banks. Rub a small amount between your fingers; clay feels smooth and soapy, not gritty. If it feels gritty, it has too much silt or sand (which can work as a natural temper, but the clay body may be weak). Test-fire a small ball of the clay: if it holds its shape and rings when tapped, you have usable clay.
