# Bitumen resist

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: photolithography

Bitumen resist (simplest, historical — Niépce, 1826) — - Dissolve bitumen of Judea (natural asphalt) in lavender oil or turpentine. Coat on substrate. Expose to UV through mask (hours of exposure — very slow). Exposed areas harden (polymerize), unexposed areas dissolve in solvent. Low resolution (~100 μm+), very slow, but requires zero chemistry infrastructure. See: [Photoresists, Masks & Lithography](../photolithography/resists-masks.md)
