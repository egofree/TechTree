# Brazing

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Brazing (joining with filler metal, later stages) — - **Principle**: Heat base metals to above the melting point of filler (but below their own melting point). Capillary action draws molten filler into the joint gap. On cooling, filler solidifies — strong joint without melting base metal. - **Brass brazing (spelter brazing)**: Filler = brass alloy (Cu + Zn, typically 60/40, melts ~900°C).
