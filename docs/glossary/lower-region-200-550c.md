# Lower region (200-550°C)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Austenite transforms to **bainite**. Upper bainite (350-550°C) consists of laths of ferrite with discrete cementite particles — moderate hardness (~40-50 HRC), good toughness. Lower bainite (200-350°C) has acicular (needle-like) ferrite plates with fine epsilon-carbide precipitates within the plates — high hardness (~45-55 HRC) with better toughness than martensite at equivalent hardness. Bainite forms over minutes to hours.
