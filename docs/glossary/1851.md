# 1851

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Dover-Calais cable — first successful submarine telegraph cable, 38 km across the English Channel, gutta-percha insulated. Proved the concept was viable for underwater telegraphy - **1858**: First transatlantic cable attempted (and failed after 3 weeks of operation — the copper conductor was too thin at ~1.6 mm² cross-section, and attempts to compensate by applying high voltage (500+ V) burned through the gutta-percha insulation).
