# Manufacturing scale

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

The 1866 transatlantic cable contained ~3,700 km of gutta-percha insulated conductor (7-wire stranded copper, 12.5 mm² cross-section, insulated with ~4 mm gutta-percha wall thickness). The Gutta Percha Company in London was the sole supplier for most of the 19th century, processing thousands of tonnes annually at peak production.
