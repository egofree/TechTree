# PTFE fume fever (polymer fume fever)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Overheating PTFE above 350°C decomposes it into toxic pyrolysis products including perfluoroisobutylene (PFIB, lethal at low ppm) and carbonyl fluoride. Inhalation causes polymer fume fever — flu-like symptoms (chills, fever, cough, chest tightness) appearing 4-8 hours after exposure. Repeated episodes cause permanent lung damage.
