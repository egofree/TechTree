# Betz limit

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

No wind rotor can extract more than 16/27 (59.3%) of the kinetic energy in the wind. This is because the wind must retain enough kinetic energy to flow away from the rotor and make room for incoming air. Practical traditional windmills achieve Cp = 0.15-0.30, well below the Betz limit. Modern wind turbines with optimized airfoil blades reach Cp = 0.40-0.50.
