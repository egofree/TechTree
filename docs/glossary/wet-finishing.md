# Wet finishing

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Wet finishing (loom-state cloth) — Cloth fresh off the loom contains spinning oils, sizing residue, and loose fiber ends. The weave structure has not yet "settled" — the yarns are straight and parallel, not fully interlocked. Wet finishing remedies this: - **Fulling** (wool): Agitate cloth in hot water (40-60°C) with mild alkali.
