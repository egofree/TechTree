# TiCl₄

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Reacts violently with moisture in air, producing dense white HCl fume and TiO₂ particulate. Severe burns to skin and eyes. All TiCl₄ handling must be in enclosed, dry systems. Leak response: water spray (from safe distance) to knock down HCl cloud, full acid-rated PPE. TiCl₄ spills on moisture generate massive exothermic reaction — approach upwind only.
