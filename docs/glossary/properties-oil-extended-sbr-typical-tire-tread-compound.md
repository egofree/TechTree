# Properties (oil-extended SBR, typical tire tread compound)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Tensile strength 17-25 MPa (unfilled SBR is weak — carbon black reinforcement at 50 phr raises tensile from ~2 MPa to 20+ MPa). Elongation 400-600%. Tg ≈ -55°C (lower than natural rubber, better low-temperature flexibility). Aging resistance superior to natural rubber (SBR has no unsaturated sites susceptible to ozone attack in the same way). Abrasion resistance comparable to natural rubber when properly compounded. Not oil-resistant — use NBR for oil contact.
