# Water hammer

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Rapid valve closure on a long penstock creates a pressure shock wave that travels at the speed of sound in water (~1400 m/s). The resulting pressure spike can burst pipes or damage turbine casings. Prevention: surge tanks (standpipe on the penstock that absorbs the shock), slow-closing valves, jet deflectors (Pelton) that reduce power without closing the nozzle.
