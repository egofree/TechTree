# Firefighting

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Do NOT use water on a coal pile fire — steam explosion risk and water spreads the fire by creating channels for air. Instead: dig out burning section, spread thin to cool, or smother with fine damp sand. For underground coal seam fires, the only reliable method is to cut off air supply by sealing mine entries, or excavate the burning coal entirely. Some coal seam fires burn for decades or centuries (Centralia, Pennsylvania has burned since 1962).
