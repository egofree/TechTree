# Solder fumes (lead-based solder)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: computing

Traditional tin-lead solder (60/40 or 63/37 Sn-Pb) generates fumes during soldering — primarily from the rosin flux core, which releases formaldehyde and other irritants. Lead itself does not vaporize at soldering temperatures (~230°C) but accumulates on surfaces and hands as oxide dust. Use local exhaust ventilation (fume extractor or open window).
