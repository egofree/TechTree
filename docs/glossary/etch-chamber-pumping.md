# Etch chamber pumping

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

A typical plasma etch chamber requires 500-2000 L/s pumping speed to maintain process pressure (5-100 mTorr) while gas flows at 50-500 sccm. Turbo-molecular pump (TMP): rotor spinning at 40,000-60,000 RPM with multiple blade stages that transfer momentum to gas molecules, preferentially directing them toward the exhaust. Backed by a dry scroll pump (15-30 L/s) that maintains the TMP foreline pressure below 0.5 Torr. Pump-down time from atmosphere to base pressure (<1×10⁻⁶ Torr): 15-30 minutes...
