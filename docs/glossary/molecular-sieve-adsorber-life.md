# Molecular sieve adsorber life

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The adsorbent beds (activated alumina + 13X zeolite) lose capacity over time due to moisture cycling, thermal stress, and contamination. Typical adsorbent life: 3-7 years. Replacement cost: $50,000-200,000 per bed (the adsorbent is inexpensive but the plant shutdown for changeout is costly). Adsorbent degradation is monitored by tracking the CO₂ breakthrough time (if CO₂ appears at the adsorber outlet before the scheduled switch, the bed is exhausted).
