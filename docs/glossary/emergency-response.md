# Emergency Response

Emergency response encompasses the procedures, equipment, and training required to handle accidents and incidents in industrial facilities. Every plant needs an emergency action plan covering fire (extinguisher types: ABC dry chemical for general fires, CO₂ for electrical fires, water for paper and wood only), chemical spill response (absorbent materials, neutralization agents, evacuation distances—e.g., 50 m minimum radius for an H₂SO₄ spill), medical emergencies (first aid stations, AED placement within a 3-minute walk of any point, posted emergency phone numbers), and rescue operations (confined space rescue teams with SCBA, rope rescue for elevated work).

In chemical plants handling ammonia, dopant gases, or other toxic materials, emergency response planning must account for toxic gas release scenarios. Wind direction indicators, evacuation routes, and shelter-in-place procedures are essential. Emergency showers and eyewash stations must be reachable within 10 seconds of travel from any point where chemical exposure is possible.

Training is the foundation of effective emergency response: regular drills for fire evacuation, spill containment, and medical response ensure that personnel can act correctly under stress. In a bootstrap industrial context, where professional emergency services may be distant or unavailable, on-site capability becomes even more critical.
