# Lime hazards

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Quicklime (CaO) reacts violently with water — CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ releases 65 kJ/mol. The reaction can generate temperatures above 150°C, causing steam explosions if water is added too quickly to large quantities. Always add lime to water (not water to lime). The resulting slaked lime (Ca(OH)₂) is also caustic — causes chemical burns on skin and eyes. PPE: long sleeves, gloves, eye protection.
