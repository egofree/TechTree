# Site Selection

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: energy, health, plants

The process of evaluating and choosing optimal locations for industrial facilities, sanitation systems, fuel production, and agriculture based on geological, hydrological, and logistical criteria.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Site selection is critical for [Charcoal Production](../energy/charcoal.md), [Pumped Hydro Storage](../energy/pumped-hydro.md), [Sanitation](../health/sanitation.md) systems, and [Edible Plants](../plants/edible-plants.md) cultivation. Each application has specific requirements: charcoal kilns need proximity to forest and transport; pumped hydro needs elevation difference and water; sanitation needs geology that prevents groundwater contamination.

## Technical Details

Sanitation site selection: minimum 300 m from water supply sources, 30 m from property lines. Avoid floodplains, wetlands, and areas with high water table (seasonal high water table >1.5 m below proposed base). Geology: clay-rich soils preferred for natural sealing — permeability <10⁻⁷ cm/s. Sandy or gravelly soils require impermeable liners (clay or geomembrane).

Charcoal production sites: proximity to timber source (minimize transport), access to road or waterway for product shipment, prevailing winds carrying smoke away from habitation, level ground for kiln construction, firebreak clearance of 10+ m around kilns.

Pumped hydro site selection: elevation difference (head) of 100+ m between upper and lower reservoirs, suitable geology for reservoir construction (or existing water bodies), proximity to electrical grid, environmental considerations (no critical habitats in reservoir footprint).

Agricultural site selection: soil depth >60 cm for grain crops, pH 6.0-7.5 for most vegetables, slope <8° for rainfed agriculture, access to water for irrigation, and frost-free growing season of sufficient length.
