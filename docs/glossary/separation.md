# Separation

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: chemistry, mining

The physical and chemical processes that isolate desired materials from mixtures, slurries, or ores. Separation is fundamental to chemical processing and mineral beneficiation.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Separation processes appear throughout the tech tree. In [Alkalis](../chemistry/alkalis.md), CaCO₃ sludge is separated by vacuum or pressure filtration, with filter cake washing to recover entrained NaOH. In [Mining Processing](../mining/processing.md), gravity separation, flotation, and magnetic separation concentrate ores. In [Black Powder](../mining/black-powder.md), mechanical separation refines raw ingredients.

## Technical Details

Filtration: passing a slurry through a porous medium that retains solids while allowing liquid to pass. Vacuum filtration uses suction to accelerate the process; pressure filtration forces liquid through under pressure. Filter cake must be washed to recover entrained solution — each tonne of CaCO₃ retains 0.3-0.5 tonnes of solution in its pores.

Gravity separation exploits density differences: heavier minerals settle faster in water or air. Sluice boxes, settling ponds, and spiral classifiers all use this principle. Flotation introduces air bubbles that selectively attach to hydrophobic mineral particles, floating them to the surface for collection.

Magnetic separation pulls ferromagnetic minerals (magnetite) from gangue using permanent magnets or electromagnets. Electrostatic separation exploits differences in electrical conductivity — charged particles follow different trajectories in an electric field.
