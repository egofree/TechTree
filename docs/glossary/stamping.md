# Stamping

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

Rags soaked in water for 3-7 days (fermentation breaks down inter-fiber bonds). Stamp with water-powered trip hammer (stamper) — wooden shafts armed with iron nails beat the rag mass in a stone trough. Duration: 12-24 hours for coarse paper, up to 48 hours for fine writing paper. The stamping separates and fibrillates fibers without cutting them (unlike mechanical grinding, which shortens fibers).
