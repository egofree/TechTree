# High and ultra-high vacuum gauges

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: gas-handling

Instruments for measuring very low pressures: ionization gauges (Bayard-Alpert, Penning) measure high vacuum (10⁻³ to 10⁻¹⁰ Pa) by ionizing residual gas molecules and measuring the resulting ion current. Accurate measurement is essential for semiconductor processing, thin-film deposition, and surface science.
