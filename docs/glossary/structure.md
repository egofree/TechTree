# Structure

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: animals, computing, energy, metals, photolithography, silicon, transport

The physical arrangement and organization of components — from crystal lattices and molecular bonds to building frames and transistor architectures. Structure determines the properties and performance of every material and system in the tech tree.

## Technical Details

Building structures: timber-frame construction with wattle-and-daub infill or stone walls. Roofs use thatch (rye straw or reed) or wooden shingles. Earthen floors with 10-20 cm of bedding material are standard. Drainage slopes floors toward the door. Post-and-beam frames carry roof loads to the foundation.

Crystal structures in metallurgy: austenitic stainless steels are face-centered cubic (FCC), ferritic grades are body-centered cubic (BCC), and martensitic grades transform to body-centered tetragonal (BCT) after quenching. Duplex stainless steels combine austenite and ferrite in a laminar microstructure for roughly twice the yield strength. Nickel superalloys use γ' (Ni₃Al) precipitates coherent with the γ matrix for high-temperature strength.

Semiconductor device structures: the MOSFET has a gate electrode separated from the channel by a thin oxide dielectric. FinFET structures wrap the gate around a thin silicon fin for better electrostatic control at small nodes. CMOS structures pair NMOS and PMOS transistors for low static power consumption.
