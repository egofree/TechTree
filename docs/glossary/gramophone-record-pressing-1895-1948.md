# Gramophone record pressing (1895-1948)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Before vinyl records, 78 RPM discs were made from shellac-based compounds. The formulation: ~25% shellac binder, ~75% mineral filler (crushed slate, limestone, carbon black pigment), and trace plasticizer. Records were compression-molded: the shellac compound powder was placed in a steam-heated mold (120-130°C), pressed at 15-30 MPa for 20-30 seconds, then cooled before demolding.
