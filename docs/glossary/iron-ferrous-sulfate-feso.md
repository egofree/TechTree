# Iron (ferrous sulfate, FeSO₄)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Darkens and "saddens" colors — turns yellow to olive, blue to gray-black. Make by dissolving iron scraps in vinegar (acetic acid) or stale urine. Use 2-5% of fiber weight. Overuse embrittles protein fibers — iron catalyzes oxidative degradation in wool and silk, causing progressive weakening and brittleness over months to years. Keep iron mordant concentrations below 5% for protein fibers.
