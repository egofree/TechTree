# Implosion risk from glass vacuum chambers

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: gas-handling

Glass bell jars and glass vacuum apparatus are under ~1 atmosphere (14.7 psi) of external pressure when evacuated. A flaw, scratch, or star crack concentrates stress and can cause sudden catastrophic implosion — the glass fragments are accelerated inward by atmospheric pressure, then bounce outward at high velocity.
