# Example: salicylic acid from willow bark

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: health

Macerate willow bark in ethanol for 3 days. Filter. Evaporate ethanol to obtain crude extract. Dissolve crude in hot water. Add NaOH to convert salicylic acid to sodium salicylate (water-soluble). Filter to remove insoluble impurities. Acidify filtrate with H₂SO₄ to precipitate salicylic acid. Collect by filtration. Recrystallize from hot water. Pure salicylic acid forms white needles, melting point 159°C (sharp melting point confirms purity — impure substances melt over a wide range).
