# Hematocrit

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: health

Draw capillary blood into a heparinized glass tube (75 mm length, 1 mm inner diameter). Seal one end with clay. Centrifuge at 10,000-15,000 RPM for 5 minutes. Read the packed cell layer as a fraction of total column length. Normal: 40-54% (males), 36-48% (females). Low hematocrit = anemia (blood loss, iron deficiency, chronic disease). High hematocrit = dehydration, polycythemia.
