# Runner geometry

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The Francis runner has 12-20 curved blades mounted between an upper crown (band) and a lower band (shroud). Water enters radially at the outer diameter and exits axially at the inner diameter (the "eye"). The blades are three-dimensionally twisted: the inlet edge is nearly radial, while the outlet edge is nearly axial. This twist accommodates the changing flow direction as water passes through the runner. The blade profiles must be accurate to within 1-2 mm for good efficiency.
