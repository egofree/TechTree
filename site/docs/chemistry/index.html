Chemistry

Bulk mineral acids, alkalis, industrial gases, distillation infrastructure, and cement production. A civilization's industrial maturity can be measured by its sulfuric acid production. Provides the etchants, solvents, reagents, and purification infrastructure needed for semiconductor fabrication.

Capabilities

  • Alkali Production

    Leblanc process (first synthetic soda ash) and Solvay process (more efficient, later) for Na₂CO₃, lime-soda process and chlor-alkali electrolysis for NaOH. Soda ash and caustic soda are essential for glass making, soap production, paper pulping, and chemical processing.

  • Cement & Concrete

    Portland cement from limestone + clay heated to 1450°C, ground with gypsum. Concrete (1:2:4 cement:sand:gravel mix), reinforced concrete with steel rebar, and curing protocols. Dominant building material for factory foundations, dams, roads, and vibration isolation pads.

  • Distillation & Gas Handling

    Distillation columns (2-10 m, copper or steel, packed with ceramic Raschig rings), batch and continuous flow reactors, piping/valves/pumps for corrosive service, shell-and-tube heat exchangers, gas compression and storage, and purification by scrubbing, molecular sieves, and catalytic getters.

  • Mineral Acid Production

    Sulfuric acid (lead chamber process 65-70%, contact process 96-98%), nitric acid (from nitrates + H₂SO₄, later Ostwald process), hydrochloric acid (NaCl + H₂SO₄ or direct H₂ + Cl₂ synthesis), and hydrofluoric acid (CaF₂ + H₂SO₄, extremely hazardous). HF is uniquely dangerous and essential for glass and silicon etching.
