# Carnot efficiency

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The theoretical minimum work to separate air into pure O₂ and N₂ at 1 atm is only ~0.06 kWh/Nm³ O₂. Real ASU efficiency is thus ~10-15% of theoretical — the remaining losses are from irreversibilities in heat exchangers (finite temperature differences), distillation trays (finite composition differences), and throttling (J-T valves). Expansion turbines recover some work and improve efficiency.
