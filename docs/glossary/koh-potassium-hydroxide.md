# KOH (potassium hydroxide)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: photolithography

KOH (potassium hydroxide): 30-45% KOH in water at 70-85°C. Etch rate for Si <100>: ~1.1 μm/min (at 30%, 80°C). Etch rate ratio <100>:<111> ≈ 100:1. The {111} crystal planes etch ~100× slower than {100} planes, producing precise V-grooves, pyramidal pits, and thin membranes bounded by {111} sidewalls. Alignment of mask features to the <110> flat is critical: a 1° misalignment produces significant undercut. See: [Core Fab Processes](../photolithography/fab-processes.md)
