# Iron teeth for heavy duty

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

When the load exceeds what wooden teeth can reliably carry (above roughly 5-10 HP), cast iron gear rims with machined teeth are substituted. Iron teeth last decades rather than years but transmit shock directly to the shaft and bearings. The gear blank is a cast iron rim shrunk onto a wooden or iron hub, with teeth cut by milling or planing. Requires a foundry and machine tools.
