# Composition and mineralogy

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

93-97% SiO₂, with 1-3% CaO (added as lime bond during manufacturing), and minor Fe₂O₃ and Al₂O₃. The brick must be fired sufficiently to convert >80% of the quartz to the high-temperature cristobalite and tridymite forms. Residual quartz causes permanent expansion during service as it slowly converts, potentially disrupting furnace linings.
