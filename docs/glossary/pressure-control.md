# Pressure control

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Column pressure affects relative volatility and tray capacity. Lower pressure generally improves relative volatility (easier separation) but reduces vapor density (may require larger diameter). Atmospheric columns: pressure controlled by a vent or nitrogen blanketing system. Vacuum columns: pressure controlled by regulating vacuum pump or steam ejector operation. Pressure fluctuations cause tray flooding or weeping, destabilizing the separation.
