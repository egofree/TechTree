# Curing

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Concrete gains strength by hydration of clinker minerals with water. C₃S + H₂O → C-S-H gel (calcium silicate hydrate — the binding phase) + Ca(OH)₂. Proper curing requires moisture retention for 7-28 days. Methods: water ponding, wet burlap, plastic sheeting, or spray-on curing compounds. Compressive strength development: 40% at 3 days, 65% at 7 days, 100% at 28 days (for OPC).
