# Pharmacopoeia standards

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: health

A pharmacopoeia is an official reference book listing drug standards (identity, purity, potency, dosage). Historical examples: De Materia Medica (Dioscorides, ~70 AD), London Pharmacopoeia (1618), United States Pharmacopeia (USP, 1820). A bootstrap pharmacopoeia should document: plant source (with botanical drawing), harvest time and method, preparation method, dosage range, known side effects, and contraindications.
