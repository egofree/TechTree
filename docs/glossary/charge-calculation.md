# Charge calculation

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: mining

The powder charge depends on rock type, hole geometry, and desired fragmentation. A starting point: 0.5-2 kg of black powder per cubic meter of rock to be broken. Soft sedimentary rock (limestone, sandstone) requires 0.5-1 kg/m³. Hard igneous rock (granite, basalt) requires 1-2 kg/m³. The burden (distance from the charge to the nearest free face) should be 20-30× the hole diameter. A 30 mm hole has an optimal burden of 0.6-0.9 m.
