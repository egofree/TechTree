# Vegetation changes

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: mining

Stunted, discolored, or absent vegetation in otherwise healthy area may indicate toxic minerals at shallow depth (copper, arsenic, sulfur). "Indicator plants": certain species thrive in metal-rich soils (e.g., Viola calaminaria for zinc). Conversely, dead zones in wet ground may indicate sulfide oxidation producing acid soils.
