# Cooking conditions

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Acidic sulfite (pH 1.5-4) cooks at 130-150°C for 6-12 hours, considerably longer than kraft (2-4 hours). The sulfonating action of HSO₃⁻ introduces sulfonate groups (-SO₃H) onto lignin, making it hydrophilic and water-soluble. This sulfonation mechanism differs from kraft's alkaline hydrolysis. The result: sulfite pulp is lighter in color (easier to bleach) and produces softer, more easily beaten fibers.
