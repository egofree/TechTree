# Electron-beam evaporation

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: optics

For refractory materials (TiO₂, Ta₂O₅, SiO₂) that require temperatures above 2000°C, an electron beam (5-10 kV, 0.1-1 A) is magnetically deflected into a water-cooled copper crucible containing the source material. The beam heats only a small spot, creating a localized melt pool while the surrounding material acts as its own crucible liner. This avoids contamination from boat material.
