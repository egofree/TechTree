# Bell telephone

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: transport

(1876): - A diaphragm (thin iron disc, 0.3-0.5 mm thick, 50-60 mm diameter) vibrates in response to sound waves. A permanent magnet with a coil of fine wire (0.1-0.2 mm, 50-100 Ω) sits near the diaphragm. Vibration changes the magnetic flux through the coil, inducing a current that is an electrical analog of the sound. - At the receiver, an identical device converts the varying current back into diaphragm vibration, reproducing the sound.
