# Proto-cuneiform

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

(~3400-3000 BCE): Sumerian pictographic impressions on clay tablets for administrative record-keeping — livestock counts, grain disbursements, tax receipts. Approximately 900 distinct signs, each representing a commodity or numeric concept. Stylus: reed cut to produce wedge-shaped impressions (Latin *cuneus* = wedge).
