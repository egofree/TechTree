# Temperature profile and volatile release

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The heating rate must be precisely controlled to avoid electrode failure. Between 200°C and 500°C, the coal tar pitch binder undergoes pyrolytic decomposition, releasing 25-35% of its mass as volatile gases (hydrogen, methane, aromatic vapors). If the heating rate exceeds 2-5°C per hour in this range, volatile generation outpaces the permeability of the electrode body.
