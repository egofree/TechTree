# Quality Testing

> **Type**: process | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: transport, silicon, metals, ceramics, health

Every meter of finished cable was electrically tested. Insulation resistance measured with a sensitive galvanometer at 500-1000 V DC — minimum acceptable: 100 MΩ/km for new cable. Capacitance measured per unit length (typical: 0.2-0.4 μF/km for gutta-percha insulated cable). Any section failing inspection was cut out and replaced.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Quality testing — verifying that a product meets its specifications — is essential at every stage of industrial production. In submarine cable manufacturing ([Gutta-Percha](../polymers/gutta-percha.md)), every meter of cable was tested for insulation resistance and capacitance, with the 1866 transatlantic cable tested for 6 weeks before shipment. In [Iron & Steel](../metals/iron-steel.md), tensile testing, impact testing (Charpy V-notch), and hardness testing verify mechanical properties. In [Silicon Production](../silicon/mg-si-production.md), XRF and spark OES measure chemical composition against grade specifications. In [Pharmacology](../health/pharmacology.md), identity testing, purity assessment, and dosage uniformity ensure drug safety.

## Technical Details

Quality testing methods in the tech tree range from simple physical inspection to sophisticated analytical chemistry. For submarine cables, the two critical electrical tests were insulation resistance (must exceed 100 MΩ/km, measured at high DC voltage to detect microscopic defects) and capacitance per unit length (which determines maximum signaling speed — high capacitance attenuates telegraph pulses).

For metals, the testing suite includes tensile testing (yield strength, UTS, elongation — the fundamental mechanical property triad), impact testing (ductile-to-brittle transition temperature — critical for structural steel in cold environments), and hardness testing (Brinell, Rockwell, Vickers — non-destructive strength estimation). Metallography (polishing, etching, microscopic examination) reveals grain structure, inclusion content, and heat treatment effectiveness.

For pharmaceuticals, quality testing includes identity verification (color, smell, crystal form, simple chemical reactions), purity assessment (melting point sharpness — pure compounds melt over 1-2°C), and dosage uniformity (all tablets within ±10% of target weight). Stability testing at elevated temperature (40°C for 1 month) provides accelerated aging data to estimate shelf life.
