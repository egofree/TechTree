# Calamine cementation process

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

(pre-industrial): Before metallic zinc was available (zinc boils at 907°C, below its melting point of 419°C, making it difficult to produce as a free metal), brass was made by cementation. Ground calamine (zinc carbonate, ZnCO₃, or zinc oxide, ZnO) is mixed with crushed charcoal and heated with molten copper in a closed crucible at 900-1000°C.
