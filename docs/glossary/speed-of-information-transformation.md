# Speed of information transformation

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: transport

Before the telegraph, transatlantic information traveled at ship speed — 10-14 days by fast sailing packet, 7-10 days by steamship. After the 1866 cable, the same information crossed in minutes. On land, news traveled at telegraph speed (~15 words per minute on a single line, multiplied by hundreds of parallel lines) versus courier or pony express speed (8-15 km/h, with relay stations every 15-25 km).
