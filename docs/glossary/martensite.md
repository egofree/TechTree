# martensite

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

by a diffusionless shear mechanism. Carbon atoms are trapped in the BCT (body-centered tetragonal) lattice, causing severe lattice strain — this is what makes martensite hard (50-65 HRC depending on carbon content). Martensite starts forming at the Ms temperature (~200-350°C depending on carbon content) and finishes at Mf (~-50 to 150°C). Transformation is instantaneous at each temperature — no holding time needed.
