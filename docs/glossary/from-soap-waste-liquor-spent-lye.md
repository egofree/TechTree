# From soap waste liquor (spent lye)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: chemistry

Recovery of glycerol from soap-making waste: after saponification, the spent lye (bottoms layer) contains glycerol and excess alkali that can be recovered through acidification, purification, and distillation. Glycerol is a valuable byproduct used for explosives (nitroglycerin), cosmetics, and chemical synthesis.
