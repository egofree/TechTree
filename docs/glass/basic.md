# Basic Glass Production

> **Node ID**: `glass.basic`
> **Domain**: [Glass](./)
> **Timeline**: Years 5-15
> **Outputs**: basic_glass, potash

**Glass production**:
- **Raw materials**: Silica sand (SiO₂, >95% purity, washed and sieved to 60-100 mesh) + flux (wood ash potash K₂CO₃, or natron Na₂CO₃) + stabilizer (limestone CaCO₃ or dolomite). Typical mix: 70% sand, 20% flux, 10% stabilizer.
- **Potash production**: Leach wood ashes with water (ash:water = 1:3 by volume). Boil concentrated solution. Crystallized potassium carbonate = potash. ~5 kg potash per 100 kg dry hardwood ash.
- **Melting**: Heat mixture in clay crucible to 1100-1400°C (requires good furnace + forced air). Melt takes 8-24 hours for full homogenization. Stir with iron rod to remove bubbles and mix.
- **Working**: Gather molten glass on iron blowpipe (1-1.5 m, 1-2 cm diameter). Blow, shape with wet wooden tools, anneal in cooling oven (500-600°C initially, cool slowly over 4-12 hours).
- **Early products**: Beads, simple vessels, flat window glass (crown method: spin blown sphere until it flattens into a disk), glass beads for trade.
