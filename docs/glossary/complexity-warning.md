# Complexity warning

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Isocyanate production is non-trivial. The industrial route reacts primary amines with phosgene (COCl₂), which itself requires chlorine + carbon monoxide (CO + Cl₂ → COCl₂ over activated carbon at 200-300°C — the reaction is moderately exothermic and must be temperature-controlled). Phosgene is one of the most toxic industrial chemicals (immediately dangerous to life at 2 ppm — see Safety below).
