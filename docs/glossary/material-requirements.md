# Material requirements

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Not all thermoplastics thermoform well. Amorphous polymers (PS, ABS, PVC, PMMA) have a broad softening range and form easily. Semi-crystalline polymers (PE, PP, nylon) have a narrow melting range — they transition from rigid to fully liquid over a very narrow temperature band (~10-20°C), making them difficult to thermoform (the sheet sags uncontrollably once the crystallites melt). HDPE and PP require special techniques (sag bands, plug assist) to control material distribution during forming.
