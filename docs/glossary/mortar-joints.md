# Mortar joints

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Refractory mortar (fireclay-based or chemically bonded) joins bricks. Joint thickness should be minimized (1-2 mm maximum) because mortar is weaker than the brick and is preferentially attacked by slag. Heat-setting mortars (fireclay + plasticizer) develop ceramic bond at service temperature. Air-setting mortars (with sodium silicate or phosphoric acid binder) develop strength at room temperature but may shrink at high temperature.
