# Basic (Thomas) process

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Dolomite (MgO·CaO) or magnesite (MgO) refractory lining. Add burned lime (CaO, 10-15% of metal weight) as flux. P₂O₅ is captured as stable calcium phosphate (3CaO·P₂O₅) in the basic slag. This enabled use of high-phosphorus pig iron (1.5-2.0% P) from the Minette ores of Lorraine and Luxembourg. The phosphorus-rich Thomas slag was sold as fertilizer (16-20% P₂O₅). Dominated Continental European steelmaking 1880-1930.
