# Fatwood

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

The resin-rich heartwood of dead pine stumps. Pine roots draw resin downward after the tree dies, concentrating it in the stump core. Fatwood is 60-80% resin by weight, shavings ignite from a spark or small ember, and it burns hot enough to dry and ignite damp kindling. Identify it by its weight (noticeably heavier than normal pine), orange-amber color, and strong turpentine smell. Split from dead pine stumps using a hatchet or wedge.
