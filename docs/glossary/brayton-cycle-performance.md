# Brayton cycle performance

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The compressor pressure ratio is the primary determinant of thermal efficiency. Early industrial gas turbines operated at 4:1 to 8:1 pressure ratio, achieving 20-28% thermal efficiency. Modern units at 20:1 to 30:1 reach 35-40% simple-cycle efficiency. The specific power output (power per unit of air mass flow) increases with both pressure ratio and turbine inlet temperature.
