# Vitreous enamel

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: glass

Powdered glass (low-melting composition, 650-850°C) applied to cleaned metal (iron, steel, copper, gold) as a slurry, then fired at 750-850°C. The glass melts, flows, and bonds to the metal surface. Multiple coats (ground coat + cover coat) produce a smooth, glossy, chemically inert surface. Enamel-coated steel is used for chemical reactor vessels, cookware, and architectural panels.
