# Petroleum Extraction & Refining

Capabilities in this domain:

- [Crude Oil Extraction](extraction.md) — Surface seeps, cable-tool percussion drilling, rotary drilling with mud circulation, well completion and production systems, and enhanced oil recovery techniques.
- [Petroleum Refining](refining.md) — Atmospheric and vacuum distillation, catalytic cracking (FCC), hydrocracking, catalytic reforming, alkylation, and product blending to produce gasoline, kerosene, diesel, fuel oil, lubricants, and asphalt.
- [Petrochemical Feedstocks](petrochemicals.md) — Steam cracking for ethylene and propylene, BTX aromatics production via catalytic reforming, and key downstream derivatives (polyethylene, PVC, polystyrene, PET, nylon, polyurethane).

## SIK Placement Decision

This domain was created as a standalone domain after passing the SIK (Shared Infrastructure & Knowledge) test against both the Energy and Chemistry domains.

### vs. Energy Domain

| Dimension | Overlap | Assessment |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Infrastructure | ~25% | Petroleum extraction (drilling rigs, wellheads, pipelines) is fundamentally different from energy generation (boilers, generators, turbines). Refining shares some process heat equipment but uses different unit operations. |
| Knowledge | ~20% | Petroleum engineering (geology, reservoir dynamics, drilling mechanics) vs. energy engineering (thermodynamics, electromagnetism, power systems). Minimal shared theory. |
| Practitioner | ~15% | Petroleum engineers, geologists, and drilling crews vs. electrical and mechanical engineers. Distinct professional training and working methods. |

**Result**: Below 50% on all three dimensions. Petroleum is distinct from Energy.

### vs. Chemistry Domain

| Dimension | Overlap | Assessment |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Infrastructure | ~45% | Refining (distillation columns, reactors, heat exchangers) overlaps with chemical processing. Extraction infrastructure (drilling rigs, wells) has zero overlap. Weighted average is below 50%. |
| Knowledge | ~40% | Refining shares organic chemistry and process engineering fundamentals. Extraction (petroleum geology, drilling engineering) is entirely separate. |
| Practitioner | ~35% | Refinery operators overlap with chemical plant operators, but drilling engineers and petroleum geologists are distinct professions. |

**Result**: Below 50% on all three dimensions. The extraction side (drilling, well completion, reservoir engineering) clearly fails SIK with chemistry. While refining has borderline overlap, the petroleum industry operates as an integrated chain from wellhead to refined product — splitting extraction from refining would create artificial fragmentation.

### Minimum Size Check

3 capabilities (extraction, refining, petrochemicals) with 2+ processes each. Exceeds the 2-3 capability minimum.

### Organizing Axis

**Process Chain** — capabilities ordered by material flow from subsurface reservoir through refining to petrochemical products.

### Override Conditions

No overrides needed. The domain passes the standard SIK test on its own merits.
