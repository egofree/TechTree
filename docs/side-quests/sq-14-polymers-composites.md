# SQ 14: Polymers & Composites

**Starts**: Phase 5  
**Priority**: High — polymers provide seals, gaskets, electrical insulation, structural composites, and packaging materials critical to semiconductor manufacturing and general infrastructure. SQ 12 produces the monomer feedstocks; this quest polymerizes them into useful materials.

## Problem

Modern civilization runs on polymers. Rubber seals keep fluids where they belong. Epoxy holds structural joints together. Fiberglass panels provide lightweight, corrosion-resistant enclosures. Without synthetic polymers, every seal is leather or metal, every adhesive is animal glue, and every structural panel is wood or sheet metal. [SQ 12](sq-12-petrochemicals.md) produces the raw feedstocks: phenol, formaldehyde, ethylene, propylene, styrene, butadiene. This quest converts those feedstocks into elastomers, thermosets, thermoplastics, and composite materials that underpin nearly every downstream technology, from plumbing seals to cleanroom consumables.

The polymer timeline is forgiving. Natural rubber vulcanization dates to 1839. Bakelite was commercialized in 1909. Polyethylene arrived in the 1930s. None required semiconductor-era precision. The challenge is scaling up reactor vessels, extruder screws, and molding presses using the machine tools already available from Phase 3.

Early polymer production feeds directly into semiconductor infrastructure. Photoresists need polymer binders. IC packaging needs molding compounds. Cleanrooms need seal materials that outgas minimally. Getting polymer basics right in Phase 5 pays dividends through every subsequent phase.

## Technologies & Systems

### Elastomers
- Natural rubber collected and vulcanized with sulfur (Goodyear process, ~1839)
- Synthetic rubber from butadiene and styrene (SBR) once [SQ 12](sq-12-petrochemicals.md) supplies feedstocks
- Silicone elastomers for high-temperature seals and medical-grade tubing
- Neoprene (polychloroprene) for oil-resistant and weather-resistant applications
- Applications: gaskets, hoses, drive belts, tire-like wheels, vibration dampers, O-ring seals
- Full detail: [Polymer Elastomers](../supporting/polymer-elastomers.md)

### Thermosetting Polymers
- Phenol-formaldehyde (Bakelite): first synthetic plastic, electrical insulators, molded components
- Urea-formaldehyde: lighter, cheaper molding compounds for housings, knobs, and fixtures
- Epoxy resins: high-strength adhesives, composite matrix resin, PCB substrate bonding
- Unsaturated polyester: room-temperature cure resins for fiberglass composite layup
- Full detail: [Polymer Thermosets](../supporting/polymer-thermosets.md)

### Thermoplastic Polymers
- Polyethylene (LDPE/HDPE): films, containers, pipe, cable insulation, moisture barriers
- Polypropylene: fatigue-resistant living hinges, chemical-resistant containers, rope fiber
- Polystyrene: rigid foam insulation, disposable molds, optical clarity applications
- Polyvinyl chloride (PVC): rigid pipe and fittings, flexible tubing with plasticizers
- Polycarbonate, ABS, acrylic noted for scope but require later-phase process maturity
- Full detail: [Polymer Thermoplastics](../supporting/polymer-thermoplastics.md)

### Composite Materials
- Fiberglass: glass fiber + polyester or epoxy matrix (see [Phase 6](../core-tech-tree/phase-06-vacuum-optics.md) for glass production)
- Layup techniques: hand layup, vacuum bagging, compression molding, resin transfer molding
- Structural applications: tank linings, pipe, electrical enclosures, vehicle body panels
- Carbon fiber and Kevlar documented as Phase 7+ aspirations, not early-phase targets
- Full detail: [Polymer Composites](../supporting/polymer-composites.md)

### Polymer Feedstock Chain
- [SQ 12](sq-12-petrochemicals.md) produces ethylene, propylene, styrene, butadiene, phenol, formaldehyde
- This quest starts where SQ 12 leaves off: monomer in, polymer out
- Polymerization reactors: batch autoclaves and continuous tubular reactors with temperature and pressure control
- Extruders and injection molding machines built from Phase 3 machine tool capabilities
- Catalyst systems: Ziegler-Natta (polyethylene, polypropylene), peroxide initiators, acid catalysts for condensation polymers

## Key Deliverables

These represent the practical outputs of the polymer production chain, ordered roughly by when each becomes achievable:

- Vulcanized rubber production capability (gaskets, belts, seals, O-rings)
- First molded thermoset parts (Bakelite electrical insulators, phenolic fittings)
- Fiberglass panel production with hand layup technique
- Epoxy resin formulation for structural adhesives and composite matrix
- Silicone rubber seal production for high-temperature and chemical-resistant applications
- Polyethylene film and container capability (blown film extrusion, rotational molding)
- Composite layup process yielding structural panels and enclosures
- Carbon fiber precursor path documented as Phase 7+ target
