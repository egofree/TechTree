# Firedamp (methane)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Methane (CH₄) trapped in coal seams is released during mining. Concentrations of 5-15% in air are explosive. Control by ventilation — large fans drive fresh air through mine workings. The **Davy safety lamp** (invented 1815) encloses the flame in fine wire gauze screen — the gauze cools flame below ignition temperature, preventing firedamp ignition. The lamp flame changes color/height in the presence of methane, serving as a gas detector. Modern mines use electronic gas detectors.
