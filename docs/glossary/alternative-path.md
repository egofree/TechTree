# Alternative path

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Resistive furnaces (silicon carbide or molybdenum disilicide heating elements) can reach ~1600°C in a controlled atmosphere. Not sufficient for full graphitization but adequate for carbon baking. Acheson furnace (graphite powder bed resistance heating) can reach 2500°C+ without pre-existing electrodes — but requires substantial power infrastructure.
