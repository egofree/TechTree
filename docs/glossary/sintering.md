# Sintering

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: ceramics, glass, metals, mining

The process of agglomerating fine particles into a cohesive solid by heating below the melting point. Sintering bonds particles through diffusion at contact points, creating strong structures without full liquefaction.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Sintering appears across multiple domains. In the [Blast Furnace](../metals/iron-steel/blast-furnace.md), fine ore particles too small for direct smelting are agglomerated by sintering with coke breeze. In [Powder Metallurgy](../metals/powder-metallurgy.md), metal powders are compacted and sintered to produce near-net-shape parts. In [Advanced Ceramics](../ceramics/advanced-ceramics.md), ceramic powders are sintered to achieve dense, strong components. In [Advanced Glass](../glass/advanced.md) and [Mining Processing](../mining/processing.md), sintering prepares materials for further processing.

## Technical Details

Ore sintering: mix fine ore with 5-10% coke breeze and moisture. Feed onto a moving grate (sinter strand), ignite the surface with gas burners, and draw air downward through the bed. The combustion zone moves through the bed, fusing particles into porous sinter cake. Sinter is then crushed and screened to the correct size (10-40 mm) for blast furnace feed.

Ceramic sintering: compacted powder is heated to 70-95% of the melting temperature. Solid-state diffusion bonds particles at contact points, reducing porosity and increasing density. Shrinkage of 10-20% during sintering must be accounted for in green (unfired) dimensions. Liquid-phase sintering adds a small amount of flux that melts and promotes faster densification.

Powder metallurgy sintering: metal compacts are heated in a reducing atmosphere (H₂/N₂) to prevent oxidation. Sintering temperatures: iron-based 1100-1250°C, copper-based 750-900°C, tungsten 2000-2200°C. Sintered density reaches 80-95% of theoretical, with corresponding strength improvements.
