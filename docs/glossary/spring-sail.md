# Spring sail

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Spring sail (wooden shutters — 1772, Andrew Meikle) — Construction**: Wooden frame divided into 6-8 rectangular panels (shutters) hinged along their top edge. Each shutter connected by linkage to a spring-loaded regulation bar. Shutters open automatically when wind force exceeds spring tension, spilling excess air — self-regulating speed control. - **Spring tension**: Adjustable via chain from inside mill (no need to stop mill to reef). Response time: shutters begin opening within 1-2 seconds of gust.... See: [Wind Power](../energy/wind.md)
