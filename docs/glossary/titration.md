# Titration

> **Type**: process | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The workhorse quantitative method for acid-base, redox, and complexometric analysis. Acid-base titration uses a burette to deliver standard solution (e.g., 0.1 M NaOH) to an unknown sample with indicator (phenolphthalein, pH 8.2-10.0 endpoint) or pH meter detection. Precision ±0.1 mL on a 25 mL burette gives ±0.4% relative error. Redox titration: KMnO₄ or I₂ as titrant for reducing agents.
