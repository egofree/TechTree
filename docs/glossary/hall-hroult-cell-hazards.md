# Hall-Héroult cell hazards

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: metals

Safety risks in Hall-Héroult aluminum electrolysis including molten cryolite burns (~1000°C), electrical hazards from high-current cells (100-500 kA), fluorine gas emission, magnetic field exposure, and explosion risk from molten metal-water contact. Requires specialized training, PPE, and emergency procedures.
