# Earthenware

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: ceramics

(low-fire, 980-1100°C): - Simplest body: 100% earthenware clay as dug, or 80% earthenware clay + 20% grog (for thermal shock resistance and reduced shrinkage). Earthenware remains porous after firing (10-25% water absorption), making it unsuitable for holding liquids without a glaze. - Working properties: very plastic, easy to form, forgiving of rough handling. Shrinks 8-12% from wet to fired.
