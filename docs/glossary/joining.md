# Joining

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Screw electrodes together using graphite nipples. Apply a thin film of graphite paste (pitch + graphite powder) to the threads to improve electrical contact and prevent seizing. Tighten to specified torque (500-3000 N·m depending on diameter) using a calibrated torque wrench or power tong. Undertightened joints cause high electrical resistance at the connection, leading to local overheating and nipple breakage. Overtightened joints crack the nipple or the electrode thread.
