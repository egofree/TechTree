# Reverse-wrap technique (2-strand)

> **Type**: process | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

Hold two fiber strands side by side. Twist both clockwise until they kink, then fold in half, and they naturally wrap counterclockwise around each other. Continue adding fibers by splicing in new strands before reaching the ends. Produces uniform cordage. This is the default method for making thin cordage (1-4 mm) used in binding, sewing, net-making, and fishing line.
