# Alternative feedstocks

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Beyond wood, gasifiers can process agricultural residues (rice husk, corn stover, coconut shells, sugarcane bagasse), but these feedstocks have different ash compositions that can cause slagging and fouling. Rice husk ash (15-20% silica) forms glassy deposits. Wheat straw has high potassium content that lowers ash fusion temperature, causing clinker formation in the gasifier hearth.
