# Burnishing

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

Rub the surface of a leather-hard pot with a smooth stone (river pebble, polished bone) in firm, overlapping strokes. This compresses the clay surface and produces a glossy sheen after firing. Burnished pots are less porous and easier to clean. Burnish after the pot has dried to leather-hard but before it reaches bone-dry; the clay must still have enough moisture to compress under the stone.
