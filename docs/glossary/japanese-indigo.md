# Japanese indigo

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Japanese indigo (*Persicaria tinctoria*) — Annual in temperate zones. Higher indigotin yield than woad — 5-10× per kg of fresh leaf. Requires rich, moist soil and warm growing season. Harvest leaves 2-3 times per season. Best choice for blue where climate permits (USDA Zone 6+). See: [Dyeing](../textiles/dyeing.md)
