Bootciv Tech Tree

Bootciv Tech Tree

A complete dependency chain for bootstrapping industrial civilization from stone-age materials to semiconductor manufacturing (GPUs, solar cells, ICs).

The tree maps every technology, material, and process needed to go from fire and stone tools to integrated circuit fabrication. It assumes a knowledgeable group starting in a resource-rich area with access to quartz, iron ore, coal, and limestone, but no pre-existing tools or infrastructure.

How to Read This Tree

Three levels, top to bottom:

Domain — a broad technology area like metals, energy, or silicon. Each domain is a self-contained world of related processes. Domains are the navigation units for this tree.

Capability — a specific achievement within a domain, like iron smelting or crystal growth. Capabilities have dotted IDs showing their parent: metals.iron-steel, silicon.crystal-growth.

Process — a detailed method or operation, like Czochralski pulling or blast furnace operation. These carry two dots: silicon.crystal-growth.cz-pulling.

Dependencies use these dotted IDs. When photolithography requires silicon, the edge reads {from: "photolithography", to: "silicon"}. The full graph lives in edges.json.

Quick Start Path

The critical path through the tree, in rough chronological order. These domains form the spine: each depends on those before it.

  1. Foundations — fire, charcoal, food surplus, stone tools
  2. Mining — ore access for every metallurgical process
  3. Metals — copper, bronze, iron, steel
  4. Machine Tools — the master enabler: lathe, shaper, mill, grinder
  5. Energy — water/wind power, steam engines, electricity, arc furnaces
  6. Ceramics — refractories, kilns, pottery, lime
  7. Chemistry — mineral acids, alkalis, distillation, oils & grease
  8. Glass — basic and advanced glass production, crucibles
  9. Gas Handling — vacuum pumps, gas compression, purification
  10. Measurement — precision metrology, measurement standards
  11. Silicon — MG-Si production, crystal growth, solar cells
  12. Photolithography — cleanrooms, lithography, IC fabrication
  13. VLSI Scaling — continuous improvement toward GPUs and advanced solar

Several capabilities in other domains are also critical because their absence blocks downstream work: knowledge (writing, education), textiles (cordage, drive belts), chemistry.lubricants (oils, grease, cutting fluid), optics (microscopes, lens grinding).

Parallel Opportunities

These domains and capabilities can begin early, independent of the main critical path. Starting them in parallel accelerates the whole effort.

  • Knowledge (knowledge.writing) — writing, printing, education. Start Day 1. The 50-200 year bootstrapping effort dies with the first generation without knowledge transmission.
  • Textiles (textiles) — fiber, spinning, rope. Start Day 1. Cordage and cloth underpin mining hoists, tool hafting, and power transmission.
  • Chemistry lubricants (chemistry.lubricants) — oils from animal fats and vegetable sources. Start Day 1. Without lubrication, every bearing and slide seizes.
  • Chemistry petroleum alternatives (chemistry.petroleum-alternatives) — fermentation produces ethanol, acetone, and acetic acid without petroleum. Start Day 1.
  • Computing (computing.mechanical) — mechanical calculation with slide rules and nomograms. Start as soon as marking tools exist.
  • Health (health.sanitation) — sanitation, water purification, quarantine protocols. Start Day 1. Skilled workers are the scarcest resource.
  • Transport (transport.roads) — road construction and basic bridges expand the reachable resource base early on.
  • Machine Tools precision metrology → now in Measurement (measurement.precision-metrology) — base unit standards and basic measuring instruments. Start alongside early toolmaking.
  • Optics (optics.inspection) — lens grinding, microscopes, optical comparators. Start once glass production is established.

Milestone Tiers

Tier 0 Survival (Years 0-5)

  • Fire-making and charcoal production (combined: friction/flint ignition + controlled pyrolysis)
  • Basic stone and wood tools
  • Agriculture surplus and animal domestication for labor
  • Basic mining and prospecting
  • Pottery and kilns
  • Writing system developed
  • Standardized basic measurements
  • Sanitation and basic health practices
  • Fiber crop cultivation (flax, hemp)
  • Drop spindle spinning and hand-laid rope
  • Surface mining, prospecting, and ore identification
  • Timber shoring and basic shaft mining

Tier 1 Industrial (Years 5-20)

  • Copper smelting and bronze alloying
  • Iron smelting to wrought iron to steel
  • Basic metalworking tools
  • Charcoal foundry operational
  • First crude lathe built
  • Steam power developed
  • First generators operational
  • Sulfuric acid production
  • Printing press operational
  • Mechanical computing aids (slide rules)
  • Animal fat lubricant supply (tallow, lard)
  • Cutting fluid production for machining
  • Grease production (soap-thickened)
  • Hydraulic fluid for presses and machine tools
  • Mine pumping and hoisting
  • Ore dressing: crushing, washing, gravity separation
  • Airframe construction capability
  • Small piston engine operational (20+ HP)
  • Carved and balanced wooden propeller
  • Natural rubber vulcanization capability
  • First molded Bakelite parts
  • Basic fiberglass panel production (hand layup)

Tier 2 Semiconductor (Years 15-40)

  • Full machine shop operational
  • Reliable electricity generation and distribution
  • Bulk chemical production (acids, alkalis)
  • Vacuum pumps and chambers operational
  • Metallurgical-grade silicon produced
  • Czochralski crystal growth producing ingots
  • First solar cells fabricated and generating power
  • Basic cleanroom or glovebox capability
  • Specialty gases supply chain
  • Lead-acid battery banks
  • Coke oven operation with coal tar collection
  • Fractional distillation (crude oil or coal tar)
  • Fermentation-derived solvents
  • Producer gas or wood gas for engines and heating
  • Early synthetic polymers (Bakelite, celluloid)
  • Epoxy resin formulation for adhesives and encapsulation
  • Phenolic laminate (FR-4) production for PCB substrates
  • Silicone seal production for high-temperature applications
  • PTFE (Teflon) production capability
  • Nitrile rubber (NBR) production
  • Neoprene (chloroprene) production
  • Unsaturated polyester resin formulation
  • Polyurethane foam production
  • Nylon (polyamide) extrusion capability
  • Polyethylene film and container production
  • PVC pipe and tubing production
  • Vacuum bagging composite manufacturing
  • Resin transfer molding (RTM) capability
  • First composite layup structural part
  • Flyable ultralight aircraft test flight completed
  • Aviation fuel supply chain

Tier 3 Advanced (Years 30-100+)

  • Photolithography capability
  • First integrated circuits (SSI/MSI)
  • Wafer packaging and testing
  • Early computers from own ICs
  • VLSI scaling in progress
  • High-end solar cells in production
  • Nuclear power research underway
  • GPU-scale VLSI design capability
  • Carbon fiber precursor development
  • Aramid/Kevlar fiber precursor development
  • Advanced composite manufacturing (filament winding, pultrusion)
