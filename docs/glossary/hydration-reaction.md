# Hydration reaction

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Cement minerals + water → calcium silicate hydrate (C-S-H) gel (the binding matrix) + calcium hydroxide (Ca(OH)₂). Heat of hydration raises concrete temperature significantly — critical for mass pours (dams, foundations). Roughly 25-35% water by weight of cement is consumed chemically; excess water creates capillary pores that reduce strength.
