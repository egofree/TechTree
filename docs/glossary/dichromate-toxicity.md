# Dichromate toxicity

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: photolithography

Ammonium dichromate ((NH₄)₂Cr₂O₇) contains Cr(VI), a confirmed human carcinogen (lung, nasal sinus). Inhalation of dust or mist is the primary route. If dichromated-gelatin resists are used for mask blanks, handle powder in a fume hood with particulate respirator (P100). Substitute novolac/DNQ resists whenever possible to eliminate chromium exposure entirely.
