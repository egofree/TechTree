# Anhydride hardeners

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

100–150°C cure, better electrical properties (lower dielectric constant and dissipation factor — critical for electronic potting and encapsulation), longer pot life (4-8 hours at 25°C — more working time for large pours), lower exotherm (reduced risk of thermal runaway in thick sections). MTHPA (methyltetrahydrophthalic anhydride) is the most common.
