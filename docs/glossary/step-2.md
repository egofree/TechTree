# Step 2

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: ceramics, chemistry, health

The second step of the START triage protocol. Step 2 assesses non-ambulatory patients through three rapid physiological checks — respirations, perfusion, and mental status — spending no more than 30 seconds per patient to assign red (immediate) or yellow (urgent) tags.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Step 2 appears in [Kiln Firing](../ceramics/kiln-firing.md) and [Alkalis](../chemistry/alkalis.md) accident response, [Ammonia](../chemistry/ammonia.md) emergency procedures, and [Medicine](../health/medicine.md) as the critical assessment phase after walking wounded have been cleared by Step 1.

## Technical Details

Three rapid checks, performed sequentially, spending no more than 30 seconds per patient:

1. **Respirations**: Not breathing after airway repositioning (head tilt-chin lift) → tag **black (expectant/deceased)**. Breathing >30 times per minute → tag **red (immediate)**. Breathing ≤30/min → proceed to next check.

2. **Perfusion**: Capillary refill >2 seconds (press nail bed, count seconds until color returns) or no palpable radial pulse → tag **red (immediate)**. Adequate perfusion → proceed to next check.

3. **Mental status**: Unable to follow simple commands ("squeeze my hand," "open your eyes") → tag **red (immediate)**. Can follow commands → tag **yellow (urgent/delayed)**.

Red-tagged patients have life-threatening conditions requiring immediate intervention (airway management, hemorrhage control). Yellow-tagged patients are seriously injured but stable enough to wait 1-2 hours for treatment. Black-tagged patients are either deceased or have injuries incompatible with survival given available resources.
