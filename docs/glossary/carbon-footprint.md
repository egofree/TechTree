# Carbon footprint

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: silicon

MG-Si production emits CO₂ from two sources: (1) the carbothermic reduction reaction itself (SiO₂ + 2C → Si + 2CO, and the CO is later burned to CO₂), producing ~3.5 tonnes CO₂ per tonne Si; and (2) electricity generation for the furnace, producing 0-5 tonnes CO₂ per tonne Si depending on the power source (0 for hydroelectric, ~5 for coal-fired electricity). Using hydroelectric power and recycling the CO gas for energy recovery reduces the total carbon footprint to ~4 tonnes CO₂ per tonne MG-Si.
