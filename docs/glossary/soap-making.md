# Soap making

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Saponification of fats and oils with NaOH produces sodium salts of fatty acids (soap) and glycerol. Typical formula: 100 kg tallow (or coconut/palm oil blend) + 14-16 kg NaOH (as 50% solution) → 100 kg soap + 10-12 kg glycerol. Temperature: 60-80°C. The reaction is exothermic (ΔH ≈ -10 kJ/mol). Cold process (artisan): mix NaOH solution with melted oils at 35-40°C, pour into molds, cure 4-6 weeks.
