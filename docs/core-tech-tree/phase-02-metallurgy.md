# Phase 2: Early Metallurgy & Mechanical Basics

**Timeline**: Years 5–15  
**Goal**: Durable tools, casting, and early chemistry.  
**Dependencies**: [Phase 1](phase-01-foundations.md) (charcoal, mining, kilns, tools)

## Objectives

- Smelt copper and alloy bronze
- Produce wrought iron and steel
- Create essential metalworking tools
- Harness water and wind power
- Produce basic glass and lime

## Key Technologies

### Copper Smelting
- Ores (malachite, azurite, chalcopyrite) + charcoal in pit or bloomery furnaces
- Bellows (skin/bag or piston types) to raise temperature
- Casting in sand or clay molds

### Bronze Alloying
- Copper + tin ores (cassiterite) → bronze
- Superior hardness and castability over pure copper

### Iron Production
- Bloomery smelting: iron ore + charcoal → spongy bloom
- Repeated heating and hammering to consolidate → wrought iron
- Carburization (adding carbon) → steel
- Quenching and tempering for hardness/toughness

### Metalworking Tools
- Anvils (stone initially, then iron)
- Hammers, tongs, chisels, files, saws
- Bellows (upgraded from Phase 1)
- Swages, fullers, punches

### Water & Wind Power
- Water wheels for grinding grain, powering bellows, early mills
- Windmills where water power unavailable
- Mechanical transmission (gears, cams, cranks from wood → iron)

### Basic Chemistry Precursors
- Glass: sand + plant ash (potash) + lime, melted in furnaces
- Lime production: limestone heated in kilns (CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂)
- Potash/lye from wood ashes
- Early soaps from fat + lye

## Key Bootstrap Loop

Hand-forged iron tools → better mining → more ore → better tools → improved furnaces → steel → precision hand tools → Phase 3 machine tools.

## Practical Bottlenecks

- **Tin availability**: Required for bronze; if locally unavailable, skip to iron
- **Temperature control**: Bloomery requires sustained ~1200°C+; good bellows design is critical
- **Carbon control**: Steel quality depends on precise carbon content — learned through experimentation

## Safety Concerns

- Severe burns from molten metal
- CO poisoning from furnaces
- Eye damage from intense furnace light
- Heavy lifting injuries
