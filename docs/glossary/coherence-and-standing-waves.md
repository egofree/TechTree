# Coherence and standing waves

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: photolithography

Partial coherence of illumination (σ parameter, typically 0.5-0.7) controls the balance between resolution and standing-wave interference effects in the resist. Standing waves cause linewidth rippling on sidewalls — mitigated by post-exposure bake (PEB) which promotes acid diffusion and smooths the profile.
