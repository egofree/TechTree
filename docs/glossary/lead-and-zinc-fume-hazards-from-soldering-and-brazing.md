# Lead and zinc fume hazards from soldering and brazing

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Soft solder (60/40 tin-lead alloy) produces lead fumes above 500°C; brass brazing filler (60/40 copper-zinc) produces zinc oxide fumes at ~900°C. Zinc oxide fume causes "metal fume fever" (flu-like symptoms: chills, fever, muscle ache, 4-8 hours after exposure). Lead fume causes cumulative neurological and organ damage. Solder and braze with local exhaust ventilation. Avoid breathing fumes directly. Wash hands after handling solder materials.
