# Teazle raising

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Teazle raising (traditional) — The dried flower head of the teasel plant (Dipsacus fullonum) has stiff, hooked bracts that catch and lift fiber ends without breaking them. Teazles are mounted in rows on a large cylindrical frame (teazle gig, 1-2 m wide, 1-1.5 m diameter). The cloth is held against the rotating teazle cylinder, and the hooks gently pull fibers upward. - Multiple passes (4-8) gradually build the nap. Each pass raises more fiber.
