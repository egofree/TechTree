# Laying operation

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Cable was paid out from the cable-laying ship (SS *Great Eastern* for the 1866 cable — at 18,915 tons, the largest ship in the world at the time) at a controlled rate matching ship speed plus an allowance for cable catenary and bottom slope. The ship carried 2500+ nautical miles of cable wound on three large drums.
