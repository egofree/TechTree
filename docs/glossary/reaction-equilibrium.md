# Reaction equilibrium

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Na₂CO₃(aq) + Ca(OH)₂(s) ⇌ 2NaOH(aq) + CaCO₃(s). The equilibrium constant K = [NaOH]² / [Na₂CO₃]. At 80-90°C, conversion reaches 85-92% depending on initial concentrations. Higher temperature increases reaction rate but shifts equilibrium slightly toward reactants (the reaction is mildly exothermic). Typical operating conditions: 10-12% Na₂CO₃ feed solution, 80-90°C, 1-2 hours residence time with agitation.
