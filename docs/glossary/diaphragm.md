# Diaphragm

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: chemistry, gas-handling, health

Deposited asbestos fiber layer (1-3 mm thick) on the cathode mesh, or modern polymer membrane (perfluorinated sulfonic acid, 100-200 µm thick). The diaphragm allows Na⁺ ions and water to pass but retards back-migration of OH⁻ ions and prevents Cl₂ and H₂ from mixing. Asbestos diaphragm deposited by vacuum-drawing an asbestos slurry onto the cathode screen. Service life: 6-12 months before replating needed.

## Context in the Tech Tree

The diaphragm is a selective barrier that separates compartments in electrochemical cells, gas compressors, and medical devices. In the chlor-alkali process — the foundation of industrial chlorine and sodium hydroxide production — the diaphragm prevents explosive mixing of chlorine and hydrogen gases while allowing ionic current flow. In gas handling, diaphragm compressors and valves isolate the process gas from the drive mechanism, preventing oil contamination of high-purity gas streams.

## Technical Details

In the chlor-alkali diaphragm cell, the separator is critical to both safety and product quality. The asbestos fiber diaphragm is deposited by vacuum-drawing a slurry onto the perforated steel cathode mesh to a thickness of 1-3 mm. It allows Na⁺ ions and water to pass through but retards OH⁻ back-migration and prevents Cl₂ (anode) and H₂ (cathode) from mixing — which would form sodium hypochlorite (bleach) or, in worst case, explosive hydrogen-chlorine mixtures.

The diaphragm cell produces 10-12% NaOH contaminated with 15-17% unreacted NaCl, requiring triple-effect vacuum evaporation to reach 50% NaOH (NaCl crystallizes out during evaporation). Modern membrane cells using perfluorinated ion-exchange membranes produce 30-35% NaOH directly with minimal salt contamination — a significant advantage, but the membranes require mature fluoropolymer chemistry.

In gas handling, diaphragm valves use a flexible metal diaphragm to isolate the gas stream from the valve packing, eliminating contamination for ultra-pure gas service. Diaphragm compressors use a similar principle — the gas is separated from the crankcase by a metal diaphragm, achieving zero oil contamination at pressure ratios of 3-10 per stage.
