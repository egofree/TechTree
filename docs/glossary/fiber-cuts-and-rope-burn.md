# Fiber cuts and rope burn

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Handling rope causes friction burns (rope burn) on bare hands, especially when rope runs through hands under load. Wear gloves when handling rope under tension. Rope fibers can cause lacerations — particularly with old, frayed rope. Cut rope ends cleanly and seal (whipping, back splice) to prevent loose fibers.
