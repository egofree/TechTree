# Explosive limits and detection

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

LEL (lower explosive limit) 1.4% in air (14,000 ppm). UEL (upper explosive limit) near 100% in pure oxygen. The gap between the TLV (5 ppm) and the LEL (14,000 ppm) is large, but gas detection must trigger early because silane can accumulate rapidly from a leaking fitting. Flammable gas sensors (catalytic bead or infrared) set to alarm at 1% LEL (140 ppm) provide adequate warning time.
