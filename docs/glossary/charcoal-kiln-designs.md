# Charcoal kiln designs

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

(1) Earth kiln (traditional): stack wood in dome, cover with earth/sod, ignite at bottom, control air entry. Yield: 20-25%, cycle: 5-10 days. (2) Concrete block kiln (semi-portable): walls of stacked concrete blocks with air inlet ports. Yield: 25-30%, cycle: 2-5 days. (3) Retort (industrial): steel vessel heated externally — wood pyrolyzes without combustion air, pyrolysis gases burned to provide heat. Yield: 30-35%, cycle: 12-24 hours.
