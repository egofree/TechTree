# Compressive strength

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: ceramics, mining

(uniaxial): - Soft rock (coal, shale, evaporites): 10-50 MPa. Easy to drill with carbon steel bits at 0.5-1.0 m/hour (hand steel) or 10-30 m/hour (pneumatic). - Medium rock (sandstone, limestone): 50-150 MPa. Requires tungsten carbide bits. Penetration rates roughly half those in soft rock. - Hard rock (granite, basalt, gneiss): 150-300 MPa. Requires tungsten carbide or button bits at maximum feed pressure. Penetration rates 30-50% of soft rock rates.
