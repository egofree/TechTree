# Sorting

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: computing, energy, metals

The classification and separation of materials by physical properties such as density, composition, size, or atomic number. Sorting transforms mixed streams into homogeneous fractions suitable for processing or recycling.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Sorting appears in [Electromechanical Computing](../computing/electromechanical.md) for data classification, [Coal Processing](../energy/fuels/coal.md) for size and grade separation, and [Aluminum Production](../metals/aluminum.md) for scrap sorting and alloy separation. The progression from hand-sorting to automated methods mirrors the broader technological trajectory.

## Technical Details

Hand-sorting: workers pick visible waste rock from a conveyor belt at 1-3 tonnes/hour per person. Effective for coarse materials (>25 mm) with visible differences. The oldest method and still used where labor is cheap relative to equipment costs.

Density-based sorting: sink-float separation uses a heavy media suspension (magnetite or ferrosilicon in water) at controlled specific gravity. Light waste rock floats; dense ore sinks. Effective for coal (separates coal from shale at SG ~1.5) and iron ore (concentrates hematite at SG ~2.8).

X-ray transmission (XRT) sorting: X-rays pass through material on a conveyor; detector measures transmitted intensity, which correlates with atomic density. High-speed air jets deflect pieces into appropriate bins based on real-time XRT analysis. Throughput: 50-200 tonnes/hour.

Laser-induced breakdown spectroscopy (LIBS): a laser pulse ablates a tiny spot on each piece, and the emission spectrum identifies alloy composition (e.g., sorting 6061 from 7075 aluminum). Enables alloy-specific recycling but requires sophisticated optics and electronics.
