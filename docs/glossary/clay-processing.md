# Clay processing

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

For finer work, slake the clay (dissolve it completely in water to form a thin slip), then pour it through a coarse screen to remove pebbles and organic matter. Let the screened slip settle for several hours, then pour off the clear water from the top. The remaining sludge is clean, refined clay. Dry it on a plaster bat or absorbent stone until it reaches working consistency. This extra processing step removes impurities that cause weak spots in the fired pot.
