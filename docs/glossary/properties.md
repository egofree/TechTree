# Properties

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: foundational | **Domains**: polymers, ceramics, metals

Moderate oil resistance (inferior to NBR but adequate for intermittent contact). Excellent weather, ozone, and UV resistance (the chlorine atom provides inherent UV stability). Self-extinguishing — flame retardant without additives (LOI ~26%). In gutta-percha: volume resistivity >10¹⁵ Ω·cm, water absorption <0.2%, thermoplastic above 60-80°C.

## Technical Details

Material properties relevant to the tech tree span several categories. Mechanical properties (tensile strength, hardness, ductility, impact resistance) determine whether a material can bear loads, resist wear, or survive forming operations. Thermal properties (melting point, thermal conductivity, coefficient of thermal expansion) govern high-temperature applications and thermal cycling behavior. Chemical properties (corrosion resistance, solvent resistance, reactivity) determine compatibility with process environments.

Electrical properties are critical for insulator and conductor selection. Gutta-percha's volume resistivity (>10¹⁵ Ω·cm) exceeds natural rubber (~10¹³ Ω·cm) by two orders of magnitude — this is why gutta-percha, not rubber, insulated submarine telegraph cables. Its water absorption (<0.2% vs. ~1% for rubber) prevents the formation of conductive pathways through the insulation that would leak telegraph signals.

Neoprene's chlorine atom on the polymer backbone provides inherent UV stability — neoprene does not crack or degrade in outdoor exposure, unlike natural rubber which requires antioxidants and UV stabilizers. Its LOI (Limiting Oxygen Index) of ~26% means it will not sustain combustion in normal air (~21% oxygen), making it self-extinguishing without flame-retardant additives.
