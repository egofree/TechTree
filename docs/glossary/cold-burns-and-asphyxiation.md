# Cold burns and asphyxiation

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Direct skin contact with cryogenic liquids or uninsulated cryogenic piping causes frostbite (tissue damage identical to thermal burns). Cryogenic liquid splashes can cause eye damage. LOX and LIN both expand enormously on evaporation: in confined spaces, the nitrogen-rich vapor displaces oxygen, creating an asphyxiation hazard.
