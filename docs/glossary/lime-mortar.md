# Lime mortar

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

(pre-Portland, simplest): - **Manufacture**: Heat limestone (CaCO₃) to 900-1000°C in lime kiln → quicklime (CaO). Slake with water → slaked lime (Ca(OH)₂). Mix with sand at 1:2.5-3 (lime:sand by volume). - **Setting**: Carbonation — Ca(OH)₂ slowly absorbs CO₂ from air → CaCO₃. Very slow (weeks-months to full harden). Softer than Portland cement mortar — flexible, self-healing (microcracks heal as CO₂ continues carbonating). Ideal for historic restoration and soft stone masonry.
