# Cost per copy

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

A printed book costs approximately 1/10 to 1/100 of a hand-copied manuscript (accounting for labor, materials, and capital investment in type and press). The initial investment is significant (type for one font size: 50-100 kg of type metal, a press: 200-300 kg of timber and iron, paper: the largest material cost), but the per-copy cost decreases rapidly with volume.
