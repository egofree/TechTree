# Turning sails parallel to wind

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The oldest method of storm protection. In a post mill, the miller turns the entire mill body so the sail frame faces edge-on to the wind. In a tower mill, the cap is rotated to point the sails away from the wind. This must be done before the storm arrives. Once the wind is at storm force, manual rotation becomes impossible.
