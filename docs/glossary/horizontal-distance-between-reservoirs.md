# Horizontal distance between reservoirs

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Shorter is better. The ratio of head to horizontal distance (the "head-to-distance ratio") determines penstock length and cost. A site with 300 m head over 1 km horizontal distance (ratio 0.3) is far more economical than 300 m over 5 km (ratio 0.06). Steep terrain near river valleys or coastal cliffs offers the best ratios.
