# Culling decisions

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: animals

Remove animals that are aggressive, chronically ill, infertile, or consistently low-producing. Cull cows that fail to conceive after two breeding seasons. Ewes that lose lambs repeatedly or produce single lambs when the flock average is twins are candidates. Culling rate of 15-20% per year in a cattle herd is normal and maintains productivity.
