# Vacuum operation

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The condenser maintains 5-10 kPa absolute pressure (90-95% vacuum). This low exhaust pressure is what makes condensing turbines so much more efficient than non-condensing types. The pressure ratio across the turbine increases from perhaps 10:1 (exhaust to atmosphere) to 100:1 or more (exhaust to vacuum). Each 1 kPa reduction in exhaust pressure improves turbine output by roughly 0.5-1% of rated power.
