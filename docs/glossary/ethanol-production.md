# Ethanol production

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Ethanol production (C₂H₅OH) — - **Feedstocks**: Grain (barley, wheat, corn — starch → sugar via malt enzymes), sugar cane/beets (direct sugar), fruit (sugars), potatoes (starch). Starch must be converted to sugar first (malting/sprouting grain produces amylase enzymes that break starch into maltose). - **Malting**: Soak grain in water 2-3 days. Spread on floor 5-7 days, keep moist and 15-20°C. Grain sprouts, producing amylase. Dry at 50-60°C to stop germination (kilning). Crush malt to coarse grist. - **Mashing*...
