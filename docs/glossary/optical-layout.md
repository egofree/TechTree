# Optical layout

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: measurement

Entrance slit (10-100 μm, adjustable) → collimating mirror or lens (focal length 200-500 mm) → dispersing element (prism or grating) → focusing mirror or lens → detector plane. The collimator converts diverging light from the slit into a parallel beam. The dispersing element separates wavelengths angularly. The focusing optic forms discrete images of the slit at each wavelength on the detector.
