# Copper wire

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: transport

Superior conductor (resistivity 17 nΩ·m vs. 100 nΩ·m for iron). A 3 mm copper wire has resistance ~24 Ω/km, roughly one-third that of iron. But copper's tensile strength is only 200-300 MPa (annealed), so it sags more between poles. Hard-drawn copper (cold-worked during drawing) reaches 400-450 MPa but becomes brittle. Copper wire became practical once galvanizing or rubber insulation prevented corrosion at attachment points.
