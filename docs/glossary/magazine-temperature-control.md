# Magazine temperature control

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Store explosives below 30°C. Above 40°C, nitroglycerin-based dynamites sweat (NG exudes from the absorbent), creating highly sensitive liquid pools on the cartridge surface. Above 50°C, mercury fulminate blasting caps decompose. Ventilated, earth-bermed magazine buildings with passive cooling (shade, insulation) are standard. Temperature monitoring with maximum-minimum thermometers, logged daily.
