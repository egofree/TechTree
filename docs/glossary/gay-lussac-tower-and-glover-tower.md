# Gay-Lussac tower and Glover tower

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The lead chamber system evolved to include two absorption/recovery towers. The Gay-Lussac tower (cooling and absorption) captures NOx gases from the chamber exhaust in 50-60% H₂SO₄, forming nitrosylsulfuric acid (NOHSO₄). The Glover tower (denitration) releases these captured NOx back into the chamber system by heating with hot SO₂ gas. This NOx recovery reduced makeup sodium nitrate consumption from 5-10% to <1% of acid produced.
