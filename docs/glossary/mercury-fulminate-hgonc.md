# Mercury fulminate (Hg(ONC)₂)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The original primary explosive for blasting caps (Alfred Nobel, 1867). Prepared by dissolving mercury in excess nitric acid (forming Hg(NO₃)₂), then adding ethanol. The reaction produces gray-white crystalline precipitate of mercury fulminate. Sensitivity: slightly less impact-sensitive than lead azide but more friction-sensitive. Detonation velocity: 4,500 m/s.
