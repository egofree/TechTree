# Bayard-Alpert hot cathode ion gauge

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: gas-handling

Heated filament emits electrons (typically at 30-100 mA emission current). Electrons accelerated toward a grid, ionizing gas molecules. Ions collected on a thin central wire (collector). Ion current proportional to pressure. Range: 10⁻³-10⁻¹¹ mbar. Most accurate and widest-range vacuum gauge. Filament can burn out (replaceable tubes). Do not operate above 10⁻³ mbar (filament oxidizes and fails rapidly).
