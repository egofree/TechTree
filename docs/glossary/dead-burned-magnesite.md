# Dead-burned magnesite

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Raw magnesite (MgCO₃) is calcined at 1700-2000°C to produce dense, inert periclase (MgO) clinker. This "dead burning" causes grain growth and densification, reducing reactivity with moisture (MgO is mildly hygroscopic at lower calcination temperatures). The resulting grains are hard, dense (3.4-3.6 g/cm³), and chemically stable. Periclase melting point: 2852°C, the highest among common refractory oxides.
