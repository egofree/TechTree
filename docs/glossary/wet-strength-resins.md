# Wet strength resins

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Cationic polyamideamine-epichlorohydrin (PAE) resin — cross-links cellulose fibers via covalent bonds that resist water. Used in tissue paper, paper towels, liquid packaging board, and paper bags. Without wet strength resin, paper loses >90% of its tensile strength when saturated with water. With PAE: retains 20-40% of dry strength. Dosage: 0.5-3% on dry fiber.
