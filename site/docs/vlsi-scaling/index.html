VLSI Scaling & Advanced Semiconductor

Continuous improvement of wafer size (2" → 12"), feature size (10 μm → 7 nm), layer count (1-2 → 10+), and yield (10% → 90%+). Advanced lithography (DUV, eventually EUV), ion implantation, ALD, CMP planarization, copper damascene interconnects, EDA tools, and high-end solar optimization. The pinnacle requiring a mature, self-sustaining industrial ecosystem.

Capabilities

  • Advanced Lithography

    Projection steppers/scanners (i-line 365 nm → 0.35 μm), DUV excimer lasers (KrF 248 nm, ArF 193 nm with immersion → 0.07 μm), and EUV (13.5 nm from Sn plasma, reflective Mo/Si optics, high vacuum beam path). Chemically amplified resists for DUV. EUV is one of the last achievements — incredibly demanding.

  • Advanced Processes

    Ion implantation (precise doping control), RIE and DRIE etching (Bosch process), atomic layer deposition (ALD, Angstrom-level control), CMP for global planarization, copper damascene interconnects with low-k dielectrics, multi-level metallization, and advanced solar cells (PERC, bifacial, fine-line metallization).

  • Continuous Scaling

    Wafer size progression (2" → 4" → 6" → 8" → 12"), feature size reduction (10 μm → 3 μm → 1 μm → 0.35 μm → 0.13 μm → 7 nm), and metal layer progression (1-2 → 10+). Each generation doubles transistor count and requires better process uniformity, larger equipment, and higher yield.

  • EDA, GPU Design & Advanced Packaging

    Electronic Design Automation (logic synthesis, place and route, SPICE simulation, DRC), GPU architecture with billions of transistors, advanced packaging (wire bonding, flip-chip, chiplets, 3D stacking with TSVs), and software stack (drivers, compilers, libraries).
