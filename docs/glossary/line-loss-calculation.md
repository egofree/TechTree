# Line loss calculation

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Power loss in a three-phase transmission line: P_loss = 3 × I² × R, where I is line current and R is the resistance of one conductor. For a 50 km line with 100 mm² ACSR conductor (R = 0.28 Ω/km per conductor) carrying 10 MW at 33 kV: I = 10,000,000 / (√3 × 33,000) = 175 A. P_loss = 3 × 175² × 0.28 × 50 = 1.29 MW. That is a 12.9% loss, high but tolerable for early systems. At 110 kV with the same power: I = 52.5 A, P_loss = 116 kW (1.2%), far more efficient.
