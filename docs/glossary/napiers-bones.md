# Napier's bones

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: computing

Napier's bones (multiplication rods) — Construction**: Ten rectangular rods (numbered 0-9), each divided into nine squares. Each square shows the product of the rod's digit multiplied by the row number (1-9), with tens above a diagonal line and units below. An eleventh rod serves as the multiplier index. - **Operation**: Arrange rods side by side to form the multiplicand digits.
