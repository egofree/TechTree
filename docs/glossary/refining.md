# Refining

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Crude lead is refined by: - **Copper drossing**: Melt at 450-500°C, cool slowly. Copper separates as dross (skimmed off). Removes Cu to <0.005%. - **Softening**: Air or oxygen blown through molten lead at 600-700°C. As, Sb, Sn oxidize preferentially and skimmed as "speiss" and "matte." - **Parkes process (desilverization)**: Add zinc at 450-480°C.
