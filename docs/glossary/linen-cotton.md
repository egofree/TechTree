# Linen-cotton

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Linen-cotton: 55-65% linen, 35-45% cotton. Linen contributes strength and crispness; cotton softens the hand and reduces the tendency to wrinkle. The blend is easier to spin than pure linen (linen's long, stiff fibers are difficult to draft evenly). Card together on a fine drum carder. The resulting yarn weaves into a fabric that is lighter and cooler than pure linen but more durable than pure cotton. See: [Spinning](../textiles/spinning.md)
