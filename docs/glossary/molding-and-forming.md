# Molding and forming

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

For specific applications, the refined gutta-percha sheets are reheated to 80-100°C and pressed into molds, extruded through dies, or formed over mandrels. The thermoplastic nature allows complex shapes to be formed with simple tooling — wooden or metal molds are sufficient for most applications. After cooling, the gutta-percha hardens into a rigid, dimensionally stable part that retains its shape indefinitely at room temperature.
