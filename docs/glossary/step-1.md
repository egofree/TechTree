# Step 1

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: ceramics, chemistry, health

The first step of the START (Simple Triage and Rapid Treatment) mass-casualty triage protocol. Step 1 identifies the walking wounded by asking all ambulatory patients to move to a designated area, instantly clearing the scene and categorizing them as minor/delayed.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Step 1 appears in [Kiln Firing](../ceramics/pottery/kiln-firing.md) and [Alkalis](../chemistry/alkalis.md) accident response, [Ammonia](../chemistry/ammonia.md) emergency procedures, and [Medicine](../health/medicine.md) as part of systematic triage. Industrial accidents involving furnaces, chemicals, and explosions can produce mass casualties requiring rapid sorting.

## Technical Details

The protocol: ask everyone who can walk to move to a designated area. Those who comply are tagged **green (minor/delayed)**. This clears the immediate scene and identifies the walking wounded in seconds. The green-tagged patients are not critically injured — they can breathe, have adequate circulation, and are conscious enough to follow commands.

This step is deliberately simple because it requires no medical training and takes seconds. It reduces the remaining patient pool to those who cannot walk — the potentially serious and critical cases that demand immediate assessment in Step 2.

The walking wounded are directed to a collection point away from the incident scene, where they can be monitored and treated for minor injuries after critical patients are stabilized. Some green-tagged patients may have significant injuries that are non-life-threatening (fractures, lacerations) and will need treatment within hours rather than minutes.
