# Stoichiometric air for bituminous coal

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Approximately 7-9 kg of air per kg of coal (average ~8 kg air/kg coal). This assumes complete combustion of carbon to CO₂: C + O₂ → CO₂. The theoretical oxygen requirement for pure carbon is 2.67 kg O₂ per kg C; air is 23.2% oxygen by mass, so ~11.5 kg air per kg of pure carbon. Real coal contains moisture, ash, and hydrogen, modifying the requirement.
