# Safety

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: ceramics, chemistry, energy, machine-tools, metals, mining

Non-negotiable protective measures across all industrial processes in the tech tree. Eye protection, skin protection, ventilation, and hazard awareness prevent debilitating injuries from UV radiation, toxic fumes, molten materials, and mechanical forces.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Safety practices appear throughout the tech tree wherever hazardous materials or processes are involved. In [Joining](../machine-tools/joining.md), welding produces UV radiation that causes photokeratitis ("welder's flash") — a painful sunburn of the cornea. In [Steam Power](../energy/steam-power.md), boiler explosions from overpressure or low water level are catastrophic. In [Mining Extraction](../mining/extraction.md), roof collapses and gas inhalation are constant threats. In [Ammonia Production](../chemistry/ammonia.md), toxic gas leaks require ventilation and emergency protocols.

## Technical Details

Welding safety requires a helmet with shade #10-#14 filter lens, leather gloves and apron (no synthetic fabrics that melt onto skin), and adequate ventilation to remove metal fume. Arc UV burns exposed skin within minutes. Lime production demands eye and respiratory protection against caustic dust. Mining requires hard hats, rock bolting for roof support, and methane monitoring. Chemical processes need fume hoods, emergency eyewash stations, and grounded containers to prevent static discharge ignition.

The fundamental principle: every industrial process since the Bronze Age has inherent hazards. Protection is not optional — a disabled worker cannot bootstrap civilization.
