# Anode slime recovery

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry, metals

Slime accumulates at tank bottoms (0.2-0.8% of anode mass). Typical composition: 10-30% Ag, 0.5-2% Au, trace Pt, Pd, Se, Te. Slime is collected, roasted, leached, and electrolytically refined to recover precious metals — a critical economic co-product that often pays for the entire refining operation. A smelter processing 1 million tonnes of copper anode per year recovers ~3,000 tonnes of silver and ~30 tonnes of gold from anode slime.
