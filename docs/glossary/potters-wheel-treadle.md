# Potter's wheel (treadle)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

A heavy flywheel (30-50 cm diameter stone or wooden disc) connected by a spindle to a small throwing head at tabletop height. The potter kicks the flywheel with a foot treadle to maintain rotation while both hands shape the clay. This is the bridge between hand-built pottery and fully mechanized wheel-thrown production.
