# Flooding diagnosis

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Column flooding is indicated by a sudden, large increase in differential pressure across the column (normal ΔP: 0.3-1.0 kPa per tray, flooded: 2-5× normal). Causes: (1) excessive vapor flow (reboiler too hot), (2) excessive liquid flow (reflux too high for the vapor rate), (3) fouled trays or packing (debris, polymerized material, corrosion products), (4) downcomer blockage (prevents liquid from draining to the tray below).
