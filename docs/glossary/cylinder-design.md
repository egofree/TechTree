# Cylinder design

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: gas-handling

Acetylene cylinders are filled with a porous material (calcium silicate or diatomaceous earth) soaked with acetone. Acetylene dissolves in the acetone (acetone dissolves ~25× its own volume of acetylene at 15 bar). The porous matrix prevents any decomposition from propagating through the cylinder. Maximum withdrawal rate: 1/7 of cylinder contents per hour (faster withdrawal carries acetone out of the cylinder, depleting the stabilizer).
