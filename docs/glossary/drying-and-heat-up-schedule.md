# Drying and heat-up schedule

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

New refractory linings contain construction moisture and chemically bound water that must be driven off slowly. A typical heat-up schedule: 50°C/hour to 150°C, hold 12-24 hours (remove free water), 50°C/hour to 350°C, hold 6-12 hours, 75°C/hour to 600°C, hold 4-8 hours (remove chemically bound water from castables), then free heating to operating temperature. Too-rapid heat-up causes steam pressure to build within the refractory, resulting in spalling (explosive shedding of the hot face).
