# Upper column

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Upper column (at ~1 atm) — Rich liquid from the lower column feeds into the upper column through a Joule-Thomson valve (pressure drop provides additional refrigeration). At atmospheric pressure, the boiling points separate more widely: pure liquid oxygen collects at the bottom (bp -183°C) and nitrogen gas exits the top (bp -196°C).
