# Tower structure

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

A brick or stone tower 8-15 m tall, tapering from 4-8 m diameter at the base to 3-5 m at the cap curb (the circular track on which the cap rotates). Wall thickness: 0.3-0.6 m at the base, thinning to 0.2-0.4 m at the top. The tower must be plumb and the cap curb perfectly level and circular for smooth cap rotation. Brick towers used English bond or Flemish bond for structural strength. Stone towers used dressed ashlar or rubble masonry with lime mortar.
