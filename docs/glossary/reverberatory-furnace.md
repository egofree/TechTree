# Reverberatory furnace

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Reverberatory furnace (traditional, 1900s-1970s) — A long, shallow hearth furnace (30 m × 10 m) with a refractory roof. Coal, oil, or gas flame heats the roof, which radiates heat onto the charge. Concentrate + flux (silica sand) fed at one end; matte (Cu-Fe-S melt, 30-45% Cu) and slag (iron silicate) separate by density. Operating temperature: 1500-1600°C.
