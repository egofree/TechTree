# Damping and reduction

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

To lower temperature, reduce air supply rather than adding fuel. A charcoal fire with no bellows input drops below 700°C within minutes. Partially blocking the tuyere with a clay plug or stone gives fine temperature control. This is how potters hold a kiln at a specific temperature for hours: alternate brief bellows pulses with periods of reduced airflow.
