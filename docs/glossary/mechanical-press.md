# Mechanical press

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: machine-tools

(crank or eccentric drive): A flywheel stores rotational energy, and a crank mechanism converts it to a linear ram stroke. Tonnage range: 10-2,000 tons. Stroke rate: 30-600 strokes per minute for progressive die work. The crank press delivers peak force at bottom dead center; the eccentric gear press provides a more uniform force curve through the stroke. Mechanical presses suit blanking, piercing, bending, and shallow drawing where cycle speed is more important than full-stroke force control.
