# Hot repair techniques

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Some furnace repairs can be performed without cooling the furnace below operating temperature. Ceramic welding: a stream of refractory powder (silica or alumina) is projected into the damaged area while simultaneously injecting fuel gas (propane or natural gas) and oxygen. The combustion heat locally melts the refractory powder, fusing it to the hot surface. Used for coke oven wall repair at 1000-1200°C without shutting down the battery.
