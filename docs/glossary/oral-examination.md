# Oral examination

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

The candidate explains their reasoning to a panel of examiners, who probe understanding with follow-up questions. "Why did you use the thin-wall formula? At what diameter-to-thickness ratio does it become inaccurate? What failure mode does the safety factor protect against?" Oral exams test depth of understanding in ways written exams cannot — they reveal whether the candidate has memorized formulas or actually understands the physics.
