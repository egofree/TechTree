# Early-stage elements

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Before specialty alloys are available, **iron wire** can serve as a heating element in a reducing atmosphere (prevents oxidation) up to ~900°C. **Graphite rods** in a reducing or inert atmosphere extend the range considerably. **Charcoal or coke beds** with buried electrodes (resistive heating of the bed) can reach ~1200–1500°C without any metal element at all.
