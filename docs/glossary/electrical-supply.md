# Electrical supply

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Large DC or low-frequency AC current is passed through the electrode column via water-cooled copper bus bars clamped to the ends. Typical parameters: 1000-5000 A at 30-80 V initially, increasing to 50-150 V as the bed resistance changes during heating. Total power: 1000-5000 kW depending on furnace size. The electrodes themselves are the primary resistive load: as they heat, their resistance decreases (semiconductor behavior), requiring voltage adjustment to maintain power input.
