# Impulse staging

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

In a pure impulse stage, all the pressure drop occurs in the stationary nozzles. Steam exits the nozzle at high velocity (500-1200 m/s depending on stage conditions) and impinges on the moving blades. The moving blades experience no pressure drop, only a change in velocity direction. The Curtis (velocity-compounded) stage is used for the first stage in many turbines: one nozzle feeds two rows of moving blades separated by a row of fixed guide blades.
