# From forging to finished blade

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Turbine blades are among the most precisely manufactured metal components in any industry. The process starts with a forged steel blank (or an investment-cast wax pattern for complex airfoil shapes). The blank is rough-machined to within 0.5 mm of final dimensions, then finish-machined on multi-axis CNC equipment or, in earlier practice, on tracer-controlled milling machines following a master template.
