# Continuous glass fiber (structural)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: glass

Molten glass flows through a platinum bushing (a plate with 200-4000 holes, 0.8-2.0 mm diameter) under gravity. The emerging glass strands are attenuated (drawn) by a high-speed winder at 1,000-3,000 m/min, reducing the diameter to 5-24 μm. A sizing (thin coating of polymer + coupling agent) is applied immediately to protect the fiber surface from abrasion and improve bonding with resin matrices.
