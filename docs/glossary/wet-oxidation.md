# Wet oxidation

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: photolithography

Si + 2H₂O → SiO₂ at 900-1200°C. Water vapor introduced by passing O₂ through a heated water bubbler (95-98°C) or by burning H₂ and O₂ directly in the furnace tube (torch ignition, producing H₂O in situ). Growth rate: 5-25 nm/min, roughly 5-10× faster than dry oxidation. The resulting oxide contains more OH bonds (water-related defects), making it slightly lower quality but adequate for field oxide, masking oxide, and passivation layers.
