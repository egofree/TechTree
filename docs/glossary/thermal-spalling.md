# Thermal spalling

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Repeated heating and cooling cycles create thermal stress (σ = E × α × ΔT, where E = elastic modulus, α = thermal expansion coefficient, ΔT = temperature gradient). When stress exceeds the refractory's tensile strength (typically 5-15 MPa), cracks initiate and propagate, causing pieces to spall off the hot face.
