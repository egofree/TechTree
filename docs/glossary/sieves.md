# Sieves

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

woven with deliberate 2-5 mm gaps between weft strands for sifting flour, separating chaff from grain, or washing gravel to find small particles. The gap size is controlled by the spacing of the warp stakes during weaving. Multiple sieves with different gap sizes allow graded separation: coarse (5 mm) for removing chaff, medium (3 mm) for sifting bran from flour, fine (1-2 mm) for producing smooth flour.
