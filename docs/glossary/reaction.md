# Reaction

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: foundational | **Domains**: chemistry, energy, silicon

NH₃ + CO₂ + NaCl + H₂O → NaHCO₃↓ + NH₄Cl. Sodium bicarbonate precipitates because it has low solubility in concentrated brine at 15-20°C. Carbonation proceeds in two stages with total residence time of 1.5-3 hours. NaCl conversion efficiency: ~72-75%.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Chemical reactions are the transformative events that convert raw materials into useful products throughout the tech tree. In the [Solvay Process](../chemistry/solvay.md), the carbonation reaction precipitates sodium bicarbonate from brine. In [Hydrogen Production](../chemistry/hydrogen-silane.md), steam reforming (CH₄ + H₂O → CO + 3H₂) and the water-gas shift (CO + H₂O → CO₂ + H₂) convert fossil fuels to hydrogen. In [Silicon Production](../silicon/mg-si-production.md), the reduction reaction (SiO₂ + C → Si + CO₂) converts quartz to metallurgical-grade silicon at 1800-2000°C.

## Technical Details

Understanding chemical reactions in the tech tree requires attention to stoichiometry (mass balance), thermodynamics (is the reaction favorable?), and kinetics (how fast does it proceed?). The Solvay carbonation reaction illustrates all three: stoichiometrically, one mole of each reactant produces one mole each of sodium bicarbonate and ammonium chloride; thermodynamically, the reaction is favorable because NaHCO₃ has low solubility in cold concentrated brine (precipitation drives the equilibrium forward); kinetically, the reaction requires 1.5-3 hours of residence time in the carbonation tower, with two stages of CO₂ absorption at different concentrations.

Temperature control is critical for reaction selectivity. In the Solvay process, 15-20°C is optimal — higher temperatures increase NaHCO₃ solubility (reducing yield) while lower temperatures slow the reaction rate. In steam reforming, 700-1000°C is required to overcome the thermodynamic barrier (the reaction is strongly endothermic, ΔH = +206 kJ/mol). In silicon reduction, the furnace temperature of 1800-2000°C is well above the minimum thermodynamic requirement because kinetics demand high temperature for practical production rates.

Catalysis appears throughout: Cu catalyst for hydrochlorination (Si + 3HCl → SiHCl₃ + H₂), Ni catalyst for steam reforming, Fe₂O₃/Cr₂O₃ for the high-temperature water-gas shift. Catalysts lower activation energy without being consumed, enabling reactions to proceed at commercially useful rates at lower temperatures than would otherwise be required.
