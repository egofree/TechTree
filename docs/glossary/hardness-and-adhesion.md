# Hardness and adhesion

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Hard when dry (pencil hardness ~2H-3H on the standard graphite pencil scale — harder than most oil-based varnishes at ~HB, but softer than catalyzed lacquers at ~4H). Good adhesion to wood, metal, and many plastics. Can be applied over most other finishes without lifting (except polyurethane and some modern finishes — shellac solvents may attack them). The excellent adhesion results from shellac's polar hydroxyl and carboxyl groups forming hydrogen bonds with substrate surfaces.
