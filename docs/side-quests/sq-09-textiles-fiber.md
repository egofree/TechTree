# SQ 9: Textiles, Fiber &amp; Cordage

**Starts**: Phase 1  
**Priority**: Critical — rope, cloth, and belts are everywhere in pre-industrial infrastructure.

## Problem

Civilization runs on string. Rope hauls stone, cordage binds tools, cloth protects workers from molten metal and chemical splashes, canvas makes sails and tents, and drive belts transmit power from water wheels and steam engines. Without a textile supply chain, most mechanical systems have no way to transfer motion or contain materials.

## Technologies &amp; Systems

### Fiber Sources
- **Flax**: Retting, scutching, hackling for linen fiber
- **Hemp**: Strong cordage and canvas; minimal processing
- **Cotton**: Requires warmer climate; ginning, carding
- **Wool**: Shearing, washing, carding, fulling

### Spinning
- Drop spindle (Phase 1, portable, low skill entry)
- Spinning wheel (Phase 2-3, 10x throughput with treadle mechanism)
- Plying multiple strands for rope and heavy thread

### Weaving &amp; Cloth Production
- Simple frame loom to pit loom to treadle loom
- Tabby and twill weaves for different strengths
- Fulling, dyeing, and finishing treatments

### Rope-Making
- Hand-laid rope (twisting strands under tension)
- Rope walk for longer, consistent rope lengths
- Hawser-laid and cable-laid rope for different loads

### Industrial Applications
- Canvas and sailcloth for transport (SQ3) and shelter
- Drive belts for power transmission from water wheels and line shafts
- Filter cloth for chemical filtration (Phase 5)
- Protective clothing for foundry and chemical work
- Fireproofed textiles for furnace proximity
- Aircraft fabric covering: Grade A cotton or linen stretched over airframe, treated with nitrocellulose dope (Phase 5 / [SQ 12](sq-12-petrochemicals.md)) for weatherproofing and structural rigidity — see [SQ 13](sq-13-aircraft-development.md)

## Integration Points

| Phase | Contribution |
|-------|-------------|
| Phase 1 | Drop spindle, hand-laid rope, basic cloth |
| Phase 2 | Spinning wheel, treadle loom, canvas |
| Phase 3 | Drive belts for machine tools, precision rope |
| Phase 4 | Steam engine packing, boiler lagging, conveyor belting |
| Phase 5 | Filter media for acid/chemical production |
| Phase 6 | Cleanroom garments, dust-free wiping cloth |
| Phase 7 | Cleanroom suits, chemical-resistant protective gear |
| Phase 8 | Cleanroom-compatible woven and nonwoven fabrics |

## Key Deliverables

- Reliable fiber crop cultivation and processing pipeline
- Spinning capability from drop spindle to wheel
- Woven cloth at industrial scale (shelter, clothing, belting)
- Rope-making for construction and mechanical use
- Drive belts for water wheel and line shaft power transmission
- Filter cloth for chemical industry
- Protective garments for foundry and cleanroom work
