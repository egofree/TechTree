# Breakage

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Yarn snaps during spinning. Causes: (1) Yarn drawn too thin for the amount of twist, (2) fiber too dry or too short for the drafted diameter, (3) sudden tension spike from uneven take-up. Fix: increase twist slightly, use a larger draft (more fiber in the yarn cross-section), check that the tension mechanism is feeding smoothly. Wool benefits from 2-3% moisture (misting with a spray bottle) to improve fiber cohesion during spinning.
