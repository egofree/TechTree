# Lime sludge from causticization

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The CaCO₃ sludge from the lime-soda process can be reburned in the lime kiln at 900-1000°C to regenerate CaO (CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂), which is then slaked back to Ca(OH)₂ for reuse, closing the lime loop. This recycling reduces limestone consumption by 80-90% and eliminates the disposal problem for the largest waste stream in alkali production.
