Chemistry

Bulk mineral acids, alkalis, industrial gases, distillation infrastructure, cement production, air separation, specialty gases for semiconductor fabrication, petroleum alternatives and coal tar chemistry, lubricants and cutting fluids, and electrolytic processes. A civilization's industrial maturity can be measured by its sulfuric acid production. Provides the etchants, solvents, reagents, and purification infrastructure needed for semiconductor fabrication.

Capabilities

  • Air Separation & Bulk Gas Production

    Linde fractional distillation of liquid air producing nitrogen (99-99.99%), oxygen (95-99.5%), and argon (99-99.999%). Argon is critical for CZ crystal growth inert atmosphere. Double-column distillation at cryogenic temperatures with Joule-Thomson cooling.

  • Alkali Production

    Leblanc process (first synthetic soda ash) and Solvay process (more efficient, later) for Na₂CO₃, lime-soda process and chlor-alkali electrolysis for NaOH. Soda ash and caustic soda are essential for glass making, soap production, paper pulping, and chemical processing.

  • Cement & Concrete

    Portland cement from limestone + clay heated to 1450°C, ground with gypsum. Concrete (1:2:4 cement:sand:gravel mix), reinforced concrete with steel rebar, and curing protocols. Dominant building material for factory foundations, dams, roads, and vibration isolation pads.

  • Distillation

    Distillation columns (2-10 m, copper or steel, packed with ceramic Raschig rings), batch and continuous flow reactors, and shell-and-tube heat exchangers. Separates liquid mixtures by boiling point for chemical purification and petroleum refining.

  • Dopant & Etch Gases

    Dopant gases (PH₃, AsH₃, B₂H₆ — extremely toxic, lethal at ppm concentrations) and etch gases (CF₄, SF₆, Cl₂, F₂). Fluorine production by KF·2HF electrolysis. Exhaust gas abatement via thermal oxidation and wet scrubbing mandatory for all dopant gas use.

  • Electrolysis

    Electrolytic cells for chlor-alkali (NaCl → Cl₂ + NaOH), water splitting (H₂ + O₂), aluminum Hall-Héroult process, and copper refining. DC power at 2-5V per cell, industrial current 100-10,000+ A. Enables production of ultra-pure hydrogen, chlorine, and aluminum.

  • Explosives and Propellants

    Manufacture of explosive and propellant compounds: black powder (KNO₃/charcoal/sulfur), nitrocellulose/guncotton (cellulose + nitric acid), dynamite (nitroglycerin absorbed in diatomaceous earth), and smokeless powders. Enables efficient mining beyond hand tools, civil engineering (tunnels, canals), and military applications.

  • Hydrogen & Silane Production

    Hydrogen via electrolysis (4-5 kWh/Nm³) with Pd membrane purification, steam reforming of methane, and silane (SiH₄) production from MG-Si via trichlorosilane redistribution. Silane is pyrophoric — rigorous handling protocols required.

  • Lubricants, Oils & Fluid Mechanics

    Animal fats and vegetable oils, lubrication regimes (boundary, hydrodynamic, EHL), cutting fluids for machining, grease production (calcium, sodium, lithium soap), bearing lubrication methods, hydraulic fluids, vacuum oils (mineral, silicone), and cleanroom-compatible lubricants. Without lubrication, every moving part seizes.

  • Mineral Acid Production

    Sulfuric acid (lead chamber process 65-70%, contact process 96-98%), nitric acid (from nitrates + H₂SO₄, later Ostwald process), hydrochloric acid (NaCl + H₂SO₄ or direct H₂ + Cl₂ synthesis), and hydrofluoric acid (CaF₂ + H₂SO₄, extremely hazardous). HF is uniquely dangerous and essential for glass and silicon etching.

  • Petroleum & Alternative Chemistry

    Petroleum extraction (surface seeps → cable-tool → rotary drilling), refining (batch distillation → fractional distillation → thermal cracking), coal tar chemistry (benzene, phenol, naphthalene from coke ovens), fermentation (ethanol, acetone, acetic acid, methanol), and wood gasification. Dual-path strategy: petroleum accelerates everything, but coal tar + fermentation covers the same molecules.

  • Semiconductor Packaging & Testing

    Wafer backgrinding, die singulation (diamond blade dicing), die attach (eutectic or epoxy), wire bonding (Au ball bonding, Al wedge), encapsulation (transfer molding with epoxy-silica compound), lead forming, wafer probing (DC parametric + functional), burn-in, and final test.
