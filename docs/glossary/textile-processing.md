# Textile processing

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Scouring: cotton fibers treated with 1-3% NaOH at 80-100°C to remove natural waxes, pectins, and impurities before dyeing. Mercerization: cotton fabric treated with 20-25% NaOH at 15-20°C under tension. The alkali swells the cotton fibers, converting cellulose I to cellulose II crystal structure, increasing dye uptake 20-30%, adding silk-like luster, and improving tensile strength 10-20%. The fabric must be held under tension during treatment and rinsing to prevent shrinkage.
