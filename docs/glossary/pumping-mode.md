# Pumping mode

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

(charging): - Surplus electrical power drives a pump-motor unit. Water is drawn from the lower reservoir and pumped uphill to the upper reservoir. The pump adds gravitational potential energy to the water: E = ρ × g × h × V, where ρ = water density (1000 kg/m³), g = 9.81 m/s², h = elevation difference between reservoir surfaces, V = volume pumped. - Pumping is typically done during off-peak hours (night) when base-load generators (steam, nuclear) have surplus capacity.
