# Diesel fuel injection timing

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Injection begins 5-15° before TDC. Timing is critical: too early causes excessive peak pressure and mechanical stress; too late reduces efficiency and raises exhaust temperature. At 1500 RPM, 40° of crank rotation takes roughly 4.4 milliseconds. Modern common-rail systems adjust timing electronically across the operating range for optimal emissions and efficiency at each load point.
