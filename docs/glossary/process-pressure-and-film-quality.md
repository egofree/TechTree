# Process pressure and film quality

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: optics

At base pressures of 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁵ Torr, the mean free path of evaporated atoms exceeds the source-to-substrate distance (typically 25-40 cm), so atoms travel in straight lines without gas-phase collisions. If pressure is too high (10⁻³ Torr or worse), evaporated atoms scatter off residual gas molecules, producing a frosty, porous film with columnar microstructure.
