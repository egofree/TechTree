# Shunt hazards

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: measurement

Current shunts carrying 100+ A produce significant heating. A 100 A shunt at 75 mV dissipates 7.5 W, reaching 60-80°C surface temperature. At 1000 A (large industrial shunts), dissipation is 75 W, requiring heatsinking or forced air cooling. Burns from touching an energized shunt are a real risk. Install shunts in enclosures with ventilation openings, and label with maximum current rating.
