# Paper deacidification

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

Acidic paper (groundwood pulp, alum-rosin sizing, common 1850-1990) self-destructs as acid catalyzes hydrolysis of cellulose chains. Paper becomes brittle and brown, losing 50-90% of fold endurance within 50-100 years. Deacidification treatments: spray or immerse in alkaline buffer solution. Magnesium bicarbonate (Mg(HCO₃)₂) solution applied by spraying deposits magnesium carbonate reserve (pH 8-9) that neutralizes existing acid and provides alkaline buffer against future acid formation.
