# H-series

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

(e.g., H13): 0.4% C, 5% Cr, 1.5% Mo, 1.0% V. Designed for tools in contact with hot workpieces (>500°C). Must resist softening, thermal fatigue (cyclic heating/cooling causes surface cracking — "heat checking"), and erosion by molten metal. H13 is the dominant grade for die-casting dies, forging dies, extrusion tooling. Tempered at 540-650°C to produce a martensite matrix with fine alloy carbide dispersion. Service hardness: HRC 42-52 (lower than cold-work grades, but maintained at 500-600°C).
