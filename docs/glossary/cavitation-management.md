# Cavitation management

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

At low heads, the runner operates close to the tailwater level, and the pressure drop across the blades can easily fall below the vapor pressure of water. Kaplan runners are typically installed with the blade centerline 2-5 m below the minimum tailwater level (submergence). This hydrostatic pressure head suppresses cavitation. The required submergence increases with specific speed. Stainless steel blade surfaces resist cavitation pitting better than carbon steel.
