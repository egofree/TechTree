# Anaphylaxis risk

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: health

Latex gloves cause allergic reactions in 1-6% of the general population and 8-12% of healthcare workers with repeated exposure. Latex allergy ranges from contact dermatitis to anaphylactic shock. Use nitrile or vinyl gloves as the default; reserve latex for procedures requiring its superior tactile sensitivity and barrier properties. Any facility using latex must have epinephrine (1:1000, 0.3-0.5 mg intramuscular) immediately available for anaphylaxis treatment.
