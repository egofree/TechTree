# Ventilation

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: animals, energy, glass, health, machine-tools, transport

Local exhaust ventilation (LEV) captures contaminants at the source before they reach the breathing zone. Key design parameters: capture velocity (0.5-1.0 m/s for gases and vapors, 1.0-2.5 m/s for dusts, 2.5-10 m/s for heavy particles from grinding), hood design, and duct velocity (minimum 10 m/s to prevent dust settling).

## Context in the Tech Tree

Ventilation — the supply of fresh air and removal of contaminated air from workspaces — is a fundamental engineering control for occupational health. In [Occupational Health](../health/occupational-health.md), ventilation is the primary engineering control after elimination and substitution in the hierarchy of controls. In [Coal](../energy/coal.md) mining, ventilation prevents buildup of methane (explosive at 5-15% in air) and coal dust. In [Advanced Glassblowing](../glass/advanced-glassblowing.md), ventilation removes furnace combustion products and glass fume. In [Casting](../machine-tools/casting.md) and [Joining](../machine-tools/joining.md), ventilation captures metal fumes and welding smoke.

## Technical Details

Ventilation design follows two approaches: local exhaust ventilation (LEV) and general dilution ventilation. LEV is far more effective per unit of air moved — capturing contaminants at their source before they disperse into the workspace.

Capture velocity requirements depend on the contaminant: 0.5-1.0 m/s for gases and vapors released with little momentum (evaporation from open tanks); 1.0-2.5 m/s for dusts generated by low-velocity processes (mixing, pouring); and 2.5-10 m/s for heavy particle ejection (grinding, chipping). Hood design progresses from enclosing hoods (most effective — surrounds the source) to receiving hoods (catches contaminants projected toward the hood) to capturing hoods (must pull contaminants from a distance — least effective).

Duct velocity must exceed 10 m/s horizontally to prevent dust settling in the ductwork. For gas-only systems, 5-8 m/s is acceptable. Fan sizing accounts for hood capture requirements, duct friction losses, and filter pressure drops.

General dilution ventilation provides 4-12 air changes per hour for comfortable occupancy but is inadequate for toxic substance control — it may reduce concentrations to acceptable levels for mild hazards but cannot provide reliable protection against toxic gases.
