Measurement & Instrumentation

Precision measurement, instrumentation, and calibration systems. Spans temperature and pressure sensing, dimensional metrology via the Whitworth three-plate method, gauge blocks and micrometers, electrical standards and calibration chains. You cannot build what you cannot measure — this domain provides the quantitative foundation for all precision manufacturing.

Capabilities

  • Precision Metrology & Standards

    Surface plates via Whitworth three-plate method, hand scraping, straight edges, squares, vernier calipers (0.02 mm), micrometers (0.01 mm), gauge blocks (Jo-blocks), go/no-go gauges, and thread standards. Includes base unit standards, timekeeping, calibration infrastructure, electrical standards, and optical metrology. You cannot build what you cannot measure.

  • Temperature & Pressure Measurement

    Thermocouples, RTDs, pressure gauges, and electrical measurement instruments (galvanometers, Wheatstone bridges). Provides the thermal and pressure sensing needed for furnace control, crystal growth, and process monitoring.
