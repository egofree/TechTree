# Rubber & Elastomers

> **Node ID**: polymers.rubber
> **Domain**: Polymers & Composites
> **Timeline**: Years 5-50
> **Outputs**: rubber, elastomers, gaskets, seals

Elastomers are polymers that recover shape after large deformation. They fill roles no rigid material can: flexible seals, vibration dampers, tires, hoses, chemical-resistant gloves, electrical insulation, and adhesives. The rubber family divides into two branches with different feedstocks and property profiles.

### [Natural Rubber & Vulcanization](./natural.md)

Latex from rubber trees (or guayule and dandelion in temperate climates), coagulated, masticated, compounded with sulfur and fillers, then cured under heat and pressure. Cured properties: tensile strength 17-28 MPa, elongation 400-650%, Shore A hardness 40-90, service range -50 to +100°C. Covers tapping, coagulation, compounding recipes, vulcanization methods, and safety hazards (latex allergy, dust explosion risk, chemical sensitizers).

### [Synthetic Polymers & Elastomers](./synthetic.md)

Monomers from petroleum cracking or fermentation, polymerized under controlled conditions. Covers four major families: nitrile rubber (NBR, oil and fuel resistance), neoprene (flame retardant, weather resistant), silicone elastomers (service range -60 to +250°C, excellent dielectric properties), and polyurethane (rigid foam insulation, cast elastomers). Each uses different monomer chains, polymerization methods, and cure systems.

### Shared Infrastructure

Vulcanization equipment (two-roll mills, Banbury internal mixers, hydraulic presses, steam autoclaves) and the general principle of compounding with sulfur, accelerators, zinc oxide, and reinforcing fillers are common to both branches. See [Natural Rubber](./natural.md) for compounding and curing details.
