# M-series

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

(Molybdenum HSS, e.g., M2): 0.85% C, 4% Cr, 5% Mo, 6% W, 2% V. The dominant cutting tool steel. Red-hardness to ~600°C — retains HRC 60+ at cutting edge temperatures that would soften plain carbon steel. The mechanism: secondary hardening during tempering. When tempered at 540-560°C, fine VC and Mo₂C precipitates form, replacing the hardness lost during the initial temper.
