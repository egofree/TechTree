# Cooking

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: plants

Boiling is the most versatile method — a clay pot over fire cooks roots, greens, grains, and legumes. Roasting (direct contact with embers or hot stones) works well for roots, tubers, and some seeds. Baking in earth ovens (pit lined with heated stones, covered with leaves and earth) cooks large quantities slowly.
