# Reaction staging (Parsons type)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

In a 50% reaction stage (the most common Parsons design), half the pressure drop occurs in the fixed blades (stator) and half in the moving blades (rotor). The rotor blades are shaped as nozzles themselves, accelerating the steam and creating a reaction force (like a rocket nozzle). This means the rotor experiences a net pressure force in addition to the impulse from velocity change.
