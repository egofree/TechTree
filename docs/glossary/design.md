# Design

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: ceramics, computing, energy, gas-handling, health, silicon, transport

Aircraft design (tube-and-fabric ultralight): Wingspan 8-12 m. Wing area 12-20 m². Wing loading 20-40 kg/m². Empty weight 150-250 kg. Gross weight 250-400 kg. Stall speed 35-50 km/h. Cruise speed 70-120 km/h. Range 100-300 km.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Design is the planning phase that translates requirements into buildable specifications. Across the tech tree, design decisions constrain everything that follows — from kiln chimney height (governing draft pressure and maximum temperature) to aircraft wing loading (governing stall speed and structural weight). Good design accounts for available materials, manufacturing precision, and the gap between theoretical performance and practical build quality.

## Technical Details

Kiln design determines whether a ceramic firing reaches 900°C or 1300°C. The downdraft kiln achieves higher temperatures through its gas flow path: hot gases rise to the crown, then deflect downward through the ware to floor-level flues. Chimney draft pressure depends on height and temperature differential — practical rule: 3-5 m chimney for temperatures up to 1000°C, 5-8 m for 1200°C+. The cross-section should be 1/15 to 1/20 of kiln floor area.

Vacuum chamber design follows different principles: cylindrical or spherical shapes distribute stress uniformly, viewports use glass windows with CF or ISO flanges, and internal surface area is minimized to reduce outgassing. Material selection (304L or 316L stainless steel) and electropolished interior surfaces directly determine achievable base pressure.

Aircraft design trades wing loading against stall speed, structural weight against payload, and engine power against fuel consumption. High-wing configurations offer better ground clearance and simpler strut bracing; low-wing designs provide shorter landing gear and better crash protection. The tube-and-fabric ultralight represents the earliest achievable powered aircraft with moderate machining capability.

Sanitation system design must ensure adequate flow velocities (>0.6 m/s in sewers to prevent solids deposition), appropriate pipe gradients, and access points for maintenance.
