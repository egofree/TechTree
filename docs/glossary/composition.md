# Composition

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: ceramics, chemistry, energy, glass, machine-tools, metals, mining, photolithography, plants, polymers

Menthol content 30-50% of the oil, with menthone (14-32%), menthyl acetate (2-8%), and 1,8-cineole (3-6%) as secondary constituents. The menthol provides the characteristic cooling sensation (activates TRPM8 cold receptors) and analgesic properties.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Composition — the chemical makeup of a material or mixture — determines its properties and suitability for applications. In [Alloys](../metals/alloys.md), composition (percentages of chromium, nickel, carbon, etc.) defines stainless steel grades. In [Glass](../glass/advanced.md), composition (silica, soda, lime ratios) determines thermal expansion and chemical resistance. In [Iron & Steel](../metals/iron-steel.md), composition controls the iron-carbon phase diagram. In [Medicinal Plants](../plants/medicinal-plants.md), composition determines therapeutic potency. In [Blast Furnace](../metals/iron-steel/blast-furnace.md) operation, slag and metal composition must be monitored continuously.

## Technical Details

Peppermint oil composition illustrates how component ratios determine function: menthol (30-50%) provides the characteristic cooling sensation by activating TRPM8 cold receptors in skin and mucous membranes, along with analgesic properties. Menthone (14-32%) contributes aroma. The balance of these constituents varies with growing conditions, harvest timing, and distillation parameters.

In metallurgy, composition is measured as weight percentages of elements. Stainless steel 304 is 18% Cr, 8% Ni, ≤0.08% C — each element serving a specific purpose (chromium for corrosion resistance, nickel for austenite stability, carbon kept low to prevent sensitization). Deviations of ±0.5% from specification can shift the material from one performance category to another.

In glass production, the three-family system — soda-lime (70% SiO₂, 15% Na₂O, 10% CaO), lead (55-62% SiO₂, 24-35% PbO), and borosilicate (80% SiO₂, 13% B₂O₃) — demonstrates how compositional differences produce radically different materials from the same basic ingredients. Batch ingredients must be weighed to ±2% accuracy to achieve target glass properties.

Analytical methods for composition determination progress from simple tests (color, density, spark test for steel) through wet chemistry (titration, gravimetric analysis) to instrumental methods (spectroscopy, XRF) as the tech tree advances.
