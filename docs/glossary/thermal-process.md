# Thermal process

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

(pure-grade, 15% of production): - Burn elemental white phosphorus (P₄) in excess air → P₄O₁₀ (phosphorus pentoxide). P₄O₁₀ + 6H₂O → 4H₃PO₄. - Produces very pure acid suitable for food additives, pharmaceuticals, and semiconductor etching. White phosphorus is produced by reducing phosphate rock with coke in an electric arc furnace at 1500°C: Ca₃(PO₄)₂ + 3SiO₂ + 5C → 3CaSiO₃ + 5CO + 2P₄. The P₄ vapor condenses under water (pyrophoric — ignites in air).
