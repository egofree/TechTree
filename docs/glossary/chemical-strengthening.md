# Chemical strengthening

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: glass

An alternative to thermal tempering for thin glass (1-3 mm). Immerse the glass in a bath of molten potassium nitrate (KNO₃) at 400-450°C for 4-16 hours. Large K⁺ ions (0.133 nm radius) exchange with smaller Na⁺ ions (0.095 nm) in the glass surface. The larger potassium ions crowd the surface layer, placing it in compression.
