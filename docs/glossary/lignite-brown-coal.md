# Lignite (brown coal)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy, foundations

Brown to blackish-brown color, often visible wood grain texture. Crumbles easily between fingers. Moisture content is 30-50% by weight, which means it feels light for its size and hisses when burned as water boils off. Energy density: 15-25 MJ/kg. It burns with a smoky, low-temperature flame. Common near the surface in sedimentary basins. Useful as a fuel where nothing better is available, but inferior for forging or smelting.
