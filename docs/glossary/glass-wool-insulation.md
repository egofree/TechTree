# Glass wool (insulation)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: glass

Molten glass flows through a spinner (rotating disk with thousands of small holes, 0.5-1 mm diameter, spinning at 2,000-4,000 RPM). Centrifugal force extrudes the glass into fine fibers 5-15 μm diameter, 5-15 cm long. A binder (phenolic resin, 5-10% by weight) is sprayed onto the fibers as they form. The fibrous mat is cured at 200°C and cut to size. Thermal conductivity: 0.03-0.04 W/(m·K) — excellent insulation. Density: 10-50 kg/m³.
