# Double-column thermodynamics

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The lower column operates at 5-6 bar and the upper column at 1.2-1.5 bar. The reboiler at the bottom of the upper column is thermally coupled to the condenser at the top of the lower column: condensing nitrogen vapor from the lower column (at ~-177°C at 5-6 bar) provides heat to boil liquid oxygen at the bottom of the upper column (at ~-183°C at 1.2 bar).
