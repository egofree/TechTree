# Chemistry

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: ceramics, chemistry, energy, photolithography, polymers, transport

HNO₃ oxidizes HCl to generate chlorine (Cl₂) and nitrosyl chloride (NOCl) in situ. These reactive species attack gold: Au + 3Cl₂ → AuCl₃ (soluble chloroauric acid H[AuCl₄]). Platinum dissolves similarly as H₂[PtCl₆]. The combination works because nitric acid provides the oxidizing power while chloride ions complex the dissolved metal ions, shifting equilibrium toward dissolution.

## Technical Details

Chemistry in the bootstrap context spans inorganic, organic, and electrochemical reactions. Aqua regia illustrates the principle: nitric acid (HNO₃) oxidizes hydrochloric acid (HCl) to generate chlorine and nitrosyl chloride, which together dissolve gold and platinum by forming stable chloro-complexes. The chemistry works because two reagents cooperate — nitric acid provides oxidizing power while chloride ions sequester the dissolved metal ions as soluble complexes, shifting the equilibrium toward dissolution.

The same pattern of cooperative chemistry appears throughout the tech tree. In the Leblanc process, sulfuric acid reacts with salt to produce HCl gas and sodium sulfate, which then reacts with limestone and carbon to yield soda ash. In the Solvay process, ammonia acts as a recyclable intermediate that enables sodium bicarbonate precipitation from brine. In steelmaking, dissolved oxygen oxidizes carbon, silicon, and phosphorus, converting impurities to gases (CO) or slag components.

In semiconductor processing, chemistry becomes precise to the atomic level. Thermal oxidation (Si + O₂ → SiO₂) grows gate dielectrics. Wet etching (SiO₂ + 6HF → H₂SiF₆ + 2H₂O) removes oxide layers. Dopant diffusion follows Fick's law. The equations may differ in scale, but the underlying principles — equilibrium, kinetics, thermodynamics — are universal.
