# Water aspirator

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

The simplest vacuum source. Water flows through a Venturi nozzle, creating suction by the Bernoulli effect. Achieves 15-25 mmHg (limited by water vapor pressure at the water temperature: ~24 mmHg at 25°C). Sufficient for reducing boiling points by 50-100°C. No moving parts, inexpensive. Disadvantage: consumes large volumes of water (5-20 L/min) and cannot achieve deep vacuum.
