# Fillers and coating pigments

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Precipitated calcium carbonate (PCC) or ground calcium carbonate (GCC) — added to paper to improve opacity, brightness, smoothness, and printability while reducing fiber cost (CaCO₃ cheaper than fiber). PCC produced on-site by calcining limestone (CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂), slaking (CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂), then precipitating CaCO₃ by bubbling CO₂ through the lime milk — crystal morphology controlled (scalenohedral, rhombohedral, or needle-like aragonite) for optimal light scattering.
