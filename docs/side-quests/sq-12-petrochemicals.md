# SQ 12: Petroleum &amp; Alternative Chemistry

**Starts**: Phase 2  
**Priority**: Medium — massive accelerator if petroleum is available, but coal tar and fermentation cover most of the same ground.

## Problem

Organic chemistry feedstocks fuel the entire materials chain: solvents for semiconductor processing, polymers for packaging and cleanrooms, fuels for transport and furnaces. Without them, the chemical industry stalls at inorganic bulk chemicals. The good news is that there are two independent paths to these molecules. Petroleum is the fast lane; coal tar and fermentation are the reliable fallback.

## Technologies &amp; Systems

### Petroleum Extraction &amp; Refining
- **Surface seeps** → hand-dug pits → **cable-tool drilling** (percussion, simple steel cable)
- **Rotary drilling** once steel tubes and mud pumps are available
- **Fractional distillation**: crude → gasoline, kerosene, diesel, lubricating oil, fuel oil, asphalt/bitumen
- Provides alkanes, cycloalkanes, aromatics in massive volume

### Coal Tar Chemistry
- Coal → coke ovens → coal tar byproduct
- Distillation fractions: **benzene, toluene, xylene** (light oil), **phenol, cresols** (carbolic oil), **naphthalene** (middle oil), **creosote** (heavy oil), **pitch** (residue)
- This was the historical source of organic chemicals before petroleum
- Requires coke production (already needed for blast furnaces)

### Fermentation Chemistry
- **Ethanol** from grain/sugar fermentation → solvent, fuel, feedstock
- **Acetone-butanol** fermentation (Weizmann process) → solvents and chemical intermediates
- **Acetic acid** from ethanol oxidation → vinegar, cellulose acetate, solvents
- Works at any scale with basic vessels and temperature control

### Wood Gasification &amp; Pyrolysis
- **Producer gas** (CO + H₂ + N₂) from partial combustion in air → engine fuel, heating
- **Charcoal gas** (CO + H₂ + CH₄) from retort pyrolysis → cleaner fuel gas
- **Wood tar** and **wood vinegar** (methanol, acetone, acetic acid) as byproducts
- Works anywhere trees grow; no mining required

### Synthetic Polymers
- From petroleum aromatics or coal tar: **phenol-formaldehyde** (Bakelite), **nylon precursors**
- From fermentation ethanol: **celluloid**, **ethyl acetate**, **acetic anhydride**
- From wood chemistry: **cellulose derivatives**, **lignin-based resins**
- Early polymers are good enough for gaskets, insulation, packaging, coatings
- Aircraft fuels and dopes: gasoline distillation fraction for piston engines; acetone for nitrocellulose dope production — see [SQ 13](sq-13-aircraft-development.md)

### The Dual Path Principle
- **Petroleum available**: everything accelerates. Cheap solvents, fuels, monomers, polymers. Decades saved.
- **No petroleum**: coal tar + fermentation + wood chemistry produces the same core molecules (benzene, phenol, ethanol, acetone, methanol, acetic acid). Slower, smaller scale, more labor, but entirely viable.
- Plan for both. Do not assume petroleum.

## Key Deliverables

- Coke oven operation with coal tar collection
- Fractional distillation capability (crude oil or coal tar)
- Fermentation-derived solvents (ethanol, acetone, acetic acid)
- Producer gas / wood gas for engines and heating
- Early synthetic polymers (Bakelite, celluloid, cellulose derivatives)
- Dual-path feedstock strategy documented
