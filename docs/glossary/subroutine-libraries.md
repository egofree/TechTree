# Subroutine libraries

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: computing

Common mathematical functions (sine, cosine, logarithm, square root) are coded as subroutines and stored on paper tape or magnetic drum. The programmer calls a subroutine by inserting a jump instruction at the appropriate point in the main program. The subroutine executes, stores its result, and jumps back to the instruction following the call. Reusable subroutines are the earliest form of software reuse.
