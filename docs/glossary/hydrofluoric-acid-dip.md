# Hydrofluoric acid dip

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: silicon

(optional, removes oxide): - A brief dip (15-60 seconds) in dilute HF (1-5% by weight) removes the native oxide layer (SiO₂, ~1-2 nm thick) that grows naturally on silicon in air at room temperature. The HF etches SiO₂ selectively (etch rate ~100 nm/min for thermal oxide at 5% HF) but does not attack silicon. - After HF dip, the surface is hydrogen-terminated (Si-H bonds instead of Si-O).
