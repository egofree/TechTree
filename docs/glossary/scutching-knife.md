# Scutching knife

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Flat wooden blade, 30-40 cm long, 8-12 cm wide, tapered to a blunt edge (not sharp). Hardwood — beech, maple, or oak. Handle at one end. Used to beat broken flax stalks against a vertical scutching board (wooden plank mounted at a slight angle, ~60 cm wide × 100 cm tall). Swing the knife edge against the hanging stalks to knock away broken shives.
