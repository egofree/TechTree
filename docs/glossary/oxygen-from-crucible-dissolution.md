# Oxygen from crucible dissolution

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: silicon

Incorporation of oxygen into molten silicon during Czochralski growth from the dissolution of the fused silica (SiO₂) crucible at ~1500°C. Results in interstitial oxygen concentrations of 5–20 × 10¹⁷ atoms/cm³ in the crystal.
