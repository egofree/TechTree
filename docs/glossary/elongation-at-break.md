# Elongation at break

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Nylon rope elongates 15-25% at breaking load, absorbing significant energy before failure (advantage for dynamic loads and shock absorption). Manila elongates only 8-12%, making it stiffer but less forgiving of sudden loads. This difference matters critically for lifting applications: nylon's stretch provides a safety buffer under shock loading, while Manila's lower stretch gives better positional control for rigging where stretch is undesirable.
