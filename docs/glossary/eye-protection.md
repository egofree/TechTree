# Eye Protection

Eye protection encompasses the specialized safety equipment required to shield the eyes from hazards specific to each industrial process. In glassblowing, the sodium flare from heating soda-lime glass emits intense yellow light (sodium D-line, 589 nm) that masks the true glass temperature and causes eye strain. Didymium glass lenses—containing neodymium-praseodymium oxide—selectively absorb the sodium emission, revealing the true orange-red color of the heated glass. Didymium lenses must always be worn when lampworking soda-lime or lead glass.

For borosilicate glasswork, standard safety glasses suffice since there is no sodium flare. For quartz work with oxy-hydrogen torches, welding-grade lenses (shade 5–8) are required because of the intense UV and IR radiation. In metalworking, grinding and cutting operations produce flying particles that require impact-rated safety glasses or face shields. Chemical handling requires splash-proof goggles, especially when working with acids, alkalis, or solvents.

Eye protection selection depends on matching the hazard to the appropriate lens type and impact rating. A bootstrap industrial operation must prioritize eye protection because eye injuries are immediately debilitating and often permanent. Multiple types of protection are needed as processes diversify.
