# Caustic soda production

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

(bootstrap path): - Sodium hydroxide is produced by the chlor-alkali process (electrolysis of brine: 2NaCl + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂ + Cl₂). This requires electricity and a membrane or diaphragm cell. - Lower-tech alternative: lime causticization. Sodium carbonate (Na₂CO₃, from the Solvay process) is reacted with calcium hydroxide (slaked lime): Na₂CO₃ + Ca(OH)₂ → 2NaOH + CaCO₃. The calcium carbonate precipitates, leaving NaOH solution.
