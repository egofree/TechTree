# Construction differences from EAF

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: energy

Structural distinctions between the induction furnace and the electric arc furnace (EAF): induction furnaces use a water-cooled copper coil surrounding a refractory crucible (no electrodes), while EAFs use graphite electrodes striking arcs onto the charge. Induction furnaces are smaller, cleaner, and suited for batch melting of known compositions.
