# CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ + 65 kJ/mol

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: ceramics

The slaking reaction: calcium oxide (quicklime) reacts vigorously with water to form calcium hydroxide (slaked lime), releasing 65 kJ/mol of heat. The resulting putty is used for mortar, plaster, and whitewash after aging.
