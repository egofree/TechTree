# Tank sizing for dry season

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

Calculate storage to bridge the longest expected dry period. For a household of 5 at 50 liters per person per day (conservation mode) through a 4-month dry season: 5 × 50 × 120 days = 30,000 liters (30 m³). Add 25% margin for evaporation and seepage losses: 37.5 m³. A single cistern of this size is feasible in concrete or stone, or split into two 19 m³ cisterns for redundancy. In arid regions with 6-month dry seasons, a 50 m³ tank is the minimum practical size for household supply.
