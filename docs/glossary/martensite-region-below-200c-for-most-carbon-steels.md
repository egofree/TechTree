# Martensite region (below ~200°C for most carbon steels)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

If austenite is cooled fast enough to miss the pearlite and bainite noses on the TTT diagram, it transforms to **martensite** by a diffusionless shear mechanism. Carbon atoms are trapped in the BCT (body-centered tetragonal) lattice, causing severe lattice strain — this is what makes martensite hard (50-65 HRC depending on carbon content).
