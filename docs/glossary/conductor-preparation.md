# Conductor preparation

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Stranded copper wire (7 strands of ~0.5 mm diameter each, giving ~1.4 mm² cross-section for the 1866 transatlantic cable) was drawn, annealed, and cleaned. The copper had to be of high purity (low oxygen content) for maximum electrical conductivity — ~98-99% IACS (International Annealed Copper Standard). Any oxide or contamination on the copper surface would impair adhesion of the gutta-percha insulation.
