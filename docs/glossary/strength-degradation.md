# Strength degradation

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Fiber rope loses strength progressively with use. Wet rope: hemp gains strength (+10-20%), cotton loses strength (-30%). UV exposure: 10-30% strength loss per year in tropical sun. Abrasion: localized strength loss at chafe points. Biological attack: mold, rot, marine borers (untreated rope in tropical water may lose 50% strength in 6 months). Inspect rope regularly — visible fiber breakage on the surface indicates advanced degradation internally.
