# Snapback

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Rope under heavy tension stores significant energy. If the rope breaks, the ends snap back violently toward the anchor points, potentially striking personnel with lethal force. Never stand in the direct line of a rope under tension. When possible, stand to one side and behind the anchor point. Use whip lines (light cord tied across the loaded rope to dampen snapback if the main rope breaks).
