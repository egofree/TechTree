# Sulfur content (0.5-5%)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

On combustion, sulfur oxidizes to SO₂, which forms sulfuric acid in the atmosphere → acid rain. Damages forests, acidifies waterways, corrodes stone and metal structures. Wash coal to reduce sulfur before burning. Flue gas desulfurization (scrubbers using limestone slurry) removes SO₂ post-combustion but is complex.
