# Insulating firebrick (IFB)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Porosity is deliberately maximized (60-80%) to reduce thermal conductivity. Manufactured by mixing fireclay with a combustible filler (sawdust, polystyrene beads, or foaming agents) that burns out during firing, leaving a network of pores. Bulk density 0.5-1.0 g/cm³ (vs 2.0-2.4 for dense fireclay). Thermal conductivity 0.15-0.5 W/m·K at 500°C, roughly one-third to one-tenth of dense brick.
