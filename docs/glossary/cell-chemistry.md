# Cell chemistry

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: metals

Electrochemical reactions in the Hall-Héroult aluminum cell where alumina (Al₂O₃) dissolved in molten cryolite (Na₃AlF₆) at 960°C is electrolyzed: 2Al₂O₃ → 4Al (cathode) + 3O₂ (anode, reacting with carbon to form CO/CO₂). Current consumption is 13-15 kWh/kg Al.
