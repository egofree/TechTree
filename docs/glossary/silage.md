# Silage

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: animals

Fermented chopped forage stored airtight (clamp silage: pit in ground, lined with clay, packed with chopped green material, covered with earth). pH drops to 3.8-4.5 through lactic acid fermentation, preserving feed for up to 2 years. Provides higher nutritional value than hay when made from leafy material cut early. A 500 kg cow eats 20-30 kg silage/day. Silage making requires airtight conditions; exposure to air causes mold and spoilage within days.
