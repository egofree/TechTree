# Cartwright power loom (1785)

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

First practical power loom, powered by steam engine via belt drive. Automated the three basic loom motions: shedding (raising warp), picking (throwing shuttle), and beating (packing weft). Early versions produced 2-3× a hand weaver's output. Required robust iron frame to withstand the forces of mechanized beating.
