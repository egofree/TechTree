# Oxygen

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: critical | **Domains**: chemistry, machine-tools, silicon

The element essential to combustion, respiration, and most industrial oxidation processes. In the tech tree, oxygen is produced at scale by cryogenic air separation (distilling liquid air — O₂ boils at -183°C, N₂ at -196°C) or by chemical generation (barium oxide cycle: heat BaO in air to form BaO₂ at ~550°C, then heat BaO₂ to decompose at ~900°C, releasing O₂). Industrial oxygen is compressed into steel cylinders at 15-20 MPa for distribution to point of use.

## Context in the Tech Tree

Oxygen is one of the most important industrial gases. In the chemistry domain, air separation produces oxygen as a primary product alongside nitrogen and argon. Oxygen is used for steelmaking (lancing, basic oxygen furnace), welding and cutting (oxy-acetylene torch at ~3100°C flame temperature), chemical synthesis (oxidation reactions), and medical applications. In the machine-tools domain, oxy-fuel welding and cutting are the primary metal-joining methods before electric arc welding becomes available. In silicon production, oxygen is used in the Czochralski crystal growth process (reacting with carbon crucible) and in thermal oxidation to grow SiO₂ gate dielectric layers on silicon wafers.

The availability of pure oxygen enables process temperatures unachievable with air alone (which is 78% nitrogen, diluting the flame). Oxy-acetylene reaches 3100°C versus 2200°C for air-acetylene. The basic oxygen furnace blows pure oxygen through molten pig iron, achieving decarburization in 15-20 minutes versus hours in older processes.

## Technical Details

Cryogenic air separation is the dominant production method. Air is compressed to 5-8 atm, cooled to ~-200°C through heat exchangers and expansion turbines, then distilled in a double-column system. The lower column produces nitrogen-rich vapor overhead and oxygen-enriched liquid bottoms. The upper column produces pure nitrogen (99.999%) overhead and pure oxygen (99.5%) bottoms. Argon accumulates at an intermediate point and is drawn off for further purification.

For lower-volume production, pressure swing adsorption (PSA) uses zeolite molecular sieves that preferentially adsorb nitrogen from compressed air, leaving oxygen-enriched product gas at 90-95% purity. PSA units are simpler and cheaper than cryogenic plants but produce lower purity and smaller volumes. The barium oxide cycle (chemical generation) produces small quantities without any sophisticated equipment.
