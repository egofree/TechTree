# Liquor viscosity management

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Above 55-60% solids at ambient temperature, black liquor becomes too viscous to pump (viscosity >500 mPa·s). Solutions: (1) maintain liquor temperature above 80°C (viscosity decreases exponentially with temperature), (2) add lignin-derived viscosity reducers, (3) use high-solids concentrators (forced circulation evaporators operating at 110-130°C under pressure).
