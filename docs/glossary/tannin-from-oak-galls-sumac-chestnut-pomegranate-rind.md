# Tannin (from oak galls, sumac, chestnut, pomegranate rind)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Essential pre-mordant for cellulose fibers. Boil 20-30% of fiber weight of tannin-rich material, strain, soak fiber 2-4 hours before alum mordanting. Tannins form hydrogen bonds with cellulose hydroxyl groups and provide sites for alum attachment. Oak galls contain 50-70% tannin by weight — the most concentrated natural source. Sumac leaves contain 20-30% tannin. Pomegranate rind contains 25-28% tannin.
