# Common sail

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

Common sail (cloth on wooden frame — standard for centuries) — - **Frame**: Wooden lattice of spruce or pine, ~1.0-1.5 m wide × 5-12 m long per sail. 4-8 horizontal bars (sail bars) connected to main stock (longitudinal beam bolted to windshaft). - **Sailcloth**: Hemp or flax canvas, 0.4-0.6 kg/m². Reefed (partially furled) by removing or rolling canvas — miller adjusts sail area to control speed and power in varying wind.
