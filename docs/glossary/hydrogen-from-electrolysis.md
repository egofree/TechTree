# Hydrogen from electrolysis

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Hydrogen from electrolysis (clean route, growing) — Water electrolysis produces pure H₂ and O₂. See Electrolysis for detailed cell designs. - N₂ must be supplied separately (from air separation) since there is no secondary reformer to introduce air. - Energy: ~50-55 kWh per kg H₂ (alkaline electrolysis). Ammonia requires ~178 kg H₂ per tonne NH₃ → ~9-10 MWh per tonne NH₃ just for hydrogen.
