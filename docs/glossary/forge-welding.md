# Forge welding

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

(the oldest welding method): - **Principle**: Heat two pieces of iron/steel to bright yellow-white (~1300-1400°C) where the surface becomes pasty. Place together and hammer forcefully. The combination of heat, pressure, and clean surfaces causes a solid-state weld (atomic diffusion across interface). - **Flux**: Sprinkle clean silica sand or borax on joint surfaces before welding.
