# Counter-twist principle

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: textiles

Twist three strands together in the **opposite** direction to the yarn twist within each strand. If the yarn is Z-twist (clockwise), lay the rope S-twist (counterclockwise), and vice versa. This counter-twist creates a balanced rope that will not kink, curl, or untwist under load. The opposing twists hold each other in equilibrium — this is the fundamental mechanical principle of all rope construction.
