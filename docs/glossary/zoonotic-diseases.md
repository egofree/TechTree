# Zoonotic diseases

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: animals

Any disease that jumps from animals to humans is a zoonosis. The most significant in a livestock context: brucellosis (unpasteurized milk from infected cows or goats), ringworm (direct skin contact with infected animals), leptospirosis (urine-contaminated water), Q fever (birth fluids and manure aerosol), and rabies (bites from infected mammals).
