# Embossing

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: machine-tools, textiles

A shallow relief pattern formed by matching male and female dies that thin the sheet locally without significant stretching. The material thickness changes little overall; the pattern is created by redistribution. Used for decorative panels, tread plate, and stiffening ribs on sheet metal enclosures. Embossing pressures are modest (50-200 MPa) compared to coining because the deformation is shallow.
