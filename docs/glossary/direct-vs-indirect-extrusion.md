# Direct vs indirect extrusion

> **Type**: equipment | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: metals

Direct (forward) — die is stationary, billet moves relative to container. Higher friction (~30% of force overcomes billet-container friction). Indirect (backward) — die moves with the ram inside the hollow container; billet is stationary. No billet-container friction → 25-30% lower extrusion force, more uniform flow. Indirect requires a hollow ram (limits die size) and more complex tooling.
