# Hall-Héroult process — detailed cell construction and operation

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: chemistry

Technical details of the Hall-Héroult electrolytic cell: carbon anodes suspended in molten cryolite bath (Na₃AlF₆), carbon-lined steel cathode shell, alumina feed system, and crust-breaking mechanism. Operating at 4.0-4.5 V and 950-970°C, the cell reduces dissolved alumina to aluminum metal and CO₂.
