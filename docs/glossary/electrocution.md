# Electrocution

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy, transport

Electrical current through the body causes death at surprisingly low levels — 100 mA across the chest can cause ventricular fibrillation. Even "low" voltages (50V+) can be lethal under wet conditions. ALWAYS de-energize circuits before working on them (lock-out/tag-out). Use insulated tools. One-hand rule when working near live circuits (keep one hand in pocket — prevents current across chest).
