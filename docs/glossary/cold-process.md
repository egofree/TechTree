# Cold process

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Cold process (simplest, longest cure) — - Heat fats and oils to 38-43°C (100-110°F). Separately dissolve NaOH in cold water (exothermic — solution reaches 80-90°C; cool to 38-43°C). Pour lye solution into fats with constant stirring (stick blender accelerates emulsification). Stir to "trace" — mixture thickens so that drips leave visible marks on the surface (indicates emulsion stability). Add fragrance or colorants at trace. Pour into insulated molds.
