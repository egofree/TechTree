# Spark-ignition engine on producer gas

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Timing advance must be increased to 15-25° BTDC (before top dead center) because producer gas burns slower than gasoline (laminar flame speed ~0.4 m/s vs ~0.8 m/s for gasoline-air). Compression ratio can be increased to 10-12:1 (producer gas has high octane equivalent, resists knock) to partially compensate for the lower energy density.
