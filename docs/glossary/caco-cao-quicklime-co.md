# CaCO₃ → CaO (quicklime) + CO₂

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: supporting | **Domains**: ceramics

The calcination reaction: calcium carbonate (limestone) heated to 900-1000°C decomposes into calcium oxide (quicklime) and carbon dioxide. This endothermic reaction is the foundation of lime production for mortar, plaster, and chemical processes.
