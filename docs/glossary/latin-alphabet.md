# Latin alphabet

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

Latin alphabet (~600 BCE) — Derived from Greek via Etruscan intermediaries. Early Latin: 21 letters. Classical Latin added G (modified C) and differentiated Y and Z for Greek loanwords. Modern Latin alphabet: 26 letters. Worldwide standard for technical notation and international communication. See: [Writing & Record-Keeping](../knowledge/writing.md)
