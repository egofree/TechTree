# Dehydration to anhydrous ethanol

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Dehydration to anhydrous ethanol (>99%) — - The ethanol-water azeotrope limits simple [Distillation](../chemistry/distillation.md) to 95.6%. Breaking it requires: - **Molecular sieves** (zeolite 3Å): Adsorb water selectively. Two-bed system alternates adsorption and thermal regeneration. Produces 99.5-99.9% ethanol. Preferred method when zeolites are available. - **Quicklime (CaO)**: Add to 95% ethanol, stir, CaO reacts with water to form Ca(OH)₂, then decant or distill the dried ethanol. Simple but labor-intensive, generates lime sludge ... See: [Solvent Production](../chemistry/solvents.md)
