# Effluent treatment

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

Pulp mill effluent contains dissolved organic matter (BOD 20-50 mg/L for modern kraft mills), suspended solids, and chlorinated organic compounds (if chlorine-based bleaching is used). Biological treatment (activated sludge or aerated lagoon) reduces BOD by 90-95%. AOX (adsorbable organic halides) in effluent: <0.5 kg per tonne pulp for ECF mills, <0.1 kg/t for TCF mills. Unbleached mills produce essentially zero AOX.
