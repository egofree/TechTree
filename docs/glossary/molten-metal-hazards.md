# Molten metal hazards

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: machine-tools

Cast iron at 1300-1400°C and steel at 1500-1600°C cause catastrophic burns on contact. Metal flows like water and adheres to skin. Aluminum at 700-750°C is less dramatic but equally dangerous — it can wet and stick to clothing, causing deep burns that are difficult to remove.
