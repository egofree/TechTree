# Wooden frame sail

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: energy

The traditional windmill sail is built around a spruce spar (the main longitudinal member), typically 50 × 75 mm cross-section for a 5 m blade length. Horizontal sail bars (roughly 25 × 50 mm, spaced 400-600 mm apart) are mortised through or bolted to the spar, creating a ladder-like frame. Canvas or hemp sailcloth is laced or nailed to this frame.
