# Bellows types and output

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: foundations

A simple hide bag bellows (goat or sheep skin, operated by hand) delivers 50-80 liters of air per minute. This is enough to push a charcoal fire past 1000°C. A double-acting box bellows, which pushes air on both the push and pull strokes, delivers 150-300 L/min and can reach 1200-1300°C with good charcoal. For comparison, modern electric blowers push 500+ L/min.
