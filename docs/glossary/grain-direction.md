# Grain direction

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: knowledge

Handmade paper has no preferred grain direction. Machine-made paper (Fourdrinier machine, ~1803) has a grain (fiber alignment) parallel to the machine direction. Paper folds cleanly across the grain; cracks along the grain. Books must be bound with grain direction parallel to the spine — otherwise pages warp and the binding fails. For the hand papermaker, grain direction is controlled by the shake pattern during sheet formation.
