# Bottom anti-reflective coating (BARC)

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: photolithography

A thin organic or inorganic layer (30-80 nm) spun onto the wafer before photoresist. BARC absorbs exposure light that passes through the resist, preventing reflection from the substrate. Without BARC, reflected light creates standing wave interference in the resist, causing linewidth rippling on sidewalls and swing curves (periodic CD variation with resist thickness).
