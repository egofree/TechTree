# Ethanol vapor inhalation

> **Type**: material | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: polymers

Ethanol vapors cause headache, dizziness, and narcosis at high concentrations (>1000 ppm). The odor threshold (~100 ppm) is well below hazardous levels, but prolonged exposure in poorly ventilated spaces causes cumulative effects. Ensure adequate ventilation — 10+ air changes per hour for brush application, local exhaust for spraying.
