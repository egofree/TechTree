# Zirconia properties

> **Type**: noun | **Tier**: important | **Domains**: chemistry

ZrO₂ has a melting point of 2715°C, making it one of the most refractory oxides. However, pure zirconia undergoes a destructive phase transformation from tetragonal to monoclinic (accompanied by 3-5% volume change) at ~1170°C on cooling. This transformation causes cracking and disintegration. Stabilization: adding 6-12 mol% CaO, MgO, or Y₂O₃ forms a solid solution (cubic fluorite structure) that is stable through all temperature ranges.
